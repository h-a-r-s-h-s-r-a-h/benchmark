--- [Severance(TV series) - Wikipedia] ---
Severance is an American science fiction psychological thriller television series created by Dan Erickson, and executive produced and primarily directed by Ben Stiller. It stars Adam Scott, Zach Cherry, Britt Lower, Tramell Tillman, Jen Tullock, Dichen Lachman, Michael Chernus, John Turturro, Christopher Walken, Patricia Arquette, and Sarah Bock. The series follows employees at Lumon Industries, a biotechnology corporation, that have undergone "severance"—a procedure that splits a person's memories between work and their personal life. This creates two separate identities for employees: the "innie", who has no knowledge of the outside world, and the "outie", who lives their life outside without any knowledge of their job.
Erickson and Stiller first developed Severance in 2015, with the series being greenlit by Apple TV in 2019, with Scott attached. The cast for the first season was rounded out by December 2020, and the cast for the second season was announced in October 2022. Principal photography for the series has taken place in New York, New Jersey and Newfoundland. Its first season aired from February 18 until April 8, 2022, and its second season from January 17 to March 21, 2025. Severance has been renewed for a third season.  In February 2026, it was reported that Apple TV had acquired the intellectual property and all rights to the series from Fifth Season, with its in-house Apple Studios to become the production company for the series starting with the third season.
Severance has received critical acclaim for its cinematography, direction, production design, musical score, story, and performances. It has received numerous accolades, including 41 Primetime Emmy Award nominations, winning for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series for Lower and Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series for Tillman. Scott's performance earned him nominations for a Primetime Emmy Award, two Television Critics Association Awards, and a Golden Globe Award, while Stiller and Erickson also received Emmy nominations for directing and writing.

Observations and Theories about Gemma/Ms. Casey I've recently watched the show for a second time, and definitely spent more time watching and thinking about Gemma/Ms. Casey. I'm sure everything I'm gonna say is well-trodden ground at this point, but I want to hypothesise because it's fun! So, first of all, lets go over some observations, and what we can infer from them: - Everyone outside of the severed floor believes Gemma is dead. Mark and his family all believe Gemma is dead. We are given no hints or suggestions that anyone believes Gemma to be alive, or that there is some mystery around her death. I think its safe to assume that Gemma was indeed in a car crash, and that this car crash was serious enough to kill a person. An open-and-shut case as far as the 'real' world is concerned. 2. Ms. Casey is alive and well. Ms. Casey, who we only interact with on the severed floor, is perfectly alive. We see her walking and talking without assistance, has no visible signs of physical trauma, and is an otherwise perfectly physically healthy person. 3. Ms. Casey has undergone a severance procedure. Her last line in the show is to ask Milchick whether her 'outtie' has an enjoyable life on the outside. In this way, we can infer that Ms. Casey believes she lives a normal life separate from Lumon, analogous to Mark or Helly. However, we know that this is not true. She goes down the lift to the testing floor, not up to the surface. From this we can also infer from this that Ms. Casey has no knowledge of the testing floor, or anything outside of the severed floor. 4. Ms. Casey is proper weird. Ms. Casey is unlike other severed workers. We see what a 'fresh' severed person behaves like with Helly: angry, confused, hurt, but an otherwise functional person. Like a memory wipe. She's capable of complex emotions, banter, and manages social situations like a non-severed person. If we the audience didn't know about the severance procedure, we'd have no reason to believe Helly is different to any other person working in an office. None of this can be said about Ms. Casey. Her emotional range is extremely muted. She struggles immensely in social situations, as seen when she spends a day at MDR, where she is well out of her depth. One may point to the fact she's only been 'alive' for 108 hours, but we see Helly exhibit much more emotion and social capability in her first few minutes than Ms. Casey is capable of after over a hundred hours. Any viewer instantly realises how different Ms. Casey is to the other innies. However, I do find it interesting how the other innies don't seem to think she's weird at all. 5. Ms. Casey. bares more than a striking resemblance to Gemma. The last line in the show - "Flash Gordon She's Alive!" points to this very fact. The show tells us that Gemma and Ms. Casey are the 'same' person multiple times through the photo, interactions between Milchick and Ms. Cobel, and the final shot. There is no mistaking that the show wants us to make this connection. With these observations and assumptions laid out, we can make some points: - Ms. Casey is too alive and well. In the early 90s my mum was in a horrendous car crash. She aquaplaned on the motorway, had to be cut out of the car, and airlifted to hospital. She survived, was conscious throughout, and was discharged the next day. This was over 30 years ago, and to this day there are lots of visible scars all over my mum's body from that accident. If Gemma had a head-on collision with a tree, I don't doubt for a second that there would be very serious injuries all over her body, not least of which her face and head. We get a good look at Ms. Casey's face a lot, and there's nary a scar nor blemish upon it. But we know that Gemma did indeed crash into a tree in an accident severe enough to kill a person. 2. Ms. Casey has undergone a different procedure to other severed people. Given how different Ms. Casey is to the other innies, and the fact that she goes to the testing floor, I think its safe to say her procedure is very different to the severance one. I don't think she goes to the testing floor because she's meant to be dead in the real world so they can't release her, I think she goes to the severance floor because she's the next stage in whatever tech Lumon are developing. With all of this I think we can make a half-baked conclusion: - Ms Casey =/= Gemma. I don't think Ms. Casey is Gemma in the same way that say, Helly R is Helly E. I don't think Ms. Casey is just different from Gemma in terms of her mind and memories, I think she's different in terms of her body as well. Initial thoughts lean towards cloning, because of the Department of Goats. However, I don't think that's all that's going on. I do think there's is some part of Ms. Casey that is biologically Gemma, but I don't think it's any part of her body or brain that results in consciousness, memory, or any other mental faculty. Other innies have no memory of their outies, but do pull on some external knowledge and faculties from their outtie, such as their ability to socialise, knowledge of muscle shows, 'Delaware', etc. I don't think Ms. Casey can pull on these faculties from Gemma, because not enough of Gemma is alive/in Ms. Casey. So unlike other innies, Ms. Casey came into this world a completely blank slate. But when we see her she's not completely blank. She does express some emotion, such as stating her enjoyment of her day at MDR, and she's clearly upset/crying in her final scene of the season. If she is a blank slate, where does she get these limited faculties from? The severance chip in her brain. And that's where I think we can hypothesise about what MDR do. They look at all those numbers that seemingly illicit emotions in the severed people, and categorise them based on the four tempers. In this way, I think MDR is the 'machine-learning' for whatever procedure Ms. Casey has undergone. This conclusion leaves us with loads of questions, but two that I would love to see what people think about are: - What happens to Ms. Casey when she goes down the lift to the testing floor? Like I said in my third observation, I think its safe to assume that Ms. Casey is only 'active' on the severed floor, in the same way that Helly R. is only 'active' on the severed floor (barring overtime contingency). I think she has no knowledge of the testing floor, so when she gets in the lift and goes down, what happens? Does she go into Gemma mode? I don't think so, because I don't think Ms. Casey is Gemma, strictly speaking. So does Ms. Casey go into this third mode that is neither Gemma nor Ms. Casey? What would that look like? As Ms. Casey is severed, there must be two personalities in that body, if one is Ms. Casey but the other isn't Gemma, then what is it? 2. Why does Irv know about the lift to the testing floor? This is not strictly speaking related to Gemma/Ms. Casey, but I do think it's tangential. The image of the lift, going down in the dark hallway, is a striking one. We know that Outtie Irv knows about this, because he paints it constantly. Innie Irv does not seem to know about this corridor. Moreso, a popular theory is that Outtie Irv is trying to implant the knowledge of the corridor into Innie Irv by constantly painting it, keeping himself awake, etc. So we can say that Outtie Irv has knowledge of a very secret part of Lumon that Innie Irv does not. Does Outtie Irv know about Ms. Casey/Gemma? I highly doubt any of my long-winded hypothesising is breaking new ground, but I love this fucking show and none of my friends have watched it so I just need to talk to people about it. Sorry its so long and rambly but I enjoy theorising about this show, and would love to hear other people's thoughts!
