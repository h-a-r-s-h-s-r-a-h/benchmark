https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergio_Flamigni Sergio Flamigni (Forlì, October 22, 1925 – Bracciano, December 10, 2025) was an Italian politician, writer, and partisan. He was a member of the Italian Communist Party (PCI) parliament from 1968 to 1987, as well as a member of the parliamentary commissions of inquiry into the Moro affair, the P2 lodge, and the anti-Mafia movement. Parliamentary Offices In May 1968, he was elected to the Chamber of Deputies, in the Bologna constituency. In the fifth legislature (1968-1972), he was a member of the Presidency of the Council of Ministers - Interior Affairs Committee and the Special Bicameral Anti-Mafia Commission. He was re-elected to the Chamber of Deputies in the sixth legislature (1972-1976), as vice president of the Presidency of the Council of Ministers - Interior Affairs Committee and a member of the Bicameral Anti-Mafia Commission. In the seventh legislature (1976-1979), he was re-elected to the Chamber of Deputies, as vice president of the Presidency of the Council of Ministers - Interior Affairs Committee. In the eighth legislature (1979-1983), he was elected to the Senate, as a member of the Committee for Constitutional Affairs, Presidency of the Council of Ministers and Interior Affairs, and of the Committee on Elections and Parliamentary Immunities. He was appointed to the Parliamentary Commission of Inquiry into the Moro case and to the Anti-Mafia Commission. In the ninth legislature (1983-1987), he was re-elected to the Senate as a member of the Constitutional Affairs Committee, Presidency of the Council of Ministers - Interior. Flamigni was born in Forlì and began his political activity in 1941, as a member of a clandestine group of young anti-fascists in his hometown, and subsequently entered the Communist Party. He fought as partisan in the Italian resistance movement against the German occupation. In 1952 he was appointed as secretary of CGIL (Italy"s left main trade union) in Forlì, and later he became secretary of the local section of Payment Card Industry. In 1959 he was elected into the party"s national central committee, and, in the following year, regional coordinator for Emilia-Romagna. He was elected to the Italian Chamber of Deputies in 1968, remaining a member until 1979, when he became a Senator. Flamigni worked in the Parliament"s commissions on mafia, the kidnapping of Aldo Moro and the Propaganda Due secret lodge, and wrote several books about these arguments. In 1943 he was named secretary of the communist youth movement in Forlì and became a member of the party"s clandestine committee in the city. A member of the Italian Communist Party (Payment Card Industry), he took part in the Italian Parliament"s investigative commissions on the murder of Aldo Moro, the Propaganda Due scandal and on mafia. He was also a member of the city council of Forlì from 1956 to 1960, and of the provincial council from 1960 until 1964.
