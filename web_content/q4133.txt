--- [Expectations (Bebe Rexha album) - Wikipedia] ---
Expectations is the debut studio album by American singer-songwriter Bebe Rexha. It was released on June 22, 2018, by Warner Bros. Records. The album was announced following the success of her collaboration with country duo Florida Georgia Line, "Meant to Be". Expectations went out for pre-order on April 13, 2018, with the release of two promotional singles: "Ferrari" and "2 Souls on Fire". The album includes the singles "I Got You" and "Meant to Be" from All Your Fault: Pt. 1 and All Your Fault: Pt. 2, respectively. It features appearances from rappers Quavo and Tory Lanez, along with Florida Georgia Line.
Expectations received generally positive reviews from music critics, who praised its production and Rexha's vocal performance, while others criticized its lyricism. The album debuted at number thirteen on the US Billboard 200 chart, earning 24,000 album-equivalent units (including 10,000 copies as pure album sales) in its first week. As of January 2019, the album has sold 604,000 units (with 37,000 copies in pure album sales) in the US. The album was later certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) for combined sales and album-equivalent units of over a million units in the United States.

Expectations (Bebe Rexha album) Background and recording Announcement and conception On November 7, 2017, Bebe Rexha revealed the title of her debut studio album, Expectations, via Twitter, positioning it as her first full-length release after the 2015 EP I Don't Wanna Grow Up and the 2017 EP All Your Fault: Pt. 1.[8] This announcement came amid her rising profile as a songwriter and performer, highlighted by hit singles such as "I Got You" (2016) and "Meant to Be" with Florida Georgia Line (2017), which served as thematic precursors to the album's exploration of emotional highs and lows.[9] The conception of Expectations stemmed from Rexha's ambition to fully express her artistry as a lead artist, moving beyond her role as a behind-the-scenes songwriter for artists like Eminem and Selena Gomez to deliver a cohesive pop album that delved into personal vulnerabilities, relationships, and self-reflection.[10] Rexha described the project as a reflection of her life experiences in Los Angeles, where unmet expectations shaped her growth, aiming to create an intimate record that balanced vulnerability with empowerment.[11] On April 8, 2018, Rexha unveiled the album's cover art on social media, depicting her in a white gown curled in a fetal position against a stark background, symbolizing emotional exposure and the rawness central to the album's themes.[12] The pre-order launched on April 13, 2018, coinciding with the release of initial promotional singles to build anticipation.[13] Rexha disclosed the full tracklist on May 27, 2018, through social media, comprising 14 songs including collaborations with Quavo on "2 Souls on Fire" and Tory Lanez on "Steady," alongside solo tracks like "I'm a Mess" and "Self Control."[14]Recording and production The recording of Expectations primarily occurred between 2017 and 2018 in several Los Angeles-based studios, including EastWest Studios for string sessions, Westlake Recording Studios for vocal and production work, and NightBird Recording Studios, with additional sessions at The Glovebox and Big Loud Studios in Nashville, Tennessee.[15][16][17][18] Rexha drew inspiration from her earlier singles to shape the album's direction during this period, emphasizing a shift toward more personal pop sounds.[10] Rexha took a hands-on approach, co-writing and co-producing all 14 tracks, which represented her evolution from a prominent songwriter for artists like Eminem and Rihanna to a front-facing performer and producer.[10][19] Key collaborators included producers such as Max Martin and Ali Payami, Jason Evigan on "Ferrari," Jussifer (Jussi Lindell) on multiple songs including "I'm a Mess" and "2 Souls on Fire," Louis Bell on "Steady," and The Stereotypes on select cuts.[9][19] The production incorporated layered pop arrangements with electronic elements, blending upbeat rhythms and vulnerable tones to reflect Rexha's emotional experiences.[10] Vocals were recorded by Rexha, with engineering support from professionals like Devon Corey, who served as vocal producer on several tracks.[20] Sessions faced challenges, including emotional hurdles from Rexha's personal struggles with anxiety and heartbreak, as well as coordinating remote contributions such as Quavo's verse on "2 Souls on Fire," which was recorded separately and integrated to enhance the track's depth.[10][9]Music and lyrics Musical style Expectations is primarily a pop album with electropop foundations, incorporating elements of R&B, dance-pop, and subtle country influences that reflect Rexha's genre explorations without fully venturing into traditional territory.[21][22][23] The album's production emphasizes modern techniques, including heavy Auto-Tune on vocals for a sleek pop polish, layered harmonies, and electronic beats drawing from 2010s EDM trends, while synths and simplistic drums provide a consistent backdrop. Upbeat, synth-driven tracks like "Ferrari" contrast with mid-tempo ballads such as "Knees," which leans into acoustic pop arrangements; the overall runtime is 43:47, fostering a dynamic flow through varied pacing.[7][24][25][26][9] Influences from 1990s pop appear in nostalgic synth elements reminiscent of Britney Spears-era sounds, blended with contemporary trap production in features like "2 Souls on Fire" with Quavo, where rap integrates over tropical and trap-influenced beats. Alt-rock guitar riffs and No Doubt-inspired lines add texture, particularly in opening tracks, while the album's cohesion stems from uniform pop verse-chorus structures amid diverse sonic shifts.[5][26][22]Lyrical themes The album Expectations centers on the theme of unmet expectations in personal relationships, exploring vulnerability, heartbreak, and the journey toward self-empowerment amid the pressures of fame and adulthood. Rexha has described the record as a reflection on growing up in a world where one anticipates parental perfection, career success, or financial stability to bring fulfillment, only to confront the reality that these ideals often lead to disappointment and emotional turmoil. This overarching narrative weaves through tracks that confront the complexities of love, including toxic dynamics and the search for genuine connection, while emphasizing resilience as a path to independence.[27] Specific motifs recur across the lyrics, such as romantic disillusionment, depicted in "Ferrari" through metaphors of luxury cars symbolizing superficial materialism over emotional depth, where Rexha laments a relationship lacking "sparks" and driven by fleeting, high-speed desires rather than lasting bonds. Longing and intense passion emerge in "2 Souls on Fire," a duet with Quavo that uses fire imagery to convey burning mutual attraction and vulnerability, with verses alternating between partners expressing desire amid shared pain from past heartbreaks. Resilience appears in "Self Control," where Rexha employs addiction-like metaphors—comparing love to an uncontrollable craving beyond substances—to illustrate the struggle against overwhelming relational pull, ultimately highlighting a push toward regaining personal agency.[28][29][30][31] Rexha's confessional songwriting draws heavily from her personal experiences, including toxic relationships and mental health challenges such as anxiety and depression; she was diagnosed with bipolar disorder in 2019 and has retrospectively connected these themes to her work on the album. In "I'm a Mess," she addresses mental health struggles directly, embracing imperfections as an empowering anthem of self-acceptance, while in "Sad," lines like "Maybe I'm just comfortable being sad" reflect a candid reckoning with inner chaos. Empowering tracks like "I Got You" promote independence by portraying post-breakup solidarity, where Rexha assures a partner of mutual support without dependency, evolving from her own healing process after romantic fallout.[32][33][34] This approach marks an evolution in Rexha's songwriting from her earlier ghostwriting for others, shifting to unpolished, autobiographical narratives that prioritize vulnerability over idealized pop tropes. Collaborations, such as Quavo's verse in "2 Souls on Fire," add layered perspectives on mutual heartbreak, enriching the album's exploration of relational expectations without diluting its personal core.[25][5]Singles Promotional singles To build anticipation for her debut studio album Expectations, Bebe Rexha released two promotional singles on April 13, 2018, coinciding with the album's pre-order launch. These tracks were designed as instant-gratification offerings for fans, providing immediate access upon pre-order without a formal push to radio or major commercial promotion. Both songs were co-written by Rexha and appear on the standard edition tracklist, positioning them as early hooks to draw listeners into the album's themes of desire, vulnerability, and emotional intensity.[35][33] The lead promotional single, "Ferrari", opens the album and explores the theme of a high-speed, luxurious lifestyle that masks inner emptiness and isolation. With lyrics depicting blurred days and nights overlooking city lights from Mulholland Drive, the song conveys a sense of fleeting escape amid personal turmoil. Accompanied by a vertical lyric video featuring Rexha against dramatic urban backdrops, it emphasized visual motifs of speed and solitude to complement its introspective tone. The track achieved moderate streaming success, accumulating tens of millions of plays on platforms like Spotify in the months following its release, serving primarily as a teaser for the album's pop-rock edge.[28][36][37] Simultaneously released, "2 Souls on Fire" featuring Quavo follows as the album's third track and delves into a passionate yet potentially doomed romance, framed by recurring motifs of burning desire and foolish infatuation. Produced by Jussifer and Aaron Zuckerman, the song blends trap-influenced beats with Rexha's soaring vocals and Quavo's verses, capturing the thrill and risk of intense attraction. Its lyric video incorporates fiery visual effects to underscore the titular imagery of consuming flames, aligning with the track's theme of emotional combustion. Like "Ferrari", it garnered solid streaming traction without radio support, functioning as a hype-building collaboration to spotlight the album's diverse sonic palette.[30][38][39]Commercial singles Expectations includes pre-album singles "I Got You" (2016) and "Meant to Be" (2017) from Rexha's earlier EPs, alongside the post-release single "I'm a Mess" (2018). "Meant to Be" featuring Florida Georgia Line, released on October 6, 2017, peaked at number 2 on the Billboard Hot 100 for three weeks and spent a total of 58 weeks on the chart. The track's country crossover appeal contributed to its longevity, topping the Hot Country Songs chart for 50 weeks and marking Rexha's first major entry into country radio programming. This success helped build anticipation for the album prior to its release, aligning with themes of romantic expectations explored throughout Expectations. "I'm a Mess" was released as the only commercial single from the album on June 15, 2018, reaching number 34 on the Billboard Hot 100. The song delves into themes of emotional turmoil and vulnerability in relationships, with its accompanying music video portraying Rexha's struggles with mental health in an institutional setting, drawing from her personal experiences with anxiety. It received significant airplay on pop and contemporary hit radio (CHR) formats, emphasizing its dance-pop production and introspective lyrics. "I Got You," released in October 2016 as the lead single from All Your Fault: Pt. 1 and included on Expectations, peaked at number 43 on the Billboard Hot 100. The track highlights self-reliance in love, with lyrics affirming support amid heartbreak and uncertainty. Its inclusion capitalized on Rexha's growing solo profile, blending electro-pop elements that resonated on mainstream radio. By 2025, "Meant to Be" had surpassed 14 million equivalent units in the United States, earning Diamond certification from the RIAA for 10 million units, while "I'm a Mess" achieved 2x Platinum status and "I Got You" reached Platinum certification, reflecting their enduring commercial impact.[40]Promotion Marketing and release Expectations was released on June 22, 2018, through Warner Bros. Records in digital download, compact disc, and vinyl formats.[41] The album's marketing emphasized digital platforms to build anticipation, including social media teasers such as the tracklist reveal on May 27, 2018, and a countdown shared across Instagram and other channels leading up to the launch.[14] A key promotional element was the release of an album sampler video on YouTube on June 21, 2018, featuring snippets of all tracks to generate pre-release excitement.[42] Post-release, Rexha participated in a Reddit AMA on June 24, 2018, in the r/popheads subreddit, where she discussed the album's inspirations and creative process, further engaging fans.[43] The standard edition comprises 14 tracks with a total runtime of 43 minutes and 55 seconds.[44] In its first week, the album sold 24,000 equivalent album units in the United States, including 10,000 in pure sales.[45] A limited-edition clear vinyl reissue, limited to 2,500 copies, was released on November 29, 2024, as part of Record Store Day Black Friday, marking the first vinyl pressing of the album and appealing to collectors without additional content.[46]Live performances Rexha debuted the lead single "I'm a Mess" from Expectations on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon on June 25, 2018, delivering a high-energy performance that highlighted the track's emotional vulnerability and pop production. To coincide with the album's release, she performed "I'm a Mess" live on Good Morning America in Central Park on June 22, 2018, during a promotional appearance that celebrated the project's launch and included discussions of her songwriting process.[47] Later in 2018, Rexha showcased album tracks at major award shows and events, including a medley of "I'm a Mess" and "Meant to Be" alongside Brett Kissel at the iHeartRadio MuchMusic Video Awards on August 26 in Toronto, which drew a large broadcast audience.[48] She also took the stage at Lollapalooza in Chicago on August 3, performing "I'm a Mess" amid a setlist featuring collaborations and covers, energizing the festival crowd with her dynamic vocals.[49] At the iHeartRadio Jingle Ball concerts in December 2018, including stops in New York and Los Angeles, Rexha included medleys of "Ferrari" and "Meant to Be" in her sets, reaching millions through live broadcasts and contributing to the holiday season's pop showcases.[50] Expectations did not receive a dedicated headlining tour, but Rexha incorporated its tracks into festival appearances and as an opening act for Bruno Mars' 24K Magic World Tour throughout 2018, where she previewed songs like "I'm a Mess" to diverse audiences across North America and Europe. In 2019, during promotional activities for her single "Last Hurrah," Rexha's North American tour sets featured several Expectations cuts, such as "Ferrari" and "I'm a Mess," alongside fan-favorite interactions that emphasized the album's themes of self-reflection and resilience.[51]Critical reception Contemporary reviews Upon its release in June 2018, Expectations received generally favorable reviews from music critics, earning a Metacritic score of 65 out of 100 based on six reviews.[52] This aggregate indicated a mixed-to-positive reception, with praise centered on Bebe Rexha's vocal versatility and the album's polished production, though some reviewers noted inconsistencies in songwriting and pacing. Positive assessments highlighted the album's nostalgic pop elements and Rexha's ability to blend genres effectively. Rolling Stone awarded it 3.5 out of 5 stars, commending its "nineties-loving loneliness" and Rexha's quirky, irresistible vocal delivery that evoked heartache across tracks like "I'm a Mess."[5] Similarly, NME gave it 4 out of 5 stars, describing it as a "compelling re-introduction" to the artist, with strong production that allowed her to stamp her identity on contemporary pop sounds, particularly in standout cuts such as "Ferrari."[26] The Quietus rated it 7 out of 10, praising Rexha as a "chameleon" whose voice adapted seamlessly to diverse rhythms and tempos, incorporating world flavors into a superb collection of pop tracks. Mixed reviews acknowledged the album's hooks and energy but critiqued its lyrical depth and overall cohesion. The Guardian issued a scathing 1 out of 5 stars, calling it "fiercely trite pop" that perfunctorily checked genre boxes, with uneven lyricism on themes of anxiety and relationships despite strong production and occasional authentic belting on songs like "Ferrari."[29]AllMusic assigned 3 out of 5 stars, noting the record's catchy hooks and emotional pull in radio-friendly pop but pointing to a stalling midsection that disrupted momentum and made some themes feel derivative. Critics commonly lauded Rexha's versatile voice, which shone across pop, R&B, and electronic styles, and the high-quality production that elevated tracks like "I'm a Mess" and "Ferrari" as highlights. However, recurring criticisms focused on pacing issues in the album's middle tracks and lyrics that, while relatable, often lacked originality, leading to a sense of unevenness. Overall, the consensus positioned Expectations as a solid debut that solidified Rexha's status as a formidable pop presence, even if it fell short of innovation.[52]Retrospective reception In the years following its release, Expectations has been reassessed for its prescient exploration of mental health struggles, particularly through tracks like "I'm a Mess," which candidly addresses anxiety and emotional turmoil. Rexha's ongoing advocacy for bipolar disorder and mental health awareness, including her public disclosure in 2019, has amplified appreciation for the album's vulnerability as a bridge to broader conversations in pop music. Critics and fans have noted how these themes foreshadowed Rexha's later work and her role as a stigma-breaker, with the song's raw honesty resonating amid rising mental health discussions post-2020.[53][7] The album's 2024 Black Friday Record Store Day reissue on clear vinyl, limited to 2,500 copies, sparked renewed interest, positioning Expectations as an underrated gem of 2010s pop. This physical rerelease highlighted its eclectic production and Rexha's vocal range, prompting reflections on its cohesive electropop sound amid evolving genre trends. While initial mixed reception focused on lyrical directness, later analyses praised its genre-blending as influential in vulnerable, introspective pop narratives.[54][7] User aggregates reflect a solid but polarized fanbase, with Rate Your Music assigning an average score of 2.39 out of 5 based on over 600 ratings, often commending the album's thematic unity despite critiques of repetitive structures. By 2025, a user review on Album of the Year described it as unmemorable yet vocally competent, underscoring persistent views on underpromotion hindering its commercial breakthrough. Nevertheless, the album's streaming longevity—surpassing 3 billion plays on Spotify—demonstrates enduring appeal, with standout tracks like "I'm a Mess" and "I Got You" driving consistent listens.[21][55][56] Critically, Expectations is now viewed as a pivotal marker in Rexha's transition from behind-the-scenes songwriter to front-facing artist, with its bold co-production and personal lyrics laying groundwork for her subsequent releases. Reassessments emphasize its role in normalizing emotional transparency in pop, though some analyses in 2025 note that limited marketing at launch curtailed its immediate impact. Tracks from the album have seen remixing and sampling in 2020s productions, reinforcing its subtle influence on confessional pop styles.[7][55]Commercial performance Chart performance Expectations debuted at number 13 on the US Billboard 200 chart for the week dated July 7, 2018, with 24,000 album-equivalent units, of which 10,000 were pure album sales. The album spent a total of 20 weeks on the chart.[57] It ranked at number 147 on the Billboard 200 year-end chart for 2018 and number 142 for 2019. Internationally, the album reached number 19 on the ARIA Albums Chart in Australia, number 31 on the New Zealand Albums Chart, number 33 on the UK Albums Chart, and number 28 on the Scottish Albums Chart.[58][59][60] It peaked at #35 on the Norwegian Albums Chart and #40 on the Irish Albums Chart.[61] By 2025, Expectations had accumulated an estimated 150 or more global chart points across various territories.[62] The album demonstrated streaming-driven longevity, re-entering several charts in 2020 following its platinum certification, bolstered by sustained visibility from singles such as "Meant to Be." As of November 2025, the album has surpassed 3 billion streams on Spotify.[56]Certifications Expectations has achieved certifications in multiple countries, recognizing combined sales and streaming equivalents as of 2025. The album reached Platinum status in the United States, with additional Gold and Platinum awards elsewhere, totaling approximately 1.3 million certified units worldwide by 2025, largely propelled by streaming performance.
