--- [Caucasia, Antioquia - Wikipedia] ---
Caucasia is a small city and municipality located in the subregion of Bajo Cauca Antioquia department, in Colombia. Bounded on the south by the department of Córdoba, in the east by the municipalities of Antioquia in Nechí and El Bagre, on the north by the municipality of Zaragoza, and west to the town of Cáceres, and is located 270 km from Medellín.
In sports they have the "Bajo Cauca F.C." that is playing in the Colombian Football Federation's Second Division.

Geography and Landscape Caucasia is in the northern part of the Antioquia department. It sits right on the border with the Córdoba department. The town is about 150 meters (490 feet) above sea level. The area around Caucasia is mostly flat. There are some small hills in the north, west, and south. It is a large municipality because of its great location. It is close to where important rivers meet, like the Cauca and Nechí rivers. It is also near the Paramillo National Park. The rural parts of Caucasia are in the Andean area. Here, the land rises from 50 to 500 meters (164 to 1,640 feet). This area is where the Paisa Region and the savannah regions of Colombia meet. This mix has created a unique local culture called the Chilapa culture. The highest point in the municipality is the Olvido mountain, which is 502 meters (1,647 feet) high. It is located in a place called Puerto Colombia. Climate and Weather Caucasia has two main types of climate. One is a warm tropical climate. The other is a warm mountainous wet climate. This is because the town is located at the foot of the Andes mountains. The temperature in Caucasia is affected by winds and rainfall. The average temperature in the town is about 27.4 °C (81.3 °F). Temperatures in the urban area usually range from 25 to 32 °C (77 to 89.6 °F). The hottest temperatures can reach 35 °C (95 °F). The cooler temperatures are around 23 °C (73.4 °F), with the lowest being about 21 °C (69.8 °F). The beginning and end of the year are usually dry seasons. However, the weather can change, and it gets wet at certain times, especially from August to November. The average yearly rainfall is about 2,500 mm (98 inches). SUBREGIÓN BAJO CAUCA CLIMA 27° C DISTANCIA A MEDELLÍN 6 h 2 min (286 km) CORREGIMIENTOS 10 Información General En 1877, el Estado estableció a Nechí como distrito mediante la Ley 50 sobre demarcación y división territorial. Seguidamente en marzo de 1878, el presidente Teodomiro Llano Botero, traslada la cabecera de Nechí al actual corregimiento Margento. En 1886, doña Petrona Arrieta y sus hijos se asentaron en un terreno ondulado lleno de árboles cañafistuleros a orillas del río Cauca, donde construyeron chozas y con el tiempo se le unen a personas provenientes de Bocas de Man, formando el caserío de Cañafístula. En 1912, Margento se convirtió en la cabecera municipal, mientras que los caseríos Nechí y Caucasia quedaron bajo su independencia administrativa. En 1927, el obispo monseñor Miguel Ángel Builes y líderes cívicos cambiaron el nombre de Cañafístula por Caucasia, y en mayo de ese año, se elevó a la categoría de corregimiento. En 1942, debido a la construcción de la carretera troncal de Occidente y la importancia económica de Caucasia, Nechí se convirtió en municipio y su cabecera municipal fue trasladada a Caucasia mediante la Ordenanza 05 del 7 de Julio de ese año. La identidad de Cáceres está estrechamente ligada a la explotación del oro. Ubicación Caucasia está ubicada en la subregión Bajo Cauca. Limita por el norte con el departamento de Córdoba, por el este con los municipios de Nechí y El Bagre, por el sur con el municipio de Zaragoza, y por el oeste con el municipio de Cáceres.
