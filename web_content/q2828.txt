--- [Indie RPG Awards - Wikipedia] ---
The Indie RPG Awards were annual, creator-based awards for Indie role-playing games and supplements. They were established in 2002 by Andy Kitkowski. The final round of awards was given in 2017.
For the purposes of the Awards, there exists a following "definition" of an Indie role-playing game:

A game where the creator is the person who has written at least 50% of the actual game content.
A game where the creator has full control of content and publishing.
A game where the creator is the publisher, with full control over expenses and profits.

--- [Downfall - Less Than Three Games] ---
Your cart is currently empty! Downfall A table-top role-playing game for 3 people. No GM, no dice, no prep. In Downfall, you choose a Flaw you want to explore and build a unique and irredeemably broken world around it. See how Greed destroys a city, how Loyalty corrupts a kingdom, or how Curiosity betrays a galactic empire. You’ll craft traditions that bring your society to life, then see how it all comes tumbling down. The game works in any kind of setting, from mythical fantasy to the real world to high-flying science fiction. It’s made to tell a whole story in a single 2-4 hour session and doesn’t require preparation, dice, or a GM. Build the world… You build your setting together, creating a rich physical and cultural landscape to destroy during play. If you enjoy world-building, you’ll love making a society in Downfall. You begin by choosing a Flaw—something like greed, complacency, or loyalty— to serve as the theme of your game. This long-ignored Flaw has finally surfaced to show its teeth. Though the Flaw has always been there, it is just now beginning to destroy everything it created. Then you create a detailed set of traditions, practices, and beliefs that your society holds dear. These are the pieces you will break throughout play. … and then destroy it We role-play to explore the collapse of the world, shaping the destruction of our home through our characters. You’ll tell the story of a Hero who tries to save their home, and fails. In Downfall you take turns as each character in the game, so everyone is at the center of the fiction. The decisions you make throughout the game lead to a unique and memorable collapse of a civilization. Even though you fail to save your world, the story you tell of its destruction will remain forever. Tuesday, August 10, 2010 Indie RPG Awards The Indie RPG Awards were announced on Friday at GenCon Indy 2010 at the Design Matters booth. Started in 2002, independently published games are nominated each year and voted on by their peers. Pirate Jenny games were well represented for 2009! Kagematsu won the Indie Game of the Year for 2009. Congratulations to Danielle! Kagetmatsu also placed third for Most Innovative Game (won by Paul Tevis' A Penny for My Thoughts). Thou Art But a Warrior and RPGirl took second and third place respectively for Best Supplement which was won by The Day after Ragnarok by Ken Hite. Congratulations to all the winners and nominees! Wednesday, July 21, 2010 Kagematsu nominated for Diana Jones Award The Diana Jones committee has chosen Danielle Lewon's Kagematsu-the RPG as one of their picks for the shortlist for the 2010 award. The Diana Jones award, described as "is an annual award created to publicly acknowledge excellence in gaming", is prestigious. It has been won in years past by a wide variety of games, communities and projects such as Grey Ranks by Jason Morningstar, Dominion the card game by Donald Vaccarino, and the Irish game convention charity auctions. This year, Kagematsu is joined by the convention-busting historical rpg Montsegur 1244, the lively and extremely informative Board Game Geek forums and website, and Chaos in the Old World a very popular board game from Fantasy Flight Games. Pirate Jenny wishes Danielle and all of the nominees the best of luck! (But maybe a little more for Danielle!)
