--- [Giant Japanese Salamander | River Monsters Wiki | Fandom] ---
Description[] The huge Giant Japanese Salamander (Andrias japonicus; in Japanese: officially 大山椒魚 / オオサンショウウオ Ōsan Shōuo, but most commonly called there 半割 / ハンザキ Hanzaki) is the world's second largest salamander, after the critically endangered Giant Chinese Salamander. The Giant Chinese Salamander itself is causing the demise of the Japanese one. This is because of cross breeding. The cross breeding creates hybrids that are not members of the same species. Growing up to 1.5 meters in length, and 25 kilograms, the Giant Japanese Salamander is huge. River Monsters[] The Giant Japanese Salamander appears in Season 3 episode 6, "Cold Blooded Horror". In the episode, Jeremy Wade travels to Japan hunting the Kappa (in Japanese: 河童 / カッパ), a large predator said to drag children to their death. It leads him to the Giant Salamander. He only catches a small one, however he is able to see a relatively large hybrid. References[] [1] [2] [3] [4]

River Monsters is a British wildlife documentary television series produced for Animal Planet by Icon Films of Bristol, United Kingdom. It is hosted by angler and biologist Jeremy Wade, who travels around the globe in search of large and dangerous fish.
River Monsters premiered on ITV in Great Britain and became one of the most-watched programmes in Animal Planet's history. It is also one of the most-viewed series on Discovery Channel in the American market.
