--- [Goldsboro, North Carolina - Wikipedia] ---
Goldsboro, originally Goldsborough, is a city in and the county seat of Wayne County, North Carolina, United States. The population was 33,657 at the 2020 census. It is the principal city of and is included in the Goldsboro, North Carolina Metropolitan Statistical Area. The nearby town of Waynesboro was founded in 1787, and Goldsboro was incorporated in 1847.
The city is situated in North Carolina's Coastal Plain and is bordered on the south by the Neuse River and the west by the Little River, approximately 40 miles (64 km) southwest of Greenville, 50 miles (80 km) southeast of Raleigh, the state capital, and 75 miles (121 km) north of Wilmington in Southeastern North Carolina. Seymour Johnson Air Force Base is located in Goldsboro. Even though there is an Air Force base, it is still considered one of the most dangerous cities in North Carolina. Its crime rate is higher than both the national, and state average.

--- [County, city population declines, 2020 census shows - Goldsboro News-Argus] ---
Preliminary data from the 2020 United States census released Aug. 12 shows the total population in Wayne County, including the city of Goldsboro, has declined during the past decade. Wayne County’s total population fell from 122,623 in 2010 to 117,333 in 2020, while Goldsboro’s population alone fell from 36,437 in 2010 to 33,610 in 2020, the data shows. When it comes to racial makeup countywide, whites, Blacks and Hispanics are the three major ethnic groups. Whites account for 52.7% of the population while Blacks make up 32.4% of the population and Hispanic or Latino residents make up 12.4% of the population. Asians account for 1.3% of the population. In just the city, Blacks make up the majority. Blacks account for 52.3% of the population, followed by whites at 34.4%, Hispanics at 5.7% and Asians at 2.7%. The 2020 census also shows that the city had 3,853 military veterans and the county as a whole had 12,045. Foreign-born persons accounted for 7.5% in Wayne County’s total population while 5.9% make up Goldsboro. When it comes to housing, data for building permits and the number of homes in Goldsboro was not available in the census data. Countywide, there were 54,732 homes in 2019 with an occupancy rate of 62.1%. The number of building permits countywide was 414 in 2020, the data shows. The numbers concern Craig Honeycutt, Wayne County manager. “If you look at our housing stats and our building permits over the last 10 years, it just doesn’t make any sense,” Honeycutt said. “More data should be available by the end of September. We’re still evaluating, but we’re going to do whatever we can to challenge the numbers because it matters for (our) population on state-shared revenues and also on federal revenues as well.” Honeycutt said municipalities and counties get one shot every 10 years, and the money they receive in state and federal revenues is based on population. “Our initial look at it is we disagree with the housing numbers, so right now that’s where we’re disagreeing and we’re having our planning department and a lot of people checking to look if anything got missed or if anything wasn’t counted where there could be deficiencies in the numbers,” Honeycutt said. As part of housing data provided by the Census Bureau, the average median gross rent in Goldsboro is $791, while countywide the median gross rent is $771. The median home value in Goldsboro is $127,300; the median value for homes in Wayne County as a whole is $123,500. The median monthly mortgage in Goldsboro is $1,190 compared to $1,142 for Wayne County as a whole. The median household income in the city was lower than countywide. For Goldsboro, the median household income is $34,083 and in Wayne County, the median household income is $44,416. The percentage of people living in poverty in Goldsboro is also higher than Wayne County as a whole, Census figures show. People living in poverty in Goldsboro accounted for 26.1% of the population and in Wayne County, 18.6%. When it comes to education, 86.4% of people in Goldsboro graduated from high school while 20.3% have a bachelor’s degree or higher. In Wayne County overall, 84.5% of people graduated from high school while 19% have a bachelor’s degree or higher. In health care, 15.4% of people in Wayne County overall were listed as not having health insurance compared to 12.6% of people living in Goldsboro. CHANGES ACROSS NORTH CAROLINAThe state’s population grew by 9.5% during the last 10 years, data from Carolina Demography shows. Carolina Demography is located within the Carolina Population Center at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and uses population-level data and other available research to provide context and perspective to people working to shape North Carolina’s future, according to its website. Carolina Demography researchers found Wake County is now the state’s most populous county with 1,129,410 people, followed by Mecklenburg County at 1,115,482, Guilford County at 541,299, Forsyth County at 382,590 and Cumberland County at 334,728. Johnston County is the fastest-growing county, which saw its population increase 28% from 168,878 to 215,999 during the past decade, according to Carolina Demography data. The population in Brunswick County increased by 27.2% from 107,431 to 136,693, followed by Cabarrus County where it increased 26.8%, from 178,011 to 225,804, Wake County increased by 25.4%, from 900,993 to 1,129,410, and Durham County increased 21.4%, where the population increased from 267,587 to 324,833. Researchers at Carolina Demography also found that 51 counties in North Carolina lost population during the past 10 years. The counties with the largest declines are Robeson, which lost 17,638 residents; Duplin, 9,790; Edgecombe, 7,652; Columbus, 7,475; and Halifax, 6,069.
