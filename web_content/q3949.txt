--- [Jame Eagan | Severance Wiki | Fandom] ---
Jame Eagan is the eighth and current CEO of Lumon Industries and the father of Helena Eagan. History[] Jame became CEO of Lumon in 2003, following in the footsteps of his father Phillip. Some time before becoming CEO, he sponsored the Jame Eagan Wintertide Fellowship, a scholarship program for students at the Myrtle Eagan School For Girls, as well as possibly other local schools. Jame is publicly credited as having invented the Severance procedure. However, the technology behind Severance was actually invented by Harmony Cobel, who was encouraged to share her knowledge freely with the rest of Lumon as part of her indoctrination.[1] Jame eventually had a daughter named Helena, in whom he saw the attributes of his revered ancestor Kier Eagan while she was growing up. However, as she grew older, he gradually no longer saw these traits in her, and found himself unable to love her as a result. He subsequently fathered a number of illegitimate children in secret in the hopes that they would grow up to reflect the qualities of Kier, but was disappointed to find that none of them did.[2] In order to keep his actions hidden from the public, he would send his "mistresses" to the Damona Birthing Retreat when the time came for them to give birth, where they would be sent to a specially-designated cabin which acted as a Severed space, presumably to ensure that the mothers would not remember what took place inside upon leaving.[3] When Natalie Kalen told Harmony Cobel she was fired, Cobel began to doubt that Natalie was really on with the Board. Jame spoke a single word to assure Cobel they were there. Cobel then tried to apologize and explain, but Natalie said they'd ended the call.[4] Jame came to the gala and told Helena that when he learned what her Innie tried to do to her, he'd cried. He also asked if she remembered him showing her the first prototype chip and how she'd said it was so pretty and everyone should get one.[5] Jame came to see his daughter Helena after her innie was activated during the Overtime Contingency and she told the public that the innies were being tortured. He called her a fetid moppet and left her to record a statement denouncing her prior statements.[6] On the day when Mark S. was scheduled to complete the Cold Harbor file, Jame joined his daughter in their family compound's living room as she ate a hard-boiled egg. When asked if he would eat, Jame responded that he would just watch. He appeared to become uncomfortable as he watched Helena eat, and commented that he wished she would eat her eggs raw instead. Later that day, he visited Helly on the severed floor and said she had tricked him, his Helly, confusing her.[3] Jame spoke with Helly about the night of the OTC, reflecting on the anger he felt before bluntly admitting that he doesn't love Helena. When she was younger, he saw the 'fire of Kier' within her, but that fire faded, causing him to obsessively sire more illegitimate children in a desperate effort to find the one that he saw Kier in. He mused that he had finally found him again, in Helly. Helly lunged at him with a pen and swore the Eagans would burn for what they did, but Jame stood unshaken and joyfully remarked on seeing Kier again, before leaving without another word. On the day of Cold Harbor's completion, Jame returned to the severed floor and entered a small room with a single console and a hanging portrait of Kier. Alongside Dr. Mauer, Jame oversaw the 'efficacy test' on Gemma Scouts's 25th Innie to observe if any residual emotions from her Outie would resurface, based on a traumatic memory trigger. He watched with glee as the Innie carried out the given task, but was surprised by the arrival of Mark Scout, erupting into a raging scream when the two escaped from the Cold Harbor testing room.[2] Personality[] Physical Description[] Jame is a tall, elderly man with short, neatly-groomed white hair and always dressed in a minimalist dark blue suit. --- [Lumon Industries | Severance Wiki | Fandom] ---
Lumon Industries is a multinational USA-based biotechnology corporation. Their headquarters are located in Kier, PE at their Branch 501 facility. The current CEO is Jame Eagan. Background[] Founded in 1865 by Kier Eagan, and subsequently run by generations of Eagans since, Lumon has grown from a business specializing in topical salves to a huge producer of drugs, biotech, medical equipment, cosmetics, personal care products, and more. According to the Branch 501 Severed Floor management, Lumon has been active for around 220 years (882 quarters) and operates in 206 countries around the world. Lumon pioneered the severance procedure, which divides a person's consciousness and memory. When severed, a person has no memory or knowledge of their workplace when they are not there, and no memory of the outside world when they are in the office. Much of the action of Severance takes place on Lumon Branch 501's severed floor. Lumon's first Severed offices publicly opened twelve years ago; however, a comment by Burt Goodman's husband Fields suggests that they may have been using Severance for longer than this. Although it is less apparent from the outside than on the Severed Floor, much of Lumon's internal workings resemble that of a religious cult, centered around the reverence of the company's founder, Kier Eagan. Paintings of Kier at various points in his life can be found throughout the Severed Floor, and Severed employees are encouraged to know Kier's sayings and philosophy by heart. In a conversation with his daughter Helena Eagan, current CEO Jame Eagan stated that the company's goal is to distribute Severance chips to every person on the planet, thus making them all "children of Kier." The exact motivation and true meaning behind this remains unknown at present. Lumon’s first product was a topical salve developed in 1866, only a year after the company’s founding, reflecting its early pharmaceutical roots. Its original logo—depicting a flask, spoon, and drop of fluid—symbolizes these beginnings and remains significant today, engraved on the Signet Ring worn by CEO Jame Eagan, a piece of regalia presumed to pass to Helena Eagan upon his “Revolving.” Within Branch 501, the Optics and Design department actively rotates paintings from the Kier Cycle throughout the Severed Floor, carefully curating the visual environment to reinforce ideological messaging. Following Severance Reform, modern motivational posters were introduced alongside traditional reverential imagery to maintain morale among Severed employees. Lumon’s official motto, “United in Severance,” encapsulates its mission of harmonizing humanity through the Severance procedure. In addition to operating in 206 countries, Lumon maintains at least five branches within the United States and holds the distinction of being the first fictional corporation to operate an official LinkedIn page as part of an immersive promotional campaign. Humanitarian Outreach & Global Expansion[] In a rare public-facing initiative, Jame Eagan directed Lumon to tackle the clean water crisis in drought-stricken Sub-Saharan Africa. After a visit to Libono, a severely affected village in northern Lesotho, Eagan established a new internal department focused on delivering clean, alkaline water to underserved communities. This effort led to the development of Klear, a patented filtration system that removes 99.1% of impurities, and Tumwater, a sodium bicarbonate-based organic additive that raises the water’s pH to safe alkaline levels. A groundbreaking underground pipeline—stretching nearly 500 miles—was constructed to bring groundwater to a new filtration center five miles outside Libono. The processed water is then distributed via Lumon wells throughout the village. Six months after deployment, cases of dysentery and hypernatremia plummeted, with residents showing marked improvement in health and vitality. In parallel, Lumon is also planning to open its first international charter school in Libono, expanding its network of over 20 U.S.-based educational institutions. Eagan positioned this initiative as an example of Lumon’s ability to “eradicate suffering” on a global scale, tying directly into the broader ideology of Kier’s vision and the company’s controversial Severance ambitions. The Board[] The Board is the secretive and mysterious agency which directs the activities of Lumon Industries. They have almost exclusively been seen to communicate with Lumon staff using Board Liaison Natalie Kalen as an intermediate. The list of Board members is currently unknown, aside from Jame Eagan.[1] History[] Lumon was founded in 1865 by Kier Eagan in the state of PE, beginning as a producer of topical salves. As of the late 20th Century, the company had expanded considerably and operated facilities in other towns near Kier, including an ether factory in Salt's Neck. Some time after Harmony Cobel graduated from the Myrtle Eagan School For Girls, she invented the neurosurgical technique known as Severance, which would go on to be used by Lumon to create Severed workforces. The credit for the invention of Severance would go to Jame Eagan. Recently, Jame Eagan's daughter Helena elected to undergo Severance as a publicity stunt, with the goal of furthering acceptance of the procedure in mainstream society. During a gala at which Helena was supposed to give a talk about her experiences as a Severed person, her "Innie" persona Helly R. was activated as a result of MDR employee Dylan G. engaging the Overtime Contingency, and seized the opportunity to speak out about Lumon's unethical treatment of Severed workers. This resulted in a flurry of controversy in the news media, forcing Helena to film an apology video where she claimed to have been inebriated while under the influence of a "non-Lumon medication." Other tenants include: - Diagnostics is a department that can assess whose brain a given Severance chip should be in, as well as look for data signatures to determine who programmed and/or deprogrammed it. - Janitorial and Hygiene is the department devoted to keeping Lumon clean, though whether its workers serve the Severed floor departments is unknown. They have access to the discarded materials of either Research and Development or Diagnostics. Except for MDR (Macrodata Refinement), D&R (Disposal and Reclamation), WN (Wellness), and O&D (Optics and Design), the meanings of the other abbreviations are unknown. Severed "Innie" employees on the Severed Floors of Lumon facilities are encouraged to follow the guidance of Kier as described in the three appendices of the Compliance Handbook, and various paintings and other iconography depicting Kier in a reverential light can be found throughout the Severed Floor. Non-Severed Lumon staff members such as Harmony Cobel, Mr. Drummond, and Dr. Mauer refer to themselves as serving Kier and his vision. Shortly before Gemma was planned to be killed at the conclusion of the Cold Harbor test, Mr. Drummond facilitated a ritualistic sacrifice of a baby goat from Mammalians Nurturable, which he stated would be entombed with a "cherished woman" so that it could guide her soul to Kier in the afterlife. Lumon's mysterious, ultimate goal appears to be the worldwide distribution of Severance chips, in order to fulfil Kier's "grand agendum" of eradicating all suffering from humankind. Mr. Drummond described it as Kier's "eternal war against pain", and he has on several occasions emphasised the history-changing ramifications of the Cold Harbor project. Presently, Lumon seems to be testing for any bleed-through effects of negative emotions and past memories in the Severance chip, in an apparent effort to perfect the technology before using it to serve their plans. Lumon's Core Principles[] Lumon upholds nine Core Principles, which are listed in the Compliance Handbook and Macrodata Refiner's Orientation Handbook: - Vision - Verve - Wit - Cheer - Humility - Benevolence - Nimbleness - Probity - Wiles These principles are apparently treated with near-religious significance by certain individuals both inside and outside the company, including Harmony Cobel and her aunt Celestine. The Myrtle Eagan School For Girls appears to employ a system in which each calendar year is assigned a virtue. For example, Harmony Cobel graduated from the school during the Year of Wiles. The Four Tempers[] The Four Tempers are a philosophical framework posited by Kier Eagan, in which he proposed that all human souls are composed of a mixture of four emotions or "tempers" - Woe, Frolic, Dread, and Malice - that must be balanced and controlled in order to live a good and effective life. The Kier Cycle oil painting "Kier Taming the Four Tempers" depicts Kier fighting with anthropomorphized representations of the Four Tempers; the dancers in a Waffle Party also wear costumes modeled after these personifications. Lumon's upper management apparently take the idea of the Four Tempers seriously, as seen when Drummond speaks with Helena following her near-drowning during an ORTBO. The four categories of numbers sorted by MDR Refiners (WO, FC, DR, and MA) correspond to the Four Tempers as they exist within the mind of Gemma Scout. According to the handbook from the Lexington Letter, WO numbers elicit "melancholy or despair", FC elicits "joy, gaiety, or ecstasy", DR elicits "fear, anxiety, or apprehension", and MA elicits "rage or a desire to do harm". The philosophy of balancing the Four Tempers also matches the handbook's to fill "each of five bins evenly with the four kinds of number clusters". The successful "refinement" of an MDR file results in the creation of a new Innie persona for Gemma on the Testing Floor. Kier Cycle Paintings[] Paintings of Kier at various points in his life may be found throughout the Lumon Branch 501 Severed Floor. These are collectively known as the "Kier Cycle." Known paintings include: - A portrait of Kier in his middle age (title unknown) - The Youthful Convalescence of Kier - The Courtship of Kier and Imogene - Kier Invites You to Drink of His Water - Kier Taming the Four Tempers - Kier Pardons his Betrayers - The Exalted Victory of Cold Harbor Kier Hymn[] The Kier Hymn (also referred to as the Kier Anthem[2]) is a song of unknown authorship extolling the virtues of Kier Eagan, the founder of Lumon Industries. A "punitive rendition" of the song was once performed by Harmony Cobel for the members of the Branch 501 Macrodata Refinement team; an 8-bit instrumental version was also played on a TV at the beginning of the Branch 501 MDR team's ORTBO excursion. Lyrics[] Kier, chosen one, Kier, Kier, brilliant one, Kier, Brings the bounty to the plain through the torment, through the rains, Progress, knowledge show no fear, Kier, chosen one, Kier. Notes and Trivia[] - The name "Lumon" may be derived from the Latin word "lumen", meaning "light" or "opening". - In conjunction with the company's historical connections with and manufacturing of ether, "Lumon" also brings to mind the now-debunked concept of the luminiferous aether, a hypothetical medium once believed to be responsible for the propagation of light throughout space. - Lumon Industries is the first and so far only fictional company to create a Linkedin account, which was set up as part of an immersive marketing campaign to promote the first season of Severance.
