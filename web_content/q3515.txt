Melville Broughton, Governor, U.S. Senator http://www.ncmarkers.com/Markers.aspx?sp=Markers&sv=H-53 http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Melville_Broughton Joseph Melville Broughton (November 17, 1888 – March 6, 1949) was the 60th Governor of North Carolina from 1941 to 1945. Biography He was born on November 17, 1888 in Raleigh, North Carolina. He attended Harvard Law School then worked as a school principal and journalist before actively entering the legal profession. As governor, one of his major legacies was the extension of the public school term from six to nine months. In 1948, Broughton was elected to the United States Senate, after defeating William B. Umstead, the appointed incumbent, in the Democratic primary. In November, Broughton won both a special election to complete the Senate term and an election for a full term. He took office on December 31, 1948, but his service in the Senate was brief, as he died on March 6, 1949 in Bethesda, Maryland. Legacy In 1959, a state mental hospital was named in his memory. Tuscany is a small province in the north central part of Italy.... In the thirteenth century, Tuscany was made a republic and two centuries later lost its independence to Germany. In 1500 when the republic became a grand duchy, Francis de Medici, Duke of Lorraine, a member of the famous Italian family was elected Grand Duke of Tuscany. Francis de Medici married Marie Theresia, princess of Austgria who belonged to the famous Hapsbury House, a German family founded in 1100 to which belonged the rulers of Austria from 1276 to 1918 (Rudolph 1 and Karl 1). The founder of the imperial house was Alabert III, Hapsbury and Hobhouse both derived from Habichtsburg, meaning hawk's castle, is taken from a castle built in the eleventh century by Werner, Bishop of Strassburg on the bank of the Aar River, in Swiss Canton of Aargau. The last great ruler of Austria was Franz Joseph. His nephew, Karl, abdicated at the end of the great war of 1918. He was the last Hapsburg on the throne. The English branch of the Hapsburg or Hobhouse family eventually became Broughton, descending from the Medici-Hapsburg marriage which off-springs for generations were the grand dukes of Tuscany. Reuben Hobhouse, or Hapsburg, son of Joseph was the first mentioned among the English records. He settled in Somerset County, England and followed by his two younger brothers, John and Benjamin (Hobhouse) Broughton, who settled in Wilts County a few miles from Reuben. Sir John and Lady Mary Broughton had issue: A careful search of the historical records has been made and the following appears definitely to be the names of their four sons who came to America about 1700 - John, Abel, Thomas, Reuben,and Mary. There is a tradition that John Broughton with his three brothers came to America in their early manhood.They evidently settled in Virginia. The records show that three of them did. Thomas Broughton who was Colonial Governor of South Carolina is believed to be one of these four brothers. He married the daughter of Sir Nathaniel Johnson in 1716. The early NC records show that Abel Broughton lived in Craven County, one of the original precincts of the Lords Proprietors. His descendants spread into Perquimans and other nearby counties very early.
