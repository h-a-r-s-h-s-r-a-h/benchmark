--- [National Supercomputing Mission - Department Of Science & Technology] ---
National Supercomputing Mission (NSM) was set up to provide the country with supercomputing infrastructure to meet the increasing computational demands of academia, researchers, MSMEs, and startups. It is a first of its kind attempt to boost the country’s computing power. NSM is steered jointly by Department of Science and Technology (DST) and Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) and implemented by Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC), Pune and Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru. With the recommendations of Technical Advisory Committee and approval of Executive Board, a revised plan based on buy to build-approach was put in place. The first indigenously assembled Supercomputer, PARAM Shivay, was installed at Indian Institute of Technology (Banaras Hindu University), Varanasi and inaugurated by the Hon’ble Prime Minister. Similarly, High-Performance Computing system at Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati was dedicated to the nation by the Hon’ble President of India. In September 2024, the Hon’ble Prime Minister inaugurated three PARAM Rudra systems, at Inter-University Accelerator Centre (IUAC), New Delhi, Giant Metrewave Radio Telescope (GMRT) facility operated by National Centre for Radio Astrophysics (NCRA), Pune and S. N. Bose National Centre for Basic Sciences, Kolkata. The installations completed as on 30 November 2025, along with their details, are provided below. All these systems are primarily equipped with applications catering to domains including Weather and Climate, Computational Fluid Dynamics, Bioinformatics, and Material Science. --- [Atos installs its first supercomputer in India at Institute of Technology (IIT ...] ---
Atos, a global leader in digital transformation, today announces the installation of its first supercomputer in India. The BullSequana, which is the first supercomputer to be installed as part of the Indian Government’s National Supercomputing Mission (NSM), was officially inaugurated at IIT-BHU in Varanasi by the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi. Named ‘Param Shivav’, this supercomputer is the first of several to be installed across India as part of a major 3-year agreement between C-DAC (Centre for Development of Advanced Computing), an organization within the MeitY (Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology, India) and Atos. It is part of the NSM (National Supercomputing Mission) – a 7-year plan of INR 4500 crores (~650M$) led by the Government of India which aims to create a network of over 70 high-performance supercomputing facilities for various academic and research institutions across India. The supercomputer is equipped with the latest processors and accelerators on the market, to provide optimal performance and improved energy efficiency. It will be used for academic projects and research. “We are proud that our BullSequana supercomputer is empowering researchers at the IIT-BHU to accelerate their digital simulation projects. This installation marks the start of a strategic relationship and partnership between France and India and cements our position as the leading supercomputing provider in India for HPC.” said Pierre Barnabé, Chief Operating Officer, Big Data & Security at Atos.

--- [Param Shivay Celebrates Completion Of Three Years Of ... - Times of India] ---
VARANASI: ‘Param Shivay’ India’s first indigenously assembled supercomputer at the Indian Institute of Technology, BHU in Varanasi has completed three years marking a significant journey for the National Supercomputing Mission (NSM). Marking a major milestone in 2019, ‘Param Shivay’ was inaugurated at the IIT-BHU as a part of the NSM in the presence of Prime Minister Narendra Modi and IIT(BHU) director Prof Pramod Kumar Jain. The IIT(BHU) director congratulated the coordinator of the facility, Hari Prabhat Gupta and others on the successful three-year journey of this supercomputer. He said that the development of this high-performance computing system is a testament to India’s growing capabilities in the field of technology and its commitment to advancing scientific research and innovation. According to him, IIT (BHU) has reached new heights with the successful operation of the ‘Param Shivay’ supercomputer, which has been running non-stop for 24 hours a day, seven days a week, for the past three years, even during the Covid pandemic. Managed by the IIT (BHU), the centre has become a crucial resource for research for not only the institute and BHU, but also for organisations and institutes across the country. He said that the ‘Param Shivay’ provides supercomputing facilities to over 23 premium institutes across the country, including various IITs, NITs, IIITs, and many more and has successfully handled over 850 active users simultaneously for the past three years. The supercomputer has become a beacon of hope for researchers in India, who now have access to cutting-edge technology and facilities that are on par with the best in the world. The successful operation of the ‘Param Shivay’ supercomputer has proved the unwavering commitment of the institute to provide the best for its researchers, and for the advancement of scientific and technological research in India . A number of publications have recognised the capabilities of the ‘Param Shivay’ supercomputer at IIT(BHU). The facility has a high computation utilization, serving a wide range of domains including AI/ML/DL, Computational Science, Computational Physics, Quantum Mechanics, Quantum Chemistry, atomic and molecular sciences, computational fluid dynamics, neuro-informatics, bioinformatics, civil engineering and meteorology, astrophysics, biomedical engineering, chemical engineering, pharmaceutics and drug discovery, health and medical, pollution control, astrophysics, mechanics and automobile, Artificial Intelligence, and robotics. When the server was first established, it was equipped with applications that catered to limited domains. However, with continued user feedback and requests and support from a dedicated team, the server now contains a vast selection of open-source applications that cater to many more domains. The institute has provided both financial and infrastructure support, and is now extending its support to reach the next milestone of converting more application domains, research directors, and critical missions into uses. Prof. Jain expressed his appreciation for the hard work and dedication of the team behind the ‘Param Shivay’ supercomputer. He acknowledged the institute’s commitment to providing financial and infrastructural support, which has been instrumental in the success of the supercomputer. With continued support from IIT(BHU), the supercomputer is poised to reach even greater heights and make a positive impact on the advancement of research and innovation in the country.


--- [India progressing rapidly towards the goal of indigenously made ...] ---
New Delhi: India is fast expanding its supercomputer facilities and developing the capacity to manufacture its own supercomputers in the country. The National Super Computing Mission (NSM) is rapidly boosting high power computing in the country through its various phases to meet the increasing computational demands of academia, researchers, MSMEs, and startups in areas like oil exploration, flood prediction as also genomics, and drug discovery. With the infrastructure planned in NSM Phase-I already installed and much of Phase-II in place, the network of supercomputers through the country will soon reach to around 16 Petaflops (PF). Phase-III, to be initiated in January 2021, will take the computing speed to around 45 Petaflops. NSM is jointly steered by the Ministry of Electronics and IT (MeitY) and Department of Science and Technology (DST) and implemented by the Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC), Pune and the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru. Param Shivay, the first supercomputer assembled indigenously, was installed in IIT (BHU), followed by Param Shakti and Param Brahma at IIT-Kharagpur and IISER, Pune, respectively. Thereafter supercomputing facilities were set up in two more institutions, and one is being set up in Phase-I, ramping up high power computing speed to 6.6 PF under Phase-1. In Phase-II, 8 more institutions will be equipped with supercomputing facilities by April 2021, with a total of 10 PF compute capacity. MoUs have been signed with total 14 premier institutions of India for establishing Supercomputing Infrastructure with Assembly and Manufacturing in India. These include IITs, NITs, National Labs, and IISERs. Some of these have already been installed, and some more will be done by December this year. The Phase-II installations will be completed by April 2021. The three phases will provide access to High-Performance Computing (HPC) Facilities to around 75 institutions and more than thousands of active researchers, academicians working through Nation Knowledge Network (NKN) – the backbone for supercomputing systems. HPC and Artificial Intelligence (AI) have converged together. A 100 AI PF Artificial Intelligence supercomputing system is being created and installed in C-DAC, which can handle incredibly large-scale AI workloads increasing the speed of computing-related to AI several times. The mission has also created the next generation of supercomputer experts by training more than 2400 supercomputing manpower and faculties till date. Indigenous capability Powered by the NSM, India’s network of research institutions, in collaboration with the industry, is scaling up the technology and manufacturing capability to make more and more parts in India. While in Phase-I, 30 percent value addition is done in India, that has been scaled up to 40 percent in Phase-II. Efforts are being made to design and develop parts like server board, interconnect, processor, system software libraries, storage, and HPC-AI converged accelerator domestically. India has developed an Indigenous server (Rudra), which can meet the HPC requirements of all governments and PSUs. This is the first time that a server system was made in India, along with the full software stack developed by C-DAC. Experts said that the pace at which things are moving forward, we may soon have the motherboards and sub-systems manufactured in India, making the supercomputers indigenously designed and manufactured. Such indigenously designed systems with most parts designed and manufactured in India will be installed at places like IIT-Mumbai, IIT-Chennai, and Inter-University Accelerator Centre (IUAC) at Delhi, C-DAC, Pune, which are covered under Phase-III and help move towards supercomputers developed and manufactured totally in India paving the way for self-reliance in the field.
