The Honda Battle of the Bands (HBOB) is an annual music competition and showcase event organized by Honda since 2003, dedicated to celebrating the marching bands of Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs) through high-energy performances that highlight Black musical expression, cultural traditions, and instrumental excellence.[1]Initiated as a commitment by Honda to amplify the talent of HBCU marching bands, the event has evolved into what is often called the "marching band homecoming of the year," drawing thousands of participants, alumni, and fans to witness showcases featuring top university ensembles. Although called a "battle," the event is a non-competitive showcase where all participating bands are celebrated equally.[1] Its purpose extends beyond entertainment, serving as a platform to preserve and promote HBCU band culture on a global stage, with performances that blend precision drill routines, choreography, and innovative arrangements of popular music.[1] Over the years, HBOB has gained recognition from major media outlets, including The New York Times, for its role in fostering community pride and showcasing the vibrancy of Black collegiate arts.[1][2]The event typically involves a selection process through public voting by fans, HBCU band directors, students, and Honda representatives, leading to live showcases at prominent venues, such as Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta for traditional events or SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles in 2025.[1][3] Each participating band receives a grant from Honda, such as $50,000 in recent years, to support music education and career development programs, with additional scholarships available through the HBCU Band Scholarship Program.[4][5] Beyond the main event, HBOB encompasses related initiatives like the Honda Campus All-Star Challenge, a quiz bowl for HBCU students, further emphasizing educational and cultural engagement.[1]In a notable expansion, the 2025 edition marked HBOB's first West Coast Invitational Showcase, held at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles on February 1, where six HBCU bands performed, bringing the event's legacy to new audiences and underscoring its growing national influence.[6] This milestone reflected Honda's ongoing dedication to evolving the program while honoring its roots in HBCU traditions.[6] Event Overview Description and Purpose The Honda Battle of the Bands (HBOB) is an annual invitational exhibition event sponsored by the American Honda Motor Company since 2003, featuring live performances by marching bands from Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs).[7] As the nation's premier showcase for HBCU marching bands, it provides a non-competitive platform for these ensembles to display their musical prowess, rather than engaging in formal judging or rivalry.[1] The event typically draws tens of thousands of attendees, including students, alumni, and fans, creating a vibrant atmosphere that celebrates collegiate traditions.[8]The primary purpose of HBOB is to highlight the talent, creativity, and precision of HBCU marching bands while fostering school spirit and promoting higher education opportunities at these institutions.[7] By featuring original halftime shows recruitment and enrollment efforts.[9] Honda supports this mission through institutional grants to participating schools for music education programs, reinforcing the event's role in advancing HBCU cultural and academic legacies.[10]HBOB serves as a key platform for cultural expression rooted in African American heritage, where bands incorporate distinctive elements such as high-stepping marches, intricate drill formations, and auxiliary units featuring dance and color guard routines.[11][12] These performances blend genres like jazz, gospel, R&B, and hip-hop, transforming military-style precision into dynamic spectacles that honor Black musical traditions and community pride.[12] Through this showcase, the event not only entertains but also educates audiences on the enduring significance of HBCU band culture as a pillar of higher education and artistic innovation.[7] Format and Structure The Honda Battle of the Bands operates as a one-day invitational showcase, typically scheduled in late January or early February at a major stadium venue, such as SoFi Stadium for the 2025 edition on February 1.[13] The event unfolds over an afternoon timeframe, with doors opening around 11:00 a.m., a pre-show commencing at noon, and the main program starting at 1:00 p.m., encompassing individual band performances and concluding within a few hours.[14] It features six --- [Honda Battle of the Bands - Wikipedia] ---
The Honda Battle of the Bands (sometimes abbreviated The Honda or HBOB) is an annual marching band exhibition in the United States which features performances by bands from historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs). Sponsored by the American Honda Motor Company, the Invitational Showcase took place in the Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, Georgia in late January from 2003 to 2020.  In 2022, it was announced HBOB selected Alabama State University in Montgomery, Alabama to be the first ever HBCU campus to host the in-person event in February 2023. Since 2023, the HBOB has been moving to new locations. In 2025, HBOB was held in the SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, California.
The Honda Battle of the Bands Celebration Tour takes place between the months of September and November at regular season football games, although Honda Battle of the Bands (and its abbreviations) is often intended to refer only to the invitational showcase, which first took place in 2003. Seemingly contradictory to the name, Honda's "battle" is not a competition in the traditional sense; that is, no winner is crowned during the event. Rather, the bands compete for the favor of the audience, each other, and the greater community.
The event is historically one of the most popular collegiate marching band event in the nation, drawing over 50,000 fans and spectators annually.

--- [Honda Battle of the Bands moving to Montgomery, AL] ---
TORRANCE, Calif., Sept. 6, 2022 /PRNewswire/ — Honda and the HBCU community will celebrate the return of Honda Battle of the Bands (HBOB) to a live event format Saturday, February 18, 2023, showcasing spectacular marching bands and dance teams from Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs). The premiere marching band event for HBCUs, HBOB also will be held in a new location when the invitational showcase takes place for the first time on an HBCU campus at Alabama State University in Montgomery, Alabama. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the live showcase event has not been held since 2020. Previous HBOB events were held in Atlanta. “I am proud of the special relationship Honda has with HBCUs,” said Yvette Hunsicker, vice president of Corporate Social Responsibility and Inclusion & Diversity at American Honda. “Born out of more than three decades of collaboration on programs that support and celebrate the dreams of HBCU students as the next generation of Black leaders, we continue to build on our relationship with HBCUs in new and meaningful ways. In 2023, we will take Honda Battle of the Bands to an HBCU campus and look forward to sharing our inspirational HBCU journey with loyal HBOB fans and society.” “We are extremely excited that Alabama State University has been selected as the site for the 2023 Honda Battle of the Bands,” said ASU President, Dr. Quinton T. Ross, Jr. “By bringing the popular event to the ASU campus, Honda has advanced and strengthened its longstanding support of the nation’s HBCUs. The ASU stadium will provide a perfect setting for the Honda Battle of the Bands; and I know that with the support of Hornet Nation, the City of Montgomery and HBOB fans, the event will be a great success.” In support of the live event, the 2023 program will include digital activations, pre-event activities in the host city of Montgomery, and the Honda Royal Fleet featuring select HBCU homecoming royalty. Tickets are on sale now at www.hondabattleofthebands.com.
