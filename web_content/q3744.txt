--- [Moving House (1876; Russian Federation) by Viktor Vasnetsov] ---
“Moving House,” created by Viktor Vasnetsov in 1876, is a genre painting rendered in oil on canvas, belonging to the Realism movement. The artwork measures 53.5 by 67.2 cm and is housed in the Tretyakov Gallery in Moscow, Russia. The artwork vividly portrays two elderly figures, clad densely to protect themselves from the harsh winter conditions, as they traverse a snow-covered landscape. The figures are burdened with a large sack, suggestive of their belongings, evoking a narrative of displacement and hardship. In the background, a distant structure with a noticeable spire rises amidst the subdued tones of the sky, while a few birds are mid-flight, adding a sense of stillness and desolation to the scene. A small dog, with a hunched posture, walks alongside them, reinforcing the theme of companionship in adversity. The overall mood is somber, with a muted color palette enhancing the starkness and realism of their predicament.
