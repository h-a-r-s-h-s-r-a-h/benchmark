--- [Aunt Jemima | South Park Public Library | Fandom] ---
Aunt Jemima was a breakfast icon for the Quaker Oats Company who appeared in the Season Eighteen episode, "Gluten Free Ebola", where she tells Eric Cartman, in his delirious dream, that the USDA's food pyramid is upside down. Appearance[] Aunt Jemima wears a red, white, and blue dress with a white lace collar, pearl earrings, black shoes, and a yellow and white kerchief on her head. She is seen holding a plate of pancakes. Trivia[] - Aunt Jemima's role in the episode is a reference to Mother Abagail from Stephen King's book The Stand. - She is the second ally of Cartman seen in a dream, the other being the Pharaoh of Egypt. - In June 2021 the Aunt Jemima product was rebranded to "Pearl Milling Company" due to the original name and image being based on a racial stereotype.

--- [Gluten Free Ebola - Wikipedia] ---
"Gluten Free Ebola" is the second episode in the eighteenth season of the American animated television series South Park. The 249th overall episode, it was written and directed by series co-creator Trey Parker. The episode premiered on Comedy Central in the United States on October 1, 2014. The episode lampoons the trend of the gluten-free diet lifestyle and the constant changes recommended to the Western pattern diet and the then-current food guide.


--- [Aunt Jemima's Shack | South Park Public Library | Fandom] ---
Aunt Jemima's Shack was featured in Season Eighteen episode "Gluten Free Ebola". Background[] The Boys loses hope on organizing a party following the widespread destruction of gluten products. During one night, Eric Cartman had a nightmare where the guests criticize the party, before finding Aunt Jemima sitting outside a shack with a plate of pancakes on her lap. She informs Cartman that the food pyramid is upside down and instructs him to contact the USDA about it.
