--- [Scandinavian Masters - Wikipedia] ---
The Scandinavian Masters was an annual golf tournament on the European Tour played in Sweden. In 2020, the tournament became co-sanctioned with the Ladies European Tour and was rebranded as the Scandinavian Mixed, in which both male and female golfers competed.

Currently the only European Tour event in Scandinavia, the Nordea Scandinavian Masters has been held since 1991 under a variety of sponsorship names. The tournament’s origins date to the late 1960s, when it was the Volvo Open. Later, that was renamed the Scandinavian Enterprise Open. In 1991, the Scandinavian Enterprise Open merged with the PLM Open to form the Scandinavian masters. Nordea is a financial services group in the Nordic and Baltic regions. Golf - Scandinavian Mixed This tournament, which has always been played in Sweden, was originally known as the Scandinavian Masters and was the only stop in the region on the European Tour until the Made in Denmark tournament began. Before 2011, it was staged at the end of July or the beginning of August but from 2012 it was shifted to early June. Venues over the years have included Stockholm, Malmo and Rosersberg. The tournament was not staged in 2020 due to the Covid-19 pandemic.
