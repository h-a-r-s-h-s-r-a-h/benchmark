--- [Dæmonen - Wikipedia] ---
Dæmonen is a floorless steel roller coaster at the Tivoli Gardens amusement park in Copenhagen, Denmark. Designed by Bolliger & Mabillard, it reaches a height of 28 metres (92 ft), is 564.0 metres (1,850.4 ft) long, and reaches a maximum speed of  77 kilometres per hour (48 mph). The roller coaster features a vertical loop, an Immelmann loop, and a zero-gravity roll. Dæmonen replaced Slangen, a family roller coaster, and officially opened on 16 April 2004. A record number of guests attended the park that year, but the public has since given the ride mixed reviews.

We visited mid week in July, as Seniors (receiving a slight discount on the ticket price); this isn't an inexpensive place to visit but we felt it was well worth it. The tour starts in rather a dark environment, leading through to the first of the exhibit rooms and it is a stunning start - the glass is intricate, luminescent and quite, quite beautiful; one could look at it for ages and still find something new to marvel at. The scale of some of the exhibits leaves one in awe of the work involved, not just in the concept and design but the production of all the individual parts and the construction of the whole. Visiting when we did, the place was reasonably quiet and so one had a good view of the glass, both for admiring it and for taking photos - photography IS allowed but no selfie sticks, gimbals or tripods - the rooms are kept dark, so a handheld twilight or night scene setting gives good colours. There is one room full of wide, deep bowls placed on stands so that they are at about eye level (granted that I'm not very tall) which seemed inlit but were actually responding to the downlight from the spots in the ceiling, rendering the sides of the bowls almost translucent. The garden is rather magical and, visiting on a sunny day, the glass sculptures catch the sunlight and yet blend in with the carefully curated plants and shrubs. There was a demonstration of glass blowing taking place outside - standing room only - you need to look at the timings to grab a front row or be really tall to get the best out of this. I notice from the website that there is an audio tour which looks as if you can load up on your phone; this wasn't pointed out to us when we bought our tickets and a heads up would have been useful because I think we would both have used it. The book and gift shop is well stocked, lots of arty items, garden themed items and some glassware - I did go and have a look at the very modestly sized vases but baulked at the starting price of around $7000 and rising to $13500, they were beautiful but just too pricey - it would have been lovely to have some glass items within the shop within an affordable price range. The only thing I could find was a Christmas tree bauble at $65, it was lovely but far too big and heavy to actually hang on a tree, it would require a stand. So I exited via the gift shop with just a bag of postcards, feeling I would have liked a more substantial souvenir of the visit. The glass here really struck a chord deep within me, it was gorgeous, uplifting and awesome in it's intricacy and beauty, I am sure this is going to be a regular trip for us when we are in Seattle.
