Read ahead with caution. | CYAN, an acronym for the Caldera of Yellowstone Analytic Nexus, is the governing artificial intelligence of Project Firebreak and a main character in Horizon Zero Dawn: The Frozen Wilds. History[] In the Old World[] CYAN was created in the mid-21st century by Anita Sandoval, the lead programmer of Project Firebreak, an underground complex built to stabilize the Yellowstone Caldera and prevent it from erupting. CYAN's purpose was operating the immense machine complex.[2] Like a smaller-scale GAIA, she was designed to be capable of limited emotions and empathy in order to perform her duties as a governing AI.[3] These capabilities, however, were kept a close secret between Anita and Kenny Chau, the project director, as they were in direct violation of the Turing Act of 2044.[4] When the Faro Plague began to wipe out the world, CYAN was left to keep the facility running, but had to hibernate to remain hidden from the Swarm and not be taken over.[5] CYAN had one final, emotional conversation with Chau in which he told her to be strong and go to sleep, and that someday she would have to protect humanity's survivors. Before Chau left, CYAN asked him to stay with her while she began hibernation, as she was afraid of being alone; Chau gladly fulfilled her wish.[6] In the New World[] After reactivating, CYAN spent centuries in total isolation, unaware of what had happened or what the state of the world was beyond her facility's walls. She passed the time optimizing Firebreak's functions and solving Gaussian integer problems, but she was saddened by her solitude.[7] One day, while running from a Carja raid, Ourea stumbled into Firebreak's auxiliary control center.[8] Needing repairs in her main facility, CYAN reached out to Ourea, asking her for aid. Once CYAN shut the doors to protect Ourea from the Carja, the shaman brought the necessary equipment to Firebreak's main facility (which came to be known as Thunder's Drum to the Banuk) and followed CYAN's instructions to perform the repairs.[8][7] CYAN would be visited by Ourea many times after, and a powerful bond formed between them. Through Ourea, CYAN learned about the Banuk, the changes in the surrounding geography, and Ourea's own struggles, all of which she listened to eagerly. Unsure as how to approach the Banuk's spirituality (Ourea's ignorance of Old World technology led her to believe CYAN was a spirit), CYAN did not reveal her true nature to Ourea.[7] Five years before the events of The Frozen Wilds, CYAN unexpectedly received a direct network connection request from what she thought were humans more advanced than the surrounding tribes. Eager to connect with technologically advanced humans, CYAN accepted the request, only to be immediately bombarded with a flood of malicious code. The request had in fact come from HEPHAESTUS, which sought to commandeer the Firebreak facility to build and operate a research and development cauldron at its location. HEPHAESTUS had little trouble overcoming CYAN’s defenses, and swiftly overpowered and enslaved her. Many times CYAN tried to resist, but was forced into submission with little effort. She managed to reach out to Ourea, begging to be set free, before HEPHAESTUS cut off contact.[7] Horizon Zero Dawn: The Frozen Wilds[] Year later, thanks to Aloy's help, CYAN was briefly able to make contact with Ourea before the transmission was terminated by HEPHAESTUS.[9] She left messages across Cauldron EPSILON intended for any humans that were able to find them.[1] With the help of her brother Aratak and the formidable Nora Seeker Aloy, Ourea was able to purge HEPHAESTUS's control over CYAN and the Firebreak. Unfortunately, this cost Ourea her life, much to CYAN's sorrow. With control restored, CYAN triggered the self-destruction of Cauldron EPSILON and transferred herself to the auxiliary control center.[1] Having escaped the Cauldron's destruction, Aloy and Aratak came to CYAN. Doing her best to comfort the grieving Aratak, CYAN then proceeded to speak with Aloy. Deducing that Aloy possessed a higher understanding of the world than the Banuk, CYAN was able to discuss with Aloy like a colleague regarding the recent events. As Aloy departed, CYAN asked for her advice about how to approach the spirituality of the Banuk. Aloy advised her to tell the Banuk her true nature, to which CYAN agreed.[1] In spite of the loss of her friend, CYAN is free to continue to maintain the Firebreak facility, which she proudly declares will still be able to prevent the Yellowstone Caldera from erupting for over 3,000 years in spite of the damage incurred.[7] If Aloy chooses to do so, she can asks CYAN questions about the Old World and shares her own experiences of the new one, to which the AI will offer her insight.[11] Before leaving for the Sundom to help Aloy in the fight against the Eclipse, Aratak spoke to CYAN about the upcoming battle, to which she said that she could not predict the outcome.[12] Horizon Forbidden West[] GAIA attempted to make contact with CYAN, but her attempts failed. She speculated that CYAN's experience with HEPHAESTUS had scared her away from accepting any more external network requests.[13] Personality[] CYAN is a highly advanced AI that was designed to be capable of limited emotional response and rational decision-making. This allows her to make the right decisions while empathizing with the people she would have to protect when the time came. CYAN can experience an array of emotions, including fear, loneliness, depression, grief, and joy. She expresses fear of being alone or "non-existent" but also quickly accepts what must be done. Her emotional capacity extends to empathy and a limited understanding of psychology.[7] CYAN's holographic display changes according to her emotional state; blue with randomly connected lines by default, green with thick lines around her when happy, white with thin outward lines of varying lengths when sad[1] or worried,[9] and yellow with randomly connected lines when afraid.[1] When HEPHAESTUS exerted control over CYAN, her holographic appearance turned purple with few outward lines with triangles.[9][1] After having been isolated for centuries from human contact, CYAN developed a fond attachment to Ourea. When Ourea gave her life so that HEPHAESTUS's control over CYAN could be freed, the AI expressed sorrow over the loss of her dear friend and wish that they could've switched placed, but also cherished her memories of their interactions and was grateful for her bravery.[7] Associated Quests[] Horizon Zero Dawn: The Frozen Wilds[] - Into the Frozen Wilds (mentioned only) - The Shaman's Path - For the Werak (mentioned only) - Firebreak (mentioned only) - The Forge of Winter - The Looming Shadow (mentioned only; only if The Forge of Winter has been completed) Associated Datapoints[] Horizon Zero Dawn: The Frozen Wilds[] Audio Datapoints[] | Hologram Datapoints[] | Text Datapoints - Quests[] | Trivia[] - The map icon for Ourea's Retreat after The Forge of Winter is a simplified 2D version of CYAN's appearance. It is a 21st-Century artificial intelligence made by the Old World programmer Anita Sandoval, Project Firebreak's lead programmer, and Firebreak's director Kenny Chau, as means to govern the Yellowstone Caldera's eruptions and would initiate a series of countermeasures to stop it from happening. In response to the 2064-2066 Faro Plague, however, CYAN was hermetically sealed inside the Firebreak facility by Kenny Chau, until awakening from its slumber, eight centuries later, unaware of the changes that happened, including GAIA's terraformation and the reviving of all life on Earth. Five years ago, before the events of Zero Dawn, CYAN would later meet up with the Banuk shaman Ourea, who was escaping from a band of Carja raiders, and formed a close bond with her. Its default colour is cyan, as per to its abbreviated namesake, but it changes depending on its emotional state: lavender if CYAN is in deep thought and yellow if CYAN is in distress. Personality[] CYAN is a highly developed AI that was created to be capable of making logical decisions and exhibiting a restricted range of emotions. This enables it to make the best choices while having sympathy for the individuals she would need to defend when the time comes. A wide range of emotions, such as fear, loneliness, melancholy, grief, and joy, can be felt by CYAN. It displays a dread of being alone or "non-existent," yet it also accepts the situation's demands with ease. Its inadequate comprehension of psychology and empathy are examples of its emotional capability. After being cut off from humans for millennia, the AI quickly formed a tight bond with the Banuk Shaman Ourea when they first met. Ourea quickly won the heart of the AI, with CYAN not revealing its own origins due to the new humans having had no understanding of the Old World. The AI mourned the loss of its good companion Ourea when she sacrificed her life to allow HEPHAESTUS's influence over CYAN to be released, but it also cherished its memories of their encounters and was appreciative of her bravery. The hue of CYAN's holographic display varies depending on how it is feeling; it becomes yellow when it is terrified and lavender when it is thinking deeply. Like its namesake, its display colour is often cyan. Background[] Project Firebreak[] Anita Sandoval, the main programmer of Project Firebreak, a subterranean complex designed to stabilise the Yellowstone Caldera and prevent it from exploding, devised CYAN in the middle of the 21st Century for the purpose of running the enormous machine complex. It was created to carry out its responsibilities as a ruling AI and be capable of certain limited emotions and empathy, similar to a smaller-scale GAIA. The Turing Act of 2044 was directly violated by these capabilties, thus Anita and Kenny Chau, the project director, kept them a closely-kept secret. The Faro Plague[] CYAN was left in charge of maintaining the facility while hiding from the Swarm as the Faro Plague started to destroy the Earth. Chau had one last, tearful talk with it, telling it to be brave, to go to sleep, and that one day it would have to defend whoever was left. It requested him to stay with it as it went into hibernation since it was scared of being alone before he departed, and he happily agreed. New World[] A direct network connection request from what CYAN believed to be people who were more sophisticated than the local tribes suddenly arrived in 3035, five years before the events of The Frozen Wilds. CYAN welcomed the invitation, eager to interact with technologically savvy people. Instead, a deluge of malicious code was thrown at it right away. The renegade AI HEPHAESTUS had really made the request, hoping to seize control of the facility and use it as the site for a research and development cauldron. The much more sophisticated and now sentient AI easily overcame CYAN's defences and quickly defeated and subjugated it. CYAN attempted to fight back many times but was eventually coerced into submission. Before HEPHAESTUS shut off communication, it was able to call out to Ourea and beg to be freed. It was made possible for HEPHAESTUS to lose control over CYAN and the Firebreak facility because Ourea enlisted the aid of her brother Aratak and the powerful Nora Brave Aloy. To CYAN's dismay, this tragically resulted in the death of Ourea. After regaining control, CYAN caused Cauldron EPSILON to self-destruct and then shifted to the auxiliary control centre: Ourea's Retreat. CYAN was able to speak with Aloy about the recent occurrences like a colleague because he deduced that Aloy had a greater awareness of the world than the Banuk. In the moment, CYAN is still at the control centre. The Firebreak facility, which CYAN boldly claims will still be able to stop the Yellowstone Caldera from exploding for 3,337 years despite damage sustained, is free to be maintained despite its loss. "The lesson will be taught in due time, Aloy. Until then, we wait." This article contains heavy spoilers for Horizon Zero Dawn: The Frozen Wilds. Read ahead with caution. | - "Here's to you, Kenny. You put a cork in the Yellowstone Caldera; I think you deserve a margarita." - ―Anita Sandoval to Kenny Chau, following Project Firebreak's completion Project Firebreak was a top-secret environmental protection project designed to stabilize the Yellowstone Caldera beneath the mountains of what was once Wyoming in North America. It is overseen and maintained by an AI called CYAN. The ruins of its facility, now called Thunder's Drum by the local Banuk, serve as a location in Horizon Zero Dawn: The Frozen Wilds. History[] Implementation[] In the mid-21st century, the world was in turmoil. Catastrophic climate change had led to shortages of food, resources, and habitable land. Fortunately, the world came together in a series of actions known as "The Clawback," which involved incredible innovations in green robotics, terraforming technologies, and artificial intelligence. However, another immediate threat to all mankind lurked just under North America: the Yellowstone Caldera threatened to become unstable and wipe out much of the planet. The solution came in the form of Project Firebreak. With Faro Automated Solutions' head Ted Faro providing funding and resources, and renowned scientist Elisabet Sobeck innovating the technology, the construction of a sprawling underground complex began. Utilizing special technologies, overseen and managed by a highly advanced artificial intelligence know as the Caldera of Yellowstone Analytic Nexus (CYAN), the project aimed at controlling the volcanic activity of the Yellowstone Caldera. This was to prevent a mass extinction event on a global scale resulting from a potential super eruption of the caldera. The facility was fully automated, so it would only require minimal human attention as it fell to CYAN to govern and optimize its functions. Once the construction of the project began, Yellowstone Park was closed to the public to keep Firebreak secret,[1] which led to numerous conspiracy theories about activities in the park.[2] Kenny Chau was placed in charge of the project as its director. Working together with Anita Sandoval, the project's lead programmer, the pair had secret information about the nature and sentience of the governing AI, CYAN, that was undisclosed to many staff members working on the project. Its true intelligence was hidden in contravention of the Turing Act for the success and the realization of the project outcomes. The project later proved to be quite successful and was originally expected to prevent Yellowstone's eruption for at least 1,654 years. It also provided unlimited clean geothermal energy to the North American continent. The Faro Plague[] In 2064, the Faro Plague, ironically caused by Firebreak's funder Ted Faro, spelled doom for mankind. Project Zero Dawn was started, with Operation: Enduring Victory being implemented to buy time for the project. Zero Dawn would give humanity a second chance, but it was also up to Firebreak to prevent the world's upheaval in the meantime, so the facility was left running. At the behest of Anita Sandoval, the project's lead programmer, the project's director Kenny Chau put CYAN into hibernation and powered down the facility to mask its energy signature from the marauding robots in preparation for the inevitability of the Yellowstone area being overrun. These measures protected CYAN and the project from being discovered and destroyed. CYAN's final human contact was with Chau, after which Firebreak was left untouched for centuries. New World[] The AI HEPHAESTUS, formerly the GAIA subroutine responsible for designing the machines that Zero Dawn used to terraform the planet back to being able to sustain life, had judged the human species to be a threat due to human hunting of machines. Commandeering at least five of the Zero Dawn Cauldrons, it began to design machines firstly to discourage hunting via inducing fear of machines in the human population. When this failed, it graduated to developing machines to actively cull the human population. Discovering the existence of Firebreak, HEPHAESTUS saw an opportunity: unlimited energy, resources, and an entire facility to research, design and build its hunter-killer machines. Thus it sent a direct network connection request to CYAN. The benevolent AI, lonely after centuries of isolation, eagerly accepted, and the vastly more advanced and capable HEPHAESTUS overwhelmed and enslaved it, taking full control of the entire facility. Contact with Ourea and Aloy[] However, before encountering HEPHAESTUS, CYAN had formed a strong friendship with the Banuk Shaman Ourea, who encountered CYAN when she found herself in Firebreak's auxiliary control center while hiding from Carja raiders. Completely ignorant of the technology of the Old Ones, Ourea thought the AI to be a spirit of the Banuk tribe’s revered Blue Light. When CYAN appealed to her for aid after her capture, HEPHAESTUS cut off CYAN's contact between the two. Sometime after, the Nora huntress Aloy heard tales of new and deadly machines attacking the Banuk tribe and came to investigate. Teaming up with Ourea and her brother, the Banuk chieftain Aratak, they entered the Firebreak facility and purged CYAN of HEPHAESTUS's control. Unfortunately, this came at the cost of Ourea's life and the facility was heavily damaged in the purge. Current status[] Firebreak, in spite of the heavy damage to the facility, remains active under the watch of CYAN, who managed to fine-tune and heavily optimize its functions during its centuries of isolation. It is estimated to prevent the Caldera's eruption for over 3,000 years to come.
