--- [The Circle (American TV series) season 3 - Wikipedia] ---
The third season of the American reality competition streaming series The Circle began on Netflix on September 8, 2021, and concluded on September 29, 2021. The season was announced in March 2020 when Netflix renewed The Circle for a second and third season. Michelle Buteau returned as host.
Like the previous seasons, players compete against each other to become the most popular, but do not actually meet their competitors. Instead, they communicate through a specially designed app and are able to portray themselves in any way they choose. In August 2021 ahead of the season premiere, the series was renewed for a fourth and fifth season.
On September 29, 2021, the season was won by James Andre Jefferson Jr., who had played the game as himself, and won the US$100,000 prize that came along with it. Matthew Pappadia as "Ashley" was the runner-up. Keisha "Kai" Ghost won the Fan Favorite award and US$10,000.

--- [The Circle US | The Circle Wiki | Fandom] ---
"Anyone can be anyone..." The Circle is an American reality television show, based off of the British version produced by Netflix. The show's first season initially started airing January 1, 2020 and concluded on January 15, 2020. The show revolves around the social media aspect with the concept and constant saying of "Anyone can be anyone (in The Circle)". The show has been heavily compared with Big Brother with a Catfish and Blackmirror twist. The first season was won by Joey Sasso, winning a total of $100,000, while the viewers champions was won by Sammie Cimarelli, winning $10,000. The second season was won by Deleesa St. Agathe, winning a total of $100,000, while the viewers champions was won by Chloe Veitch, winning $10,000. The third season was won by James Andre Jefferson Jr., winning a total of $150,000, while the viewers champions was won by Kai Ghost, winning $10,000. The fourth season was won by Frank Grimsley, winning a total of $100,000, while the viewers champions was won by Josh Brubaker, winning $10,000. The fifth season was won by Sam Carmona, winning a total of $100,000. Format[] In the Circle, the "Players" each move into a block of flats, each player getting their own private apartment. The players are never allowed to see each other face to face, but instead communicate through a specialized social media network; The Circle. This would mean they'd be able to portray themselves in whatever way they wanted to be. As the series progresses, players will get the chance to rate each other. During these ratings, the 2 players who have the highest rating, will be made the influencers and will get to make the joint and deciding decision as to who gets blocked (eliminated) from The Circle. The Influencers will be immune to said blocking, putting the rest of the players at risk. When blocked, a player is therefore eliminated from The Circle, and they are also given a chance to see one of the remaining players face-to-face. At the finale, the contestants would be tasked with rating each other one more time, and the highest rated player will be crowned the winner, winning the cash prize of $100,000 (except for Season 4, when during the Spice Girls event, the prize increased to $150,000). The viewing public will also be given the chance to choose their "Viewers Champion", who will take home $10,000.
