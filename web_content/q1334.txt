Kokernag (Urdu pronunciation: [koːkərnɑːɡ] ; Kashmiri pronunciation: [kɔkarnaːɡ]) is a sub-district, town and a notified area committee in the Breng Valley (also known as the Golden Crown of Kashmir) of Anantnag district in the Indian union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The place is known for its botanical gardens, pristine freshwater springs, and rainbow trout farm. It is 25.3 km from Anantnag District via NH244. It is one of the most popular weekend getaways from the state capital of Srinagar.

Kashmir is often described as one of the most beautiful regions in the world, known for its snow-covered mountains, green fields, rivers, and rich traditions. Among its many scenic landscapes, one valley stands out for its natural charm, fertile land, and historical importance. This valley has played a major role in shaping the culture, economy, and identity of the region for centuries. The Golden Crown of Kashmir The place known as the Golden Crown of Kashmir is Kokernag, located in the Anantnag district of Jammu and Kashmir. It lies at a height of about 2,000 metres above sea level and is famous for its crystal-clear freshwater springs, scenic gardens, and peaceful surroundings. Kokernag is not just beautiful to look at, but also very important for nature, science, and local life. Meaning of the Name Kokernag The name Kokernag comes from two local words: - “Koker”, meaning fowl or cock - “Nag”, meaning spring or water source The main spring here divides into several small streams that look like the claws of a bird when seen from above. This unique shape is one of the reasons the place is linked with the idea of a crown. Why is Kokernag called the Golden Crown of Kashmir? Kokernag has earned this royal title because of its natural richness, historical value, and scientific importance. The word “golden” does not only describe its beauty but also its value to the region. Historical Importance of Kokernag In ancient times, Kokernag was highly respected by Mughal rulers. They preferred the water from this spring because of its purity and pleasant taste. The surrounding Breng Valley was one of the most prosperous areas of Kashmir, known for farming and silk production. Due to its wealth and productivity, the region came to be associated with gold and royalty. Largest Freshwater Spring in Kashmir Kokernag is home to the largest freshwater spring in Kashmir. The spring flows continuously throughout the year and provides clean water to nearby areas. Its steady flow plays a major role in local agriculture and daily life, making it extremely valuable. Scientific Importance of Kokernag Spring The spring works through a natural siphon system, where water rises through limestone rocks under pressure. This keeps the water supply strong and stable in every season. The water contains useful minerals and is known for its high purity and freshness. Asia’s Largest Trout Hatchery One of the most unique features of Kokernag is that it hosts one of the largest trout fish hatcheries in Asia. The cold, oxygen-rich water creates perfect conditions for trout breeding. This fishery supports the local economy and adds to the “golden” status of the valley. Rich Natural Environment and Biodiversity Kokernag is surrounded by pine and cedar forests, which release natural compounds into the air that help reduce stress and improve health. The valley supports rare plants, local fish species, and migratory birds, making it an important biodiversity zone in Kashmir. Golden Beauty During Autumn In autumn, the famous Chinar trees and garden plants around Kokernag turn bright shades of golden yellow and orange. During this season, the entire valley looks like a shining crown, perfectly matching its royal title. The Saffron Town of Kashmir Another important place often mentioned with Kashmir’s natural wealth is Pampore, known as the Saffron Town of Kashmir. It is located about 15 kilometres from Srinagar in the Pulwama district, along the banks of the Jhelum River. Why Pampore is Famous Worldwide? Pampore is one of the very few places in the world where high-quality saffron is grown. Because of its superior saffron production, it is also called the Saffron Capital of India. The town is known for its Karewa plateaus, which provide perfect soil and climate for saffron farming. Importance of Pampore in Kashmir’s Economy Saffron from Pampore is exported across India and abroad. It plays a major role in supporting local farmers and preserving traditional farming methods. The deep red saffron fields during blooming season attract tourists from all over the world.

Vinod Kumar Anantnag is the southernmost district of Kashmir Valley. In this district there is a beautiful valley known as Breng Valley. It is is located on Anantnag – Kishtwar Road via Daksum. Kokernag is the capital of Breng valley. It is a sub-district town and a notified area committee . There is now a Tehsil Headquarter at Kokernag. The place is known for its flower gardens, pristine fresh water springs and trout farms. It is about 25 kms from Anantnag via NH244 (Anantnag – Kishtwar Road). It is one of the most popular weekend getaways from the state capital Srinagar. In Nilmatpuraan and Rajtarangini the area of Breng Valley is referred to as Bring Khshetra. Known as ‘ The Golden Crown of Kashmir ‘, The valley is named after the River Bringi ( now turned into a stream ) which is a tributary of famous Vitasta ( River Jhelum ). The valley spans over 40 kms on either side of Bringi. Kokernag is the center of the valley where famous Kokernag Botanical Gardens are located. The valley has been praised by the Kashmiri saint Nund Rishi as ” Breng chhu sonsund preng “, i.e. Breng Valley is the Golden Crown (of Kashmir). The lush green Breng valley is blessed with many places of religious and historical importance. There is a place in Breng valley (near Wailoo) on Kokernag – Daksum road named as Pindubal which according to religious experts has the mahima (status) equivalent to Gaya of Bihar. During earlier times, there used to be a heavy snowfall in Kashmir during winter months. Breng valley also comes under heavy snowfall areas. The area used to remain cut off from the rest of the world during winters. It is said that Hindus of Breng area used to do pend dhaan (offerings to the deceased people) at Pindubal particularly during winters. The place was called Pendobal in earlier times. With the passage of time the name changed from Pendobal to Pindubal. There is a holy sangam (confluence) of two streams at Pindubal. One stream comes from Daksum side known as Dodhkol. The primary source of Dodhkol is the glaciers in Sinthan area. Another stream comes from Gadole side known as Gadole Nallah. The source of Gadole Nallah is Mawar Nag. Mawar Nag is an alpine lake situated above Fattan. It is about 50 kms from Kokernag at an altitude of 12500 feet. The valley comprises of attractive meadows and very big and beautiful spring surrounded by mountains. Mawar Nag has crystal clear water. Some water of Mawar Nag flows down via Gadole. It is called Gadole Nallah and is one of the tributaries of Bringi River. Both the streams i.e. Dodhkol and Gadole Nallah meet at Pindubal and thus a sangam (confluence) is created. The river so formed is called Bringi River. Thus Bringi flows from Pindubal onwards. At later stages many tributaries also add waters to the Bringi River. Bringi flows down through many villages including Irkumoo. Bringi is an important tributary of holy Vitasta (River Jhelum). It is believed that the seat of local Bairva Jumbkeshwar (Jeshtu) is at Pindubal. It is said that there was once a cave at Pindubal in which there was a Shivling. It is also said that the famous tantrik saint of Irkumoo, Swami Alakhram Ji, used to do meditation at Pindubal. It is a very sacred place as per Hindu mythology. There is a legend that once the entire Breng area was dry and arid. There was not any proper water source. People had to depend upon snowfalls and rains only. People approached Rishi Kashyap for help. On the instructions of Rishi Kashyap a sage called as Bring Rishi camped at Mawar Nag. Due to his saadhna (strong meditation and worshiping of Lord Shiva) he succeeded in creating a water source which flows through Gadole and reaches Pindubal. Since some areas of Kishtwar side were also dry and arid, therefore Bring Rishi blessed that area too. With the result some portion of water from Mawar Naag also flows towards Kishtwar and finally mixes with the mighty Chandrabaagha (River Chenab) at Bandaarkoot after passing through Chingaam, Chhatroo and Mughal Maidaan. According to Pt. Janki Nath of Irkumoo, people used to do pend dhaan (offerings to the deceased people) at Pindubal. The importance of Pindubal has been revealed to him and to other people of his village by the old people of Irkumoo Biradari such as Pt. Shiv Ji Kak (his father), Pt. Maheshwer Nath and Pt. Tarachand. The same has been endorsed by the late Purohit (Guru Ji) Pt. Kanth Kak of Sagam. There is a proper mention of Bring Rishi, Bringi and Bring (Breng) area in Nilmatpuran as well as Rajtarangini. The holy place of Pindubal needs to be protected and developed for religious tourism.
