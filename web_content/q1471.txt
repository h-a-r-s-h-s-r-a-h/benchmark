Boavita is a town and municipality in the Colombian Department of Boyacá, part of the subregion of the Northern Boyacá Province. The urban centre of Boavita is situated in the Eastern Ranges of the Colombian Andes at an altitude of 2,114 metres (6,936 ft) and a distance of 184 kilometres (114 mi) from the department capital Tunja. The municipality borders Capitanejo, Santander, and the Nevado River in the north, San Mateo and La Uvita in the east, Tipacoque and Soatá in the west and Susacón in the south.

Contents What Does the Name Boavita Mean? The name "Boavita" comes from the ancient Chibcha language. It doesn't mean "good life" as some might think. Instead, its true meaning is "Point on the hill worshipping the Sun" or "Gate of the Sun." This shows how important the sun was to the people who lived here long ago. A Glimpse into Boavita's Past Long before the Spanish arrived, the area where Boavita is today was home to a group of people called the "Guavitas." They were part of the Laches and Muisca tribes. These tribes had their own leaders, called caciques. The local cacique of the Guavitas was loyal to a powerful leader known as the Tundama from Duitama. Modern Boavita was officially founded on February 9, 1613. It was established by a person named Sr. Hugarte. This date marks the beginning of the town as we know it today. What Keeps Boavita's Economy Going? The people of Boavita mainly earn their living through a few key activities. These include agriculture (farming), livestock farming (raising animals), and mining. - Farming: Farmers in Boavita grow many different crops. Some of the most important ones are dates, sugarcane, yuca (also known as cassava), coffee, and cotton. They also grow common foods like potatoes, maize (corn), chick peas, beans, peas, wheat, and arracacha (a root vegetable). - Mining: The main mining activity in Boavita is the extraction of carbon (coal). This resource is important for energy and other industries. Boavita's Climate: What's the Weather Like? Boavita has a special type of weather called a subtropical highland climate. This means that the temperatures stay moderate and pleasant throughout the entire year.
