[Junya (song) - Wikipedia]
" Junya " is a song by American rapper Kanye West from his tenth studio album, Donda (2021). The song features vocals from rapper Playboi Carti. Lyrically, the rappers pay tribute to Junya Watanabe and reference their watches made by him. The song's second part, "Junya pt 2", was also included on the album and features vocals from Ty Dolla Sign.

| Junya | Junya pt 2 | Junya (Remix) | "Junya" is the ninth track from Kanye West's tenth studio album, Donda (2021), and the sixteenth track from the deluxe version of Donda.[1][2] The song features a guest verse from Playboi Carti. An alternate version of the track, titled "Junya pt 2", was released as the twenty-sixth track from Donda and the thirtieth track from the deluxe version of Donda.[1][2] This song features an extended verse from Carti along with new vocals from Ty Dolla $ign. The song is named after Japanese fashion designer Junya Watanabe, who is also mentioned in the song's chorus.[3] Production[] The track is played 4/4 times at 148 BPM, and it is in the key of G Major. Background[] The song, intiially solely produced by Digital Nas was among the 150 beats created by him that were sent to Kanye to freestyle on. The first freestyle which had been titled "Godspeed" had been recorded for on May 26 of 2021 during the Green Tiles sessions.[4] The following day, the freestyle was cut down and retitled to "Jr Watanabe", though production on the song was shortly paused.[5] The following month on the third of June Ty Dolla $ign met up with Kanye to record multiple references for songs from the earlier freestyle sessions for this album that he had continued to work on such as Let Go and All These Friends Of Mine, this song being one of them.[6] His verse on the song would later be taken and cleaned up in a rerecording for it for the remix of the song. 3 days later, the song's production would be heavily tweaked by the producer duo Ojivolta which had included the new addition of a backing vocal sample as well as different drums before it was ultimately redone completely.[7] Writer Malik Yusef would record a reference on the 8th and rapper Tee Grizzley would record one on the 18th.[8] The last known version of the song before it's production paused dates to the 23rd where Kanye's cutdown freestyle was added to the new Ojivolta beat.[9] A photo of Carti recording for Donda surfaced in July 2021. In the background, an early track list for the album can be seen with "Junya" as the fourteenth track.[10] The song was likely picked back up in late-July, where UK rapper Lancey Foux had recorded a reference for it and rapper Playboi Carti recorded a verse for it.[11] This verse would also document the first use of Carti's deep voice in some capacity in which he would use on later songs "2024" and "BACKDOOR". Carti would end up rerecording his verse for the final version which had been previewed at the first Donda listening party on July 22nd.[12] Past that point, the song underwent several production changes such as an added church organ and piano, although most of them were left unused.[13] On August 4, Pusha T, CyHi and Consequence recorded references for the song. Ty Dolla $ign would also rerecord his verse for the song intended as a feature which was added to the base song for a short period of time, though it had ultimately been cut from the final version and replaced with a new third Kanye verse.[14] The final version had been previewed at the last listening party on August 26, featuring a slightly different Kanye final verse compared to earlier versions.[15] The song, along with the Ty Dolla $ign version were released three days later as Junya pt. 1 and pt. 2 as the ninth and sixteenth track of Donda. A second remix to Junya was also said to have been planned and had featured an entirely different beat produced by Dr. Dre.[16] The song was stated to have been intended to release on the Donda stem player as a bonus, though never did. The full legitimacy of this is unclear, as Dr. Dre was not known to have been working with Kanye around the time periods in which this beat could have been materialized.
