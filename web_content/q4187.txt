--- [Rafael | Money Heist Wiki - Fandom] ---
Rafael de Fonollosa, is a character in the Netflix series Money Heist, portrayed by actor Patrick Criado. Biography[] Rafael, the prodigal son of Berlin, is an Electronics Engineers from MIT, Massachusetts with a post-degree in cybersecurity and has only one thing clear that he does not want to be a thief like his father. Relationships[] He is known to be a friend of The Professor and the son of Berlin. Tatiana[] During Tatiana and Berlin's marriage, Tatiana falls in love with Berlin's son, Rafael and divorces Berlin. As Berlin previously told Tatiana the plans of the heist and later denied her his inheritance, Tatiana and Rafael worked together to steal all the extracted gold (90 tons) from the robbers by having a group of people pretending to be police officers. The gold was buried in the ground and covered by a house and fake lawn. However, their plan does not work out as Alicia Sierra, who was working with The Professor, uncovers the hidden location and takes the gold back. --- [What the Professor wrote to Rafael : r/LaCasaDePapel] ---
What the Professor wrote to Rafael Let's try to discuss what the Professor wrote to his nephew Rafael and what really happened at the end of the show. I've read and watched some explanation and I'm not really satisfied with their explanation. Rafael wasn't a part of the plan We know that Rafael knew most of the plan and he took his father's (Berlin) advice to heart: he'd steal what he wants. That's why he "stole" his own father's wife. => It makes sense that he could just sit and watch until he could steal the gold. That's why I also think the Professor would never include Rafael in his plan because he'd never trust anyone who betrayed his own gang/family. That's mentioned a few times in the show. Some people think Rafael was part of the plan - I don't think so. Proofs - During the confrontation with Alicia in P5:E10, Tatiana admitted that she didn't know that the gold would be used as "a bargaining chip". She only knew the plan up to the part to get the gold out (Berlin & Palermo's plan) which neither she or Rafael was participating in. - The Professor's reaction when he found out the "real" gold had been stolen proves that he was surprised. He actually didn't know who stole it at first until Alicia helped him think about it. So I'm sure that Rafael was NEVER a part of the plan like some articles and people believe. Rafael got the fake gold The Professor needed both the real gold and the fake gold. He wanted the gold but also wanted to guarantee that his gang would get out. Palermo knew that and asked him in a scene. The Professor knew it's just an illusion. The same illusion of the paper that made the money notes that his father paid his life for. However, as a thief (as he later admitted), he wanted to have the gold. He was shocked because without he fake gold - his out-of-jail card, his gang, including his love (Lisbon) would never be free. That's why he asked Alicia - the one who was most competent to search for the stolen fake gold. He knew he had to play his role at the bank for his final act so he went there. All the close up shots of the old ingots: when Benjamin's gang was making the real ingots, when Rafael's gang was burying the fake ingots, when Tamayo showed the Professor the fake ingot, etc. were all intentional. Proofs - P5:E7 Wishful Thinking, minute 40:19 The real gold ingots made by Benjamín (Manila’s father) and his gang do not have the Dali mark. It’s a close up shot and it’s clear that they don’t. That close up shot was intentional. There’s no way they “forgot” to put the seal on in a shot like that. - Minute 49:11: You can see the real ingots again. No mark. Note that nobody put a mark on AFTER the gold ingots have been cooled down. Gold ingots are produced by pouring the molten metal into molds. [1] - P5:E8 The Elegance Theory, minute 8:40, the Professor was filming the gold being loaded to the truck. That footage was used to broadcast later to convince people that the gold with the Deli mark is the real gold that would be stored in the bank of Spain. - P5:E10 A Family Tradition, minute 55:20, The ingot Tamayo showed the Professor had big scratches. Again, another intentional close up shot. Those scratches look like a damage caused by a tooth of an excavator's bucket. That's an indication that the ingot was damaged because it was dug up using an excavator. - Minute 1:03:41: another close up shot of the ingots in the bank. The Deli marks are created by using molds (not print) so it's obvious that the ingots that were shown when Benjamin's gang was making them, aren't the same as the ones Rafael stole and later what Tamayo had. Conclusion The gold had been transported out before Rafael and his gang arrived. Showing the movement of Suarez’s troop and Rafael’s gang at the same time created an excellent drama. It was a genius touch by the director. The Professor looked surprised because he didn’t expect the police to find out or to come that early. The Professor needed both the real and the fake gold in order to make his plan work. The fake gold is used to let Tamayo "win" in the public's eye so he'd let the gang out. The real gold is his real hostage in this case so the government would have to honour the deal and stop hunting them down. That's why it's a win-win or lose-lose situation and forced Tamayo to accept the deal. There are several close up shots of the ingots to emphasise what the show creators wanted to show the viewers. It's like they push the viewers' faces to those ingots and say: "pay attention, look at them". Even if the gold Rafael stole was real And for some reasons they "forgot" to put the Deli mark on the ingots in some of the shots, Rafael was never a part of the plan (explained above). So what would make Rafael hand over the gold to Alicia? It's not because Rafael was responsible for hiding it and he got the note saying that Alicia is team Professor now. My thoughts: - Rafael realised the got the fake gold which he could easily check. It's the same trick his father did in The Netherlands. There's no reason to fight against Alicia's group. - Giving Rafael a share of the gold was always a part of the plan. It's not just because the Professor wanted him to honour his dead father but also Berlin planned that. It's "a family matter" (as Rafael said it and probably that's part of the message) and since Rafael proved that he's also a thief, he's entitled to carry on the family's "tradition" even though he broke Berlin's heart and stole from his own uncle. - Even if you're not convinced that Rafael got the fake gold: Rafael knew most of the plan but Berlin and Palermo never thought about it throughly. They did not have a solid plan to deal with that amount of gold once they got it out of the bank. That was mentioned a few times during the show by the Professor, including Palermo's conversation with the Professor when he asked about the last part of the plan in P5:E10. Rafael isn't at Berlin and the Professor's level so obviously he wouldn't know what to do either. That's why he chose to bury it. The Professor, however, knew how. So it makes sense for him to accept the cut from the Professor. Alicia had to lift the fabricated house to take the fake gold back to continue the plan. She's smart so she might as well let Benjamin transport the real gold in the house to Portugal. I hope this makes sense. Feel free to criticise my thoughts. Would love to have some conversations here. UPDATE More detailed explanation [1] It's true that there's a few ways to make ingots. However, there's no scene or any detail suggests that they marked the ingots after making them. In fact, there are scenes as mentioned to prove the opposite. First, why wouldn't they just use a mold because they already knew how the fake gold would look (with Deli mark)? Second, in a heist, time is money (quote directly from the show), using a mold is way faster than wasting time printing/carving the Deli mark on every single ingot after. It's not like they were in a factory. They were in a water tank with limited equipments. The faster they make the ingots the better. Q&A Marseille must have the fake gold in the trucks that lured the authorities to the ocean. P5:E10 - minute 27:01 and 39:09 - it shows the "cargo" in those breadcrumbs trucks are just random objects (e.g. ocean buoys) in boxes covered with tarps. Throughout the whole episode, there's no scene shows any gold in any of the trucks. Also those trucks carried "fake loot". So there's no evidence to conclude that the fake loot on those boats or the containers dumped in the ocean contained any fake gold. The fake gold must be damaged by the forklifts when they transferred them to the bank. That's why Tamayo found the scratched ingot. No, the ingots were on wood pallets all the time. That's how forklifts can lift them up. Also the people at the bank would be careful/they know how to do the job. The damage was on the side of the ingot. It's not a forklift's scratch. The ingots shown when they're making them are up side down. Far fetch. The close up shot shows all of them without the Dali mark, not just a few. They need to keep the side with the seal/mark on to make sure they're identical. That's a normal practice. There's no reason to put them all up side down. There's not enough time to dig up the gold, hose the dirt and bring it to the bank. First, it really doesn't take long to hose some dirt off smooth metal like ingots, even dry them with industrial blowers. The gang already had industrial tools. Second, we know the gang was held in the bank for at least a few days (proof: P5:E10, minute 32:10 at the land registry when the employee arrived at 6:50AM). So there's no evidence to make the assumption that the gold was delivered to the bank right away when Tamayo accepted the deal. It could take a few hours and that's enough to dig up the gold, clean it and deliver it. Third, the Los Migueles stormwater tank (shown at P5:E8 - 45:05) is located north of Madrid. Even if you drive from Bilbao (furthest north) it'd take about 4 hours. So there's enough time. The fake gold was on stand by in a different location. Then the police would smell it. Also, it's the "exquisite exchange" that Berlin did and the Professor was impressed so he did the same. On top of that, Rafael said "He would've wanted this" when he talked to Tatiana. That an indication Rafael realised it's his father's "exquisite exchange" and agreed to hand over the fake gold so the Professor can finish the swap as Berlin would've wanted. Netflix's explanation published on 11 Dec 2011 So after I posted this explanation, on 11 Dec 2011, Netflix UK released the ending explained video. It confirmed the fact that Rafael wasn't a part of the plan and he got the real gold. Thank you for the discussion. I hope you had a good time.

--- [What Did The Professor's Note to Rafael Say in 'Money Heist' Season 5 ...] ---
Money Heist has enthralled fans for five seasons, with the final five episodes being released by Netflix on Friday, December 3. The Spanish crime drama certainly ended on a high, and while the show will not be back (at least in its current form) there were some surprising moments that left fans with questions they needed answers to. One such plotline is related to Berlin's son Rafael (Patrick Criado) and his role in the gang's heist in the Bank of Spain. **Warning: This article contains spoilers for Money Heist Season 5 Part 2** What did The Professor's note to Rafael say? For the majority of Season 5, Rafael is only seen in the flashbacks about his father Berlin (Pedro Alonso), in which the pair embark on their own heists. This culminates in Rafael running off with Berlin's fifth wife Tatiana (Diana Gómez) after they fell in love with each other. That seemed to be the end of the character's journey, until the gang's stolen gold was stolen from them in turn... by Rafael and Tatiana. Rafael and Tatiana had it all planned out, they knew exactly where to find the gold and dressed as police to trick The Professor (Álvaro Morte) and his crew into surrendering, and locked them in a fake police van. The duo then hid the gold by burying it in a big hole and covering it with a house and decorating the land around it to make it appear as if it had been there for years, instead of mere minutes. On The Professor's request, Alicia Sierra (Najwa Nimri) goes out looking for the gold and eventually discovers the house that Rafael and Tatiana had buried it under, and the trio, and their crew, end up in a tense stand-off. This stand-off is called off when Alicia gives Rafael a note from The Professor, which convinces him that it is best to hand over the gold and work with the gang of thieves, rather than against them. When Tatiana questions his decision, Rafael tells her it is a "family matter" and leaves things at that. Prior to the scene where he is given the note, the show shares one last flashback with Rafael, Berlin and The Professor, in which the latter explains to his nephew what happened to his grandfather. Explaining how he witnessed his father being gunned down outside a bank following a botched robbery, The Professor tells Rafael: "When you ask me why I choose to plan the heists, the answer is simple. To talk to him." The note The Professor wrote for his nephew is never shown to viewers, but it's likely that in it he reminds Rafael of that story, and links it to his own father Berlin who died in Season 2 following the gang's heist in the Royal Mint of Spain.
