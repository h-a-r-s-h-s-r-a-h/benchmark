This area is part of the beautiful Tenza Valley. Contents What's in a Name? La Capilla was first known as La Capilla de Tenza. This name came from a special event. People believed that Our Lady of Candle appeared near the village. Because of this, a small church, called a chapel (capilla in Spanish), was built. That's how the town got its name, La Capilla. A Look Back in Time The very first people to live in the area of La Capilla were the Muisca. They were a group of people who lived in a loose confederation. The Muisca in this specific area were connected to the leaders (called caciques) of nearby towns like Garagoa, Sutatenza, and Somondoco. In 1537, a Spanish explorer named Gonzalo Jiménez de Quesada traveled through the Tenza Valley. He and his soldiers were on their way to the lands of the zaque (another Muisca leader) in Hunza. Modern La Capilla was officially founded on November 13, 1793. The person who founded it was Juan de la Cruz Aguirre. Where is La Capilla? La Capilla is surrounded by other towns and areas. - To its north are the towns of Úmbita and Pachavita. - To its south are Tenza and the Cundinamarca Department. - To its west is the Cundinamarca Department. - To its east are Pachavita and Tenza. How People Make a Living The main way people in La Capilla earn money is through farming. They grow different crops like cucumbers, tomatoes, and beans. Quick Facts About La Capilla - Market Day: Mondays are when people gather for the market. - Distance from Tunja: La Capilla is about 92 kilometres (57 mi) away from Tunja, a larger city. - Average Temperature: The weather is usually mild, with an average temperature of about 18 °C (64 °F). - Local Name: People from La Capilla are called Capillense. Colombia Travel Bucket List: La Capilla, Boyacá Welcome to my travel blog! In this special edition, I want to share with you one of the most charming towns in Boyacá: La Capilla. This small municipality is surrounded by green mountains, kind people, and traditions that make it unique. It is the perfect place to relax, discover local culture, and enjoy beautiful landscapes. A Little of Its History Its name comes from the La Capilla doctrinal temple. Named a parish in 1793, it was founded by Spanish origin.The name "LA CAPILLA" comes from the apparition of the Virgin of La Candelaria (near the La Gusba stream), and a chapel (San Miguel Chapel) was built in her honor, hence the current name of the municipality. This chapel dates back to the colonial era and depended on the Tenza parish doctrinal chapel. There was a small chapel on the site where masses were celebrated at certain times of the year, hence the name "La Capilla de Tenza." This chapel is still preserved under the name "La Capilla San Miguel." The main industry was pottery, supplying pottery and clay tiles to all the towns in the region. Fique sacks, hats, and flower baskets were also made. Its establishment as a municipality took place on December 23, 1793, during the government of Viceroy José de Ezpeleta y Galdeano, whose original name was La Capilla de Tenza. Its first mayor was Juan Antonio Díaz. On February 6, 1821, the town's inhabitants paid a grand tribute to the Liberator Simón Bolívar, who spent the night in the town.
