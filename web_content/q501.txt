--- [Carole & Tuesday - Wikipedia] ---
Carole & Tuesday (Japanese: キャロル&チューズデイ, Hepburn: Kyaroru & Chūzudei) is an original anime television series directed by Shinichirō Watanabe. It was produced by Bones in commemoration of the studio's 20th anniversary and the 10th anniversary of record label FlyingDog. It aired from April 11 to October 3, 2019, on Fuji TV's +Ultra programming block. A manga adaptation by Morito Yamataka began serialization in Young Ace in May 2019 and ended in July 2020. The manga is licensed in English by Yen Press. An English dub premiered worldwide on Netflix on August 30, 2019.

--- [Mocky - Carole & Tuesday Original Soundtrack Lyrics and ...] ---
In 2019, Japanese director Shinichirō Watanabe reached out to Canadian musician Mocky in order to produce the soundtrack for the anime Carole & Tuesday. In an interview with Ele-King, Watanabe commented that when he saw Mocky on a live show in Japan, every song felt like a video and it’d be really good for a soundtrack. At that time, when I was planning Carole & Tuesday, I was thinking about what made me happy about music. What is this feeling of euphoria? Carole & Tuesday is a series about two different girls with the same love for music. Decades from now, most culture is made by AI, including music, and humans are just happy consumers, meaning no one would understand Carole and Tuesday’s passion. This is what Mocky had to say regarding the anime: I think everyone should be interested in AI. That era is already here. So is music. A computer or a robot. Unlike the 80’s and 90’s, now is a time when drummers are not needed, autotune sings, and music is being controlled by robots. I’m fighting it now. To find a balance in that. So the theme of “Carole & Tuesday” was very familiar to me. For example, people make mistakes at concerts because they are not perfect. But I think that is what makes art. The movie is the same, and it’s not interesting if you know in advance what will happen and what you can do perfectly. I think the best techno music is when something goes wrong, and I want to capture the interesting things that are born at that time. When asked if Watanabe had listened to Mocky’s soundtrack, he answered: It’s good to have a Mocky song as well as a soundtrack. Some musicians suddenly go away when they make soundtracks (laughs). In fact, there is a secret to making a good soundtrack: It’s about asking someone who has never done a soundtrack. There is always something like beginner’s luck or magic that is unique to the first person. I think it’s similar to the magic that is unique to an artist’s first album, but I think it’s only born when you’re groping for something new.


--- [Review: Netflix anime is clear and melodic - The Ithacan] ---
“Carole & Tuesday” is an anime Netflix series that depicts the music industry in the futuristic world of Alba City, Mars. Carole Stanley (voiced by Miyuri Shimabukuro and vocals sung by Nai Br.XX) is a keyboard player with a fiery personality. Tuesday Simmons (voiced by Kana Ichinose and vocals sung by Celeina Ann) is a guitar player who ran away from home. After Tuesday meets Carole at Carole’s street performance, the girls decide to pursue music together. The show portrays the title characters as determined performers while revealing the uncertainty that comes with living in a world influenced by artificial intelligence. Although the girls are opposites, they connect over their love of music and feelings of loneliness. Because Carole grew up in an orphanage, she is tough and independent but isolated. Tuesday grew up in a luxurious mansion, but her mother is distant. The girls are interesting to watch together because of how different their lives are. The first song Carole and Tuesday write together is called “The Loneliest Girl.” The song is soulful and truthful, and the girls sing about how they felt alone in the world until they met each other. Though Tuesday’s speaking voice is slightly annoying, the vocal actors’ singing voices together are beautiful and capture the emotion of the lyrics well. The song’s lyrics about overcoming their loneliness together remain a central theme. Every episode is inspired by a well-known song. The second episode is inspired by “Born to Run” by Bruce Springsteen. In this episode, the title characters find themselves running from the police once again after sneaking into a concert hall to perform their song on a grand piano. The subtle tie between the plot and the song title is a creative way of weaving well-known music with the girls’ up-and-coming music. The animation portrays Alba City as a vibrant metropolis. Tsunenori Saito’s animation for “Carole & Tuesday” is detailed and crisp. For example, the viewer can clearly see the sadness or excitement in the characters’ faces. The series also conveys how artificial intelligence can give performers in this futuristic era an advantage. For example, Carole and Tuesday’s competitor Angela Carpenter’s (voiced by Sumire Uesaka and vocals sung by Alisa) songs are written through monitoring her heartbeat, words and tone of voice. Though Angela’s subconscious is reflected in the lyrics at times, it doesn’t compare to real human emotions. Carole and Tuesday prove they do not need fancy, artificially created songs to be successful. The role of artificial intelligence in the series makes the story more interesting because there are new obstacles in the music industry for the title characters to overcome. “Carole & Tuesday” is a series of an unlikely friendship in an innovative world. Their songs are honest, and their originality is monumental in the fictional music industry. The role of artificial intelligence uniquely shapes the narrative of the series in the visually pleasing anime.
