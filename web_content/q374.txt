George Henry Johnson (18 August 1926 – 26 December 2021) was a New Zealand artist who made his name in Australia.


--- [George Henry Johnson. 1926-2021 New Zealand, Australia - Prices of Art ...] ---
Born in Nelson, New Zealand in 1926, George Johnson studied art under the emigre artist Theo Schoon, who confirmed an early commitment to modernist art, especially Geometric Abstraction. He graduated from Wellington Technical College in 1947. Johnson relocated to Melbourne, Australia, in 1951 where he was soon drawn into contemporary art circles, mixing with Leonard French, Roger Kemp, Inge King, Julius Kane, Peter Graham, Clement Meadmore and others. He held his first solo exhibition there at the age of 30 in 1956, a selection of boldly geometric abstractions. He became an art teacher at Footscray Technical School and also exhibited regularly with the Charles Nodrum Gallery for over 30 years. He has won a number of awards and his work is represented in the state galleries in Melbourne and Sydney and in regional galleries. George Johnson passed away the 26th of December, 2021 at the age of 95. The earliest auction listing we have for George Johnson is in 1978 and in total 24 works by the artist have been offered for sale, of which 19 (79%) were sold. The highest price recorded for the artist is $10,810 for Clipped Blue Triangle Study I sold by International Art Centre in October 2023. No works have been offered for sale this year, and the last sale we have recorded for the artist was in 2023.
