--- [The most popular baby names of 2025 - BabyCenter] ---
The most popular baby names of 2025 Key Takeaways - Olivia and Noah held onto their spots as the No. 1 names for girls and boys. - Eliana and Aurora joined the top 10 girl names for the first time, while Ava and Luna fell out of the top 10. - Luca is back in the top 10 boy names after a one-year hiatus, but Leo is out. Olivia and Noah aren't budging from their No. 1 titles among BabyCenter parents – but there's plenty of other baby name shakeups to take note of this year. Olivia and Amelia are hanging on tight to the No. 1 and 2 spots for girls, but the rest of the top 10 has seen some changes: Sophia (No. 3) and Emma (No. 4) traded places; Isabella (No. 5) and Charlotte (No. 6) also flipped; Ellie (No. 8) rose; and Mia (No. 10) fell. There are two new girl names in the top 10 for the first time ever this year: Eliana (No. 7) and Aurora (No. 9). But that means two other names – Ava (No. 11) and Luna (No. 13) are out. The top 10 boy names saw less movement – in fact, the top 5 are exactly the same as last year: Noah (No. 1), Liam (No. 2), Oliver (No. 3), Elijah (No. 4), and Mateo (No. 5). Meanwhile, Levi (No. 6) and Lucas (No. 7) traded places. Ezra (No. 8) and Asher (No. 9) didn't move. The biggest change in the top 10 boy names was in the No. 10 spot: Luca rejoined the top 10 after falling out in 2024, and Leo (now No. 11) dropped. A new class of girl names is joining the top 100 for the very first time – or rejoining it after years away: - Eloise jumped more than 20 spots to join the top 100 names for the very first time at No. 75. - At No. 84, Sarah is rejoining the top 100 after a year away. - Emersyn climbed a whopping 50 spots to join the top 100 at No. 89. - At No. 92, Sienna is new to the top 100. - Last seen in the top 100 girl names in 2021, Charlie is back at No. 93. - Catalina is in the top 100 for the very first time, at No. 95. - After falling out of the top 100 names in 1943, Josephine is in the top 100 for the first time this century at No. 96. - Juniper is making her first appearance in the top 100, at No. 97. - Oaklynn is debuting in the top 100 at No. 98. - Vivian last appeared in the top 100 in 1934 – and she's back this year at No. 99. - And at No. 100, Kehlani is joining the top 100. But that means just as many girl names fell out of the top 100 this year: Nevaeh (No. 103), Kennedy (No. 105), Anna (No. 107), Ember (No. 108), Jade (No. 109), Savannah (No. 113), Audrey (No. 118), Cora (No. 119), Natalie (No. 120), Skylar (No. 122), and Oakley (No. 142). Meanwhile, just like with the top 10, the top 100 boy names saw less of a shakeup: - Malachi is in the top 100 for the first time at No. 91. - After disappearing from the top 100 in 1970, Arthur skyrocketed 44 spots to reenter the top 100 at No. 95. - Ali is making his first appearance in the top 100 at No. 96. - Clocking in at No. 98, Nathaniel is rejoining the top 100 after falling out in 2016. Hunter (No. 102), Axel (No. 104), Greyson (No. 115), and Max (No. 116) fell out of the top 100 boy names. Read on for the 100 most popular baby names in 2025. It's no surprise that parents are looking to this retro decade for baby name inspiration, as well. But that's not the only naming pattern BabyCenter uncovered as we analyzed the top names of the year so far. BabyCenter gathers baby names from hundreds of thousands of parents who register their babies' names on the BabyCenter appOpens a new window in real time. (We've done this for 20 years!) This means we have a live look at what names are trending and falling, and we're sharing the latest here. (Take a look at the top 100 baby names of 2025 so far.) That's right: Parents love the '90s If you suddenly regret tossing your scrunchie collection and Tamagotchi all those years ago, same! Not only are '90s trends back in style, but the girl names we grew up with are in vogue, too: Some underrated classics from the 1990s are making a comeback. Sabrina – of teenage witch fame – is having a moment, rising 19 spots to No. 323. Nineties chart-toppers Britney and Shania are on the rise again at No. 3,958 (up more than 1,200 spots) and No. 1,915 (up more than 800 spots), respectively. Kelsey and Briana (yes, from your childhood playgroup) are making their way back up the ranks, too, at No. 804 and No. 1,122. The people haven't forgotten their princess, either: Diana has risen 39 spots to No. 293. Loud luxury is in when it comes to naming babies Stealth wealth is out, and showing off your money is in. On TV we have new-money families vacationing at White Lotus resorts. On your FYP there are nepo babies name-dropping their family fameOpens a new window. Baby names that scream 1% are no exception. Parents are looking to luxury brands for name inspiration: Tiffany and Bentley have risen for girls to No. 822 and No. 2,035, and Manolo (Blanik) and Laurent (like Yves Saint Laurent) are up for boys, to No. 1,903 and No. 6,518. Word names that exude wealth and luxury are on the rise, too. Lux – think literally "luxury" – is rising for both boys and girls: It's up 110 spots for boys to No. 1,202, and 401 spots for girls to No. 2,370. Treasure is also on the rise for both boys and girls: It has risen 122 spots for girls to No. 482, and 1,307 spots for boys to No. 2,510. NFL star Patrick Mahomes and his wife Brittany named their daughter Golden earlier this year, and actor Rupert Grint – best known as Ron Weasley in the Harry Potter movies – named his daughter Goldie. The rest of us are taking note: Golden is up 647 spots for boys to No. 1,876, and it's also risen 65 spots for girls to No. 2,330. Bird names fly to the top of the list In the latest evolution of the ever-popular nature names trend, names inspired by birds are taking flight among BabyCenter parents – maybe thanks to Cameron Diaz's extra unique baby name choice, Cardinal, last year. Robin is gaining popularity as a gender-neutral name, rising 274 spots to No. 987 for girls and 97 spots to No. 960 for boys. For girls, Raven has risen 39 spots to No. 241, and Mavis – from an Old French word for the song thrush – is up 17 spots to No. 496. Advertisement | page continues below Dove gets a special mention, too: It's been on an upward trajectory ever since Disney Channel darling Dove Cameron had her breakout roles in the show Liv and Maddie in 2013. This year, she's releasing a new album – and her name has risen 717 spots to No. 1,678. For boys, Callum – which comes from the Latin word for dove – has risen 21 spots to No. 186, and Jay is up 41 spots to No. 232. Baby names nab 2025 Oscar nominations Anora didn't just rake in the Academy Awards at the Oscars this year: It's also a rising star in baby names. The name Anora has jumped up 1,105 spots for girls so far this year, ranking No. 2,291. But what about the other Oscar winners? Both men who got top honors – Adrien Brody won best actor for his role in The Brutalist, and Kieran Culkin won best supporting actor for his role in A Real Pain – have seen their names rise this year. Adrien is up 206 spots to No. 1,132, and Kieran has risen 48 spots to No. 488. And there's more: Wicked star Cynthia Erivo's name is defying gravity, rising 204 spots to No. 1,089. But a new class of word names is coming up now, and it sounds like parents are looking to raise scholarly, honorable young men. For boys, Wisdom is up 868 spots to No. 1,340 – maybe thanks in part to the popularity of fashion influencer Wisdom Kaye. Loyal (up 225 spots to No. 829), Sincere (up 160 spots to No. 534), and Knowledge (up 83 spots to No. 1,054) are also climbing the baby name ranks. Meanwhile, parents are showing their adoration for their baby girls with lofty names like Praise, which has risen 489 spots to No. 1,156; Divine, which has risen 145 spots to No. 1,175; Adore, which at No. 1,365 is up 106 spots; Queen, which is up 75 spots to No. 940; Miracle, which is up 69 spots to No. 181; and Hope, which at No. 217 has risen 40 spots. Boy names ending with -y are falling Names that end with "-y" are on the outs for boys – Finnley especially. After more than a decade as a modern favorite, Finnley fell 177 spots for boys and looks to be on its way out of the top 1,000, at No. 937. Other "-y" names that aren't so popular anymore include Harry, which is down 252 spots to No. 804; Huxley, which fell 223 spots to No. 857; Kingsley, which is at No. 1,021 after falling 168 spots; Corey, which fell 139 spots to No. 839; and Kacey, which is down 87 spots to No. 887.
