Rohini is a series of satellites launched by ISRO. The Rohini series consisted of four satellites, each of which was launched by the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV) [1] and three of which made it successfully to orbit. --- [Rohini RS-1 - SPACE INFORMATION] ---
Rohini is a series of satellites launched by ISRO. The Rohini series consisted of four satellites in which the second satellite was Rohini RS-1. The series were mostly experimental satellites. Rohini RS-1 was launched on July 18,1980 by ISRO by using Satellite Launch Vehicle – 3 (SLV-3) from Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC) Or Sriharikota Range (SHAR), Sriharikota, India. It was an experimental satellite. Rohini RS-1 was a 35 kilogram experimental spin stabilized satellite designed with a power handling capability of 16 W. It was launched into an orbit of 305×919 km with an inclination of 44.7°. This satellite had an orbital life of 20 months. The satellite carried Digital Sun Sensor, Magnetometer and Aluminium Alloy. It’s decay date was May 20,1981. The communication was made using VHF band (Very High Frequency band). The mission life of Rohini RS-1 was 1.2 years. The Objective of this satellite was to provide data on the fourth stage performance and ranging. At that time, Professor Satish Dhawan was the chairman of ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation). The Rohini RS-1 satellite mission launched by Space Launch Vehicle – 3 (SLV-3 E2) was under the leadership of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam and Dr. Satish Dhawan. For the flight of this mission on July 18,1980,Dr. Dhawan decided to allow Doordarshan to telecast the launch,but DD did not accept to telecast this mission. On 19th July, 1980,the SLV-3 Launch Vehicle lifted-off with the Rohini RS-1 satellite making India the seventh nation to conduct an orbital flight. The launch as tracked by the SHAR Centre indicated slight over performance, but the Down Range Satation (DRSN) at Nicobar missed the visibility. Professor Dhawan and all senior scientists were waiting for the news on Orbit insertion and health of the satellite. This Orbit insertion point was not within the SHAR ISTRAC station Range. Therefore, the first communication from Rohini RS-1 was hear by Trivandrum station. Dr. Ved Prakash maintained real-time contact with the station. At about 5°-10° elevation, a background hissing sound of Rohini RS-1 satellite telemetry was heard. The scientists checked all the overall performance and health of the satellite. And then, Professor Dhawan called Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, who announced the news to the nation that India was the seventh nation to conduct an orbital flight which was successful.
