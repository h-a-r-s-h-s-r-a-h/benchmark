--- [Mithila Makhana - Wikipedia] ---
Mithila Makhana (botanical name: Euryale ferox)  is a special variety  of aquatic fox nut cultivated in Mithila region
 of Bihar state in India and in Nepal.
In Mithila, Makhana is also termed as Makhan. It is one of the three prestigious cultural identities of Mithila: Pond, Fish and Makhan (Maithili language: पग-पग पोखरि, माछ, मखान). It is also used in the Kojagara festival celebrated for newly married couples among the Maithil Brahmins and Kaysaths.


--- [Mithila Makhana: India's premium quality gi-tagged superfood from Bihar] ---
In a historic milestone for India’s dry fruits industry Mithila Makhana, the premium fox nut cultivated in the ancient Mithila region of Bihar has been awarded the prestigious Geographical Indication (GI) tag. This recognition officially establishes Mithila Makhana as a unique agricultural treasure of India, strengthening its position in the fast-growing healthy snacks and agri-heritage sector. The GI certification was announced on August 20, 2022, by Union Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal, marking a proud moment for farmers across Bihar. Earlier in April 2022, Mithila Makhana became the 5th product from Bihar to receive GI status, a recognition achieved through persistent efforts led by Darbhanga MP Gopal Jee Thakur, who raised the demand for GI protection in the Parliament of India. A crucial institutional role in securing this GI tag was played by Bihar Agricultural University, Sabour, which provided scientific documentation and technical validation to establish the crop’s uniqueness. Why Mithila Makhana Received GI Tag Recognition The GI tag confirms that authentic Mithila Makhana: - Is grown specifically in Bihar’s Mithila region - Uses traditional cultivation and processing techniques - Possesses superior size, texture, whiteness, and nutritional value - Derives its distinct quality from the region’s unique agro-climatic conditions This certification ensures that only genuine fox nuts produced within the designated geographical area can be marketed as “Mithila Makhana,” protecting it from imitation and misbranding. Mithila Makhana Renowned for its high protein content, low fat, and rich antioxidant profile, Mithila Makhana is rapidly emerging as a global superfood. With GI certification, it now carries both nutritional authority and geographical authenticity, a powerful combination for international branding. The recognition places Mithila Makhana on the global stage, opening doors to premium export markets while ensuring that the legacy of Mithila’s farmers continues to grow. Mithila Makhana is no longer just a regional crop now it is a protected agricultural identity of India. Registration and Implementation The GI tag has been officially registered in the name of the Mithilanchal Makhana Utpadak Sangh, Purnia, marking a milestone in the preservation and promotion of this indigenous product. Adding further momentum to the promotion of Mithila Makhana, the Postal Department of India launched a dedicated courier service for the product within Bihar on 28 January 2021. This registration not only validates the product’s origin and quality but also protects it from unauthorized use. The GI tag is valid for ten years, after which it can be renewed to maintain the product’s exclusive recognition and protection. Impact and Benefits According to the official registration certificate, Mithila Makhana is geographically linked to several districts of Bihar. The recognized areas for its production include Darbhanga, Madhubani, Muzaffarpur, Champaran, Begusarai, Purnea, and Katihar, among others. This designation highlights the unique regional identity of Mithila Makhana, ensuring that only Makhana cultivated in these districts can be marketed under this name, preserving its authenticity and traditional quality. The GI tag brings several advantages: - Market Differentiation: Mithila Makhana is distinguished from other varieties, enhancing its appeal to consumers and providing a competitive edge. - Protection and Authenticity: The GI tag ensures that only authentic Mithila Makhana can be marketed under this name, safeguarding the product’s reputation and quality. - Economic and Legal Security: The tag provides economic and legal protection to farmers, supporting their livelihoods and promoting sustainable cultivation practices. Encouraging Cultivation and Future Plans To promote the cultivation and trade of Makhana, the government established the Makhana Board in Bihar to support farmers and enhance production. Farmers benefit from subsidies and guidance provided by the Makhana Board in Bihar. Following this milestone, the Bihar state government is actively encouraging farmers to expand Makhana cultivation. Currently, many farmers in Darbhanga, Madhubani, Katihar, Purnia, Saharsa, Supaul, Kishanganj, Madhepura, and Araria districts depend on Makhana cultivation for their livelihood. The state government is also planning to introduce Makhana cultivation in West Champaran and Sitamarhi to further enhance production. Government of India Reduces GST on Makhana to 5% Government of India has reduced the Goods and Services Tax (GST) rate on makhana from 12% to 5%. This decision, announced by the Ministry of Finance following the recommendation of the GST Council, aims to benefit farmers, small processors, and traders, especially in the Mithilanchal region of Bihar, which is the main production hub of GI-tagged Mithila Makhana. The tax reduction is expected to enhance market competitiveness, boost domestic consumption, and support rural livelihoods dependent on makhana cultivation and processing. Significance Bihar, as a major Makhana-producing state, supplies approximately 90% of the world’s Makhana. Mithila Makhana enjoys popularity not only within India but also internationally. Despite the labor-intensive and challenging nature of Makhana cultivation, including risks from climate conditions and meticulous processing requirements, the GI tag has strengthened the economic prospects for farmers. Contextual Recognition The awarding of the GI tag to Mithila Makhana is particularly notable as it marks the fifth product from Bihar to receive this prestigious designation. Before Mithila Makhana, Bihar had secured GI tags for: - Bhagalpur’s Jardalu Mango - Katarni Dhaan (rice) - Nawada’s Magahi Paan - Muzaffarpur’s Shahi Litchi These recognitions underscore Bihar’s rich agricultural heritage and its commitment to preserving and promoting its unique products. The GI tag enhances the value of their produce and provides a path toward better market prices and recognition. For generations, farmers in the Mithila region have cultivated makhana using traditional pond-based methods. The GI tag not only preserves this agricultural heritage but also enhances rural livelihoods and strengthens Bihar’s economy. It provides substantial benefits to local farmers, enhances the product’s market presence, and ensures the preservation of Mithila Makhana’s unique characteristics. With this recognition, the future of Mithila Makhana looks promising, offering both economic security and a quality guarantee to the dedicated farmers of Bihar.


--- [Govt awards GI tag to Mithila Makhana for ‘farmer’s profit’] ---
The government has awarded Geographical Indication (GI) tag to Mithila Makhana, a move which is expected to help growers get the maximum price for their premium produce. “Mithila Makhana registered with GI Tag, farmers will get profit and it will be easier to earn. Due to Geographical Indication Tag to Mithila Makhana in the festive season, people outside Bihar will be able to use this auspicious material with reverence,” Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal said in a tweet. GI Tag से पंजीकृत हुआ मिथिला मखाना, किसानों को मिलेगा लाभ और आसान होगा कमाना।त्योहारी सीजन में मिथिला मखाना को Geographical Indication Tag मिलने से बिहार के बाहर भी लोग श्रद्धा भाव से इस शुभ सामग्री का प्रयोग कर पाएंगे। pic.twitter.com/SzSOlsugRB — Piyush Goyal (@PiyushGoyal) August 20, 2022 Once a product gets this tag, any person or company cannot sell a similar item under that name. This tag is valid for a period of 10 years following which it can be renewed. The other benefits of GI registration include legal protection to that item, prevention against unauthorised use by others, and promoting exports. A GI is primarily an agricultural, natural or a manufactured product (handicrafts and industrial goods) originating from a definite geographical territory. According to the GI registry certificate, it is certified that the GI has been registered in the name of Mithilanchal Makhana Utpadak Sangh. Typically, such a name conveys an assurance of quality and distinctiveness, which is essentially attributable to the place of its origin. There is a proper process of registration of GI products which includes filing of application, preliminary scrutiny and examination, show cause notice, publication in the geographical indications journal, opposition to registration, and registration. Any association of persons, producers, organisation or authority established by or under the law can apply. The applicant must represent the interest of the producers. It is a legal right under which the GI holder can prohibit others from using the same name. The famous goods which carry this tag include Basmati rice, Darjeeling Tea, Chanderi Fabric, Mysore Silk, Kullu Shawl, Kangra Tea, Thanjavur Paintings, Allahabad Surkha, Farrukhabad Prints, Lucknow Zardozi and Kashmir Walnut Wood Carving.
