--- [Şahika Ercümen - Wikipedia] ---
Şahika Ercümen (born 16 January 1985) is a Turkish dietitian and world record holder free-diver.
Born on January 16, 1985, in Çanakkale, she was educated at the Gazi Primary School, and received a secondary education at the Milli Piyango Anatolian High School in her hometown. For her higher education, she moved to Ankara, and became a dietitian graduating from the Department of Nutrition and Dietetics at Başkent University's Faculty of Health Sciences.
Her participation in underwater sports began with scuba diving and underwater rugby. For twelve years, she is interested in various branches of underwater sports, and is a member of four national teams since 2001. She participated with the underwater hockey, underwater rugby, underwater orienteering and free diving national teams in the World and European Championships.




--- [National diver Şahika Ercümen breaks world record - Bianet] ---
* Photo: Anadolu Agency (AA) Click to read the article in Turkish Şahika Ercümen, a national diver, broke the world record at variable weight freediving without using breathing on October 26. Ercümen managed to complete a 100-meter dive in 2 minutes and 53 seconds in offshore Kaş in Turkey's Mediterranean province of Antalya. Speaking to Turkey's state-run Anadolu Agency (AA) afterwards, Ercümen has said that she was also without her swim fins and dived into 100 meters with the help of a weight but then reached the sea surface, gripping a rope, which was a hard objective to accomplish. Şahika Ercümen previously "dedicated her success to Turkish soldiers and the 98th anniversary of the foundation of the Republic of Turkey", which is marked as the "Republic Day" today (October 29). The diver stated that she did not train for nearly two years due to the coronavirus pandemic as she previously tested positive for the virus. She said that the disease frightened her so much as she suffers from asthma: "I thought my sports career was over (when she started workouts). It was very hard but I adjusted myself very quickly after several training sessions. And I took my best degrees in a short time." She noted that mental power and belief are the key factors for success, saying that "she can hold her breath for a long time underwater thanks to her iron will" as she recently set a new world record in freediving. "Diving is a sporting branch that requires mental strength and belief. If you do not believe in yourself and your mind is not ready, it is not easy to stay underwater without breathing for so much time," she has added. Ercümen, who is also a diet expert, was named Turkey's life below water advocate by the United Nations Development Programme in 2020 to raise environmental awareness. In 2019, she was part of Turkey's Antarctic science expedition team to travel to the frozen continent. Ercümen dived without an air tank in Antarctica. (EMK/SD)


--- [Turkey's Şahika Ercümen dives to 100 meters for new world record] ---
Turkey's Şahika Ercümen descended to a depth of 100 meters (328 feet) to set a new variable weight freediving world record in southwestern Turkey’s Kaş. Ercümen attempted the 100-meter dive to celebrate the 98th Republic Day of Turkey on Nov. 29. The multiple world-record-holding freediver descended to the record depth in 2 minutes 53 seconds, without any oxygen or diving fins. The record-breaking dive took place at the Inceboğaz district of Kaş. After setting a new world record, Ercümen said: "I want to use this record to draw attention to issues like climate change, global warming and gender equality, as well.” Ahead of Tuesday's record, she dived with women diagnosed with breast cancer to draw attention to breast cancer for the Pi Women's Cancer Society in Antalya. They unfurled a banner reading "Our Strength Is Greater Than Cancer." She bagged three medals and broke five Turkish records in the 2021 Vertical Blue freediving competition at Dean’s Blue Hole in the Bahamas. The United Nations Development Program previously declared Ercümen, 36, Turkey's ambassador for underwater life.

Şahika Ercümen Early life and education Childhood and family background Şahika Ercümen was born on January 16, 1985, in Çanakkale, Turkey, to a bank manager mother and a civil engineer father.[5][6] As an only child, she was largely raised by her grandparents due to her parents' demanding careers, which fostered a close-knit family environment centered on her well-being. Her family's protective nature stemmed from her early health challenges, shaping a childhood marked by caution and limited outdoor freedoms.[7] From infancy, Ercümen struggled with allergic asthma, experiencing severe breathing difficulties, frequent fevers, and an inability to engage in typical play, often spending nights unable to sleep comfortably. This condition led to a highly restricted lifestyle, where she rarely ventured outside, likening herself to a child living in a "bubble" isolated from peers. Her family's emphasis on survival and health care influenced her early years, with no additional siblings pursued due to concerns over her fragility, prioritizing her stability above all.[5][7] Growing up in coastal Çanakkale, surrounded by the Aegean Sea and local maritime culture, Ercümen developed an innate fascination with water despite her limitations. At around age five, she was first exposed to swimming during family outings, but her true passion ignited at seven or eight during a school trip to a local yacht club, where she secretly began practicing in the Çanakkale Strait. With no indoor pools available, she swam year-round in the cold, currentswept waters, even attending school with wet hair in winter, an activity that paradoxically improved her respiratory health and provided a sense of liberation underwater. To fund her informal sessions, she sold handmade necklaces, marking her initial non-competitive engagements with the sea as a personal escape and source of joy.[5][7][8]Academic and professional training Şahika Ercümen began her formal education in Çanakkale, attending Gazi Primary School for her early years. She continued her secondary education at Milli Piyango Anatolian High School in the same city, where her interest in sports began to shape her priorities alongside academics.[6][9] To pursue higher education, Ercümen relocated to Ankara and enrolled at Başkent University in the Faculty of Health Sciences, Department of Nutrition and Dietetics. She graduated with a bachelor's degree in nutrition and dietetics, qualifying her as a professional dietitian. She later completed a master's degree in clinical nutrition at the same institution, enhancing her expertise in the field.[6][10][11] Ercümen selected the nutrition and dietetics program due to its central role in her life, alongside sports and education, recognizing its importance for physical performance early on. During her university years, she began integrating her dietary knowledge into her personal fitness routines, tailoring nutrition plans to support her athletic demands and physiological changes from training. This practical application extended to preparing customized nutrition programs for national sports teams, combining her academic training with hands-on experience in sports nutrition.[10][12]Sports career Involvement in underwater sports Şahika Ercümen began water sports around 1998 to manage her childhood asthma, entering the world of underwater sports in 2001, starting with scuba diving and underwater rugby, which marked the beginning of her competitive athletic journey.[13][14] From that year onward, she became a member of the Turkish national teams across multiple disciplines, including underwater hockey, rugby, underwater orienteering, and free diving, representing her country in numerous World and European championships.[13] Early in her career, Ercümen achieved significant success in national competitions, such as winning the Turkish championship in underwater navigation in 2003.[14] She also excelled in team events, securing third place at the 2008 European Underwater Hockey Championships in Istanbul as part of the Turkish national team.[15] These accomplishments highlighted her versatility and contributed to her accumulation of over 100 medals in swimming and underwater sports tournaments by the mid-2000s.[14] Her training for these breath-hold-dependent disciplines involved intensive regimens focused on enhancing apnea capacity and teamwork under water, essential for the fast-paced, cooperative nature of sports like underwater hockey and rugby.[15] Ercümen's educational background as a dietitian further supported her preparation by allowing her to optimize nutrition for sustained performance in these demanding activities.[13]Transition to freediving Following her participation in team-based underwater sports such as hockey and rugby, Şahika Ercümen shifted her focus to individual freediving in 2006, seeking to push her personal boundaries in apnea disciplines.[16] This transition was influenced by her lifelong engagement with water sports, which began as a means to overcome childhood asthma, fostering a deep affinity for underwater exploration.[9] Ercümen's early freediving endeavors included intensive preparation leading to her international debut, with training emphasizing breath control and mental resilience in coastal environments. Her breakthrough came at the 2006 AIDA Freediving World Championship in Tenerife, Spain, where she delivered a standout national performance that established her as a rising talent and paved the way for subsequent competitive successes.[16][17] As a certified dietitian, Ercümen applied her nutritional knowledge to tailor her regimen for freediving's physiological requirements, prioritizing diets that enhance oxygen utilization and sustain prolonged breath-holds during dives.[9] This integration of her academic background in nutrition and dietetics allowed her to optimize recovery and performance from the outset of her solo career.Freediving achievements Major competitions and awards Şahika Ercümen has excelled in international freediving competitions under the auspices of organizations like CMAS and AIDA, securing multiple medals that underscore her dominance in depth disciplines. At the 2024 CMAS Freediving World Cup held in Kaş, Antalya, Turkey, she claimed four gold medals across key categories: constant weight at 86 meters, constant weight bi-fins at 77 meters, constant weight no fins with rope support at 76 meters, and constant weight no fins at 46 meters.[18] This performance highlighted her versatility and positioned her as a leading contender for subsequent global events. Additionally, during the CMAS World Freediving Championships, Ercümen earned a gold medal in a depth discipline.[19] In AIDA-sanctioned events, Ercümen has also achieved notable placements, including a bronze medal in free immersion at the 2024 AIDA Depth World Championship with a dive to 77 meters.[20] Her participation in earlier AIDA competitions, such as the 2022 World Championship where she reached 68 meters in constant weight, further illustrates her consistent presence on the international stage despite occasional procedural challenges like yellow cards for protocol issues.[21] On the national level, Ercümen has dominated Turkish Underwater Sports Federation events, amassing numerous titles that affirm her status as Türkiye's premier freediver. At the 2025 Free Diving Open Water Seniors and Masters Individual Türkiye Championship in Kaş, Antalya, she swept four gold medals in constant weight with fins, constant weight no fins, constant weight bi-fins, and constant weight with guide rope categories.[22] Similarly, in the 2024 Turkish Free Diving Championship, she secured three golds: 85 meters in constant weight monofin, 77 meters in constant weight, and 65 meters in constant weight no fins.[23] Over her career, she has won more than 100 medals in underwater sports tournaments since 1998, including multiple national records recognized by the federation.[9] Beyond competition medals, Ercümen has received honors from Turkish sports bodies for her overall contributions, such as recognition from the Turkish Underwater Sports Federation as a flagship athlete driving the nation's success in freediving.[24] These accolades, combined with her competitive record, have cemented her ranking among the world's top freedivers, currently at No. 80 globally by depth performance metrics.[21]World records Şahika Ercümen has established several world records in various freediving disciplines, primarily verified by the Confédération Mondiale des Activités Subaquatiques (CMAS), with one recognized by Guinness World Records. These achievements span dynamic apnea, constant weight, and variable weight categories, often conducted in challenging environments such as icy lakes, saltwater seas, and freshwater bodies without breathing apparatus. The records highlight her progression in depth and technique, utilizing methods like no-fins descents aided by weighted sleds for variable weight attempts or self-propelled swims for dynamic and constant weight dives.
