--- [David Crombie - Wikipedia] ---
David Edward Crombie  (born April 24, 1936) is a former Canadian academic and politician who served as the 56th mayor of Toronto from 1972 to 1978. Crombie was elected to Parliament following his tenure as mayor. A member of the Progressive Conservative (PC) Party, he served as minister of national health and welfare from 1979 to 1980, minister of Indian affairs and northern development from 1984 to 1986, and secretary of state for Canada from 1986 to 1988.

--- [David Crombie - Birthdate: April 24th, 1936] ---
A Canadian educator and politician who served as the 56th Mayor of Toronto, known for his progressive policies and revitalization of the city's waterfront. He's a champion of urban renewal and community development. David Crombie is best known for his pioneering efforts in urban reform, serving as the 56th mayor of Toronto from 1972 to 1978 and leaving an indelible mark on the city's development. As a champion of socially responsible urban development, Crombie's policies differed sharply from those of his predecessors, prioritizing community interests and curbing the excesses of the development industry. Born on April 24, 1936, in Swansea, a village west of Toronto, Crombie was the son of Vera Edith Beamish and Norman Davis Crombie. He began his academic career as a lecturer in politics and urban affairs at Ryerson University in the 1960s, where he became involved in Toronto's urban reform movement. Crombie's involvement in the urban reform movement led to his election to Toronto's city council in 1970, and subsequently, his election as mayor in 1972. As mayor, he ushered in a new era of socially responsible urban development, inspired by thinkers such as Jane Jacobs. Crombie's tenure as mayor was marked by his efforts to rein in the development industry. He initially imposed a 45-foot height limit on new constructions, but this was overturned by the Ontario Municipal Board. Undeterred, Crombie put forward a new official plan that imposed varying height restrictions across the city, which was upheld by the board. Following his tenure as mayor, Crombie was elected to Parliament as a member of the Progressive Conservative Party. He served as Minister of National Health and Welfare from 1979 to 1980, Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development from 1984 to 1986, and Secretary of State for Canada from 1986 to 1988. Crombie's legacy is built on his commitment to community-centered urban development. He believed in the importance of preserving community character and prioritizing social services over unchecked development. His policies reflected a deep understanding of the intricate relationship between urban planning and community well-being. Crombie's influence on modern society is evident in the many urban reform movements that have followed in his footsteps. His commitment to socially responsible development has inspired a new generation of urban planners, policymakers, and community activists.
