Biography [edit]Born in Ashington, Northumberland,[1] her father Dave Elliott was a professional football player at Sunderland and Newcastle United.[2] At the age of five, her family moved to Wales - she was brought up at Llandegfan near Menai Bridge on Anglesey.[3] Newspapers [edit]After competing her A Levels, Elliott joined the North Wales Chronicle in Bangor as a trainee reporter. She then worked on local newspapers in Colwyn Bay and Rhyl, before moving to the Wrexham Evening Leader, where she covered the Hillsborough disaster. Four years later, she became education correspondent for the Preston-based Lancashire Evening Post.[3] After a year, Elliott joined the Daily Post, where, for reports including one from Bosnia with the Red Cross on the Mostar massacre,[2] she twice won the regional Young Journalist of the Year award. She then became news editor of three editions of the Daily Post, becoming at the age of 26, one of the youngest news editors in regional newspapers.[3] Broadcasting [edit]Elliott then took a job with HTV Wales as a North Wales correspondent for Wales Tonight, based at the station's Colwyn Bay newsroom.[4] She then moved to BBC Wales' Bangor newsroom as a North Wales reporter, before relocating to Cardiff to become education correspondent. As part of her BBC training, she also reported for BBC Radio 4, Radio 5 Live and later the BBC News Channel, as both a regional and special UK correspondent. She later became the presenter of BBC Wales' flagship current affairs series Week In, Week Out and the consumer affairs programme X-ray.[3][4] Elliott later switched to radio, joining Jamie Owen in 2007 as host of a weekday morning show on BBC Radio Wales.[2][3] On 24 September 2012, she became the host of the station's weekday afternoon show, replacing Roy Noble, but quit just over a year later in order to pursue writing ambitions.[5] She returned to BBC Radio Wales in February 2015 as the new main presenter of the flagship breakfast news programme, Good Morning Wales, alongside Oliver Hides.[6] Elliott left the programme ten months later. --- [Louise Elliott returns to BBC Wales - Media Centre] ---
Louise Elliott returns to BBC Wales Broadcaster Louise Elliott is returning to BBC Radio Wales in early 2015 to present the station’s flagship news programme, Good Morning Wales. Louise left Radio Wales in December 2013 to take a career break. She joined the station in 2007 after nine years in news, current affairs and consumer journalism on television and radio at BBC Wales. In this time, she hosted a number of award-winning programmes, picking up accolades from Sony and Bafta Cymru amongst others. Louise will join Oliver Hides to present Good Morning Wales, Monday to Friday from February. She says, "It is a real privilege to join the Good Morning Wales team and I'm looking forward to being part of the programme's success." Steve Austins, Editor of BBC Radio Wales, says: “I’m delighted that Louise is re-joining the Radio Wales team in the New Year. She’s a terrific presenter who has a real rapport with her audience and I know she’ll form a formidable partnership with Ollie on Good Morning Wales”. Notes to Editors: • Louise Elliott worked as presenter of the BBC Wales TV programme X-Ray for almost three years and was awarded Consumer Journalist of the Year in 2006. • She worked as a BBC network news correspondent in London, reporting for Radio 4's Today programme, Breakfast, Radio 4's PM programme, News 24 and for Radio 5 Live. • She reported for BBC Wales Today for two years, including as North Wales correspondent and education correspondent. • She has also presented BBC Wales' investigative current affairs series, Week In Week Out. • Previously she worked on the North Wales Chronicle in Bangor, the Wrexham Evening Leader, Lancashire Evening Post, the Daily Post and regional papers in Colwyn Bay, Rhyl, Manchester and Liverpool. Roles included education correspondent, health correspondent and news editor. • She was named Young Journalist of the Year in 1993 for reports from Bosnia in the aftermath of the Mostar massacre.
