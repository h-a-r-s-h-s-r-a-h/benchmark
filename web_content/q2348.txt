Bilger was born Leonora Neuffer on 3 February 1893, in Boston, Massachusetts. Her father was George Neuffer and her mother was Elizabeth Neuffer. Bilger matriculated at the in 1909 and earned her Bachelor of Arts degree in Chemistry in 1913, as well as her master"s degree in 1914. In 1916, Bilger received her Doctor of Philosophy in chemistry from the University of Cincinnati, where she would later work. She was a noted teacher and administrator at the University of Hawaii for the majority of her career. She died on 19 February 1975, 82 years old. In 1919, she returned to her alma mater"s department of chemistry, where she stayed for 10 years. Bilger spent a year from 1924-1925 at Newnham College Cambridge University as a Sarah Berliner Fellow. She was promoted to the department head in 1943 and held that position for 11 years. In 1953 she became a Senior Professor there. In 1960, she retired and became a professor emerita at the university until 1964, when she retired fully. During her time at the University of Hawaii, she designed their new chemistry laboratory. Completed in 1951, the 70,000 square-foot facility cost $1.5 million and was named after the Bilgers in 1959. Bilger"s studies of nitrogenous compounds began with her thesis work, which concerned hydroxylamines and hydroxamic acids. When she returned to the University of Cincinnati"s chemistry department, she was tasked with directing its research. She herself studied asymmetric nitrogenous chemicals during this time. Bilger continued this research at the University of Hawaii, however, she allocated more time to teaching and administrative duties. Her later research concerned the toxicity of cancer-fighting chemicals. She also examined the sterols found in tropical oils, and pigments in red peppers. She was a member of the American Association for the Advancement of Science and the New York Academy of Sciences.
