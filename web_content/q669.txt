--- [Between Dante and Vergil who is really the mature one who ... -...] ---
For Dante, it's mostly coming form the first DMC light novel. Hiding his identity as Tony Redgrave he works as a bounty hunter. Gunsmith Nell, who makes him Ebony and Ivory at the end of the novel, acts a bit as a mother. His bountyhunter partner Grue is a bit of a father figure. He has a small family of his own, where Dante often spents time. And Grue's daughter Jessica can be interpretated as a love interest. Then Gilver shows up and demons kill everything and everyone. That's the moment where Dante switches back to his true name to not hide anymore and instead wants to start working as a demon hunter with his identity out in the open to get revenge. (So basically leading up to DMC3.) Now as for most lore stuff, it's often debateable how canon it is, since the general story went through a lot of changes over time for reasons, often being full of holes and contradicting. The whole Gilver storyline doesn't even really match with DMC1, let alone DMC3, so is not considered canon anymore. All the Tony stuff got referenced again later, and thus made it into canon. DMC5 mentions gunsmith Nell, with Nico even being related to her. The DMC5 light novel mentions the bar, they used to get their jobs as bounty hunters. V goes there to talk to Morrision to hire Dante. The DMC anime has a whole episode dedicated to his Tony Redgrave past. As a small kid, so before the light novel, he was living with an unnamed woman in a town. Befriended a boy and even went to school like a "normal kid". Until a demon sensed him, destroyed the town, everyone blamed him and his "mother", making them leave. It is never clarified, who that woman was. It could be Nell, but also another woman, who took care of him for a while, and maybe they parted later on. For Vergil, it was more a sidenote made behind the scenes. From the DMC3 manga we already knew, Dante was hidden at home and Vergil attacked somewhere outside by the demons. DMC5 elaborates on that, with the whole monologue Eva dumps on Dante, about changing his name. So it's save to say, that's where he got the idea from to name himself Tony. Vergil didn't get the memo, and the thought might not have crossed his mind on his own. So he always wandered around as himself.
