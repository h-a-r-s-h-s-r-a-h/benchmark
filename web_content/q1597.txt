--- [Jackie Tour | Concerts Wiki - Fandom] ---
The Jackie Tour is the fourth concert tour by Ciara in support of her fourth studio album, "Jackie." The tour began on February 18, 2015 and ended on June 5, 2015 where she performed in 9 shows in Australasia (in New Zealand & Australia) and 19 shows in North America. Tour Background[] Ciara posted on her social media accounts a string of concerts in New Zealand and Australia. Those appearances marked her first performances in those territories, along with it being her first concert tour in 6 years. Although sales were strong, the opening dates of the tour proved to be disaterious. Many events were cancelled nearly minutes before the concert was to commence. Also, many spectators purchased meet & greet packages, of which Ciara did not appear. The venues blamed Ciara's management and the booking agent, Sin City Promotions. Despite the hiccups in New Zealand, concerts in Australia went smoothly. The North American tour dates were announced in March 2015 which consisted of 19 shows solely in the United States. In October, Ciara announced a second US leg to be sponsored by Topshop which included dates in Canada; these dates were later postponed to March & April 2016 and ultimately, the tour dates were cancelled. The concert in Silver Springs, Maryland was streamed live via Yahoo! Screen in partnership with Live Nation. Speaking about the tour, Ciara stated: "I am so excited about this tour. It has been almost 6 years since my last tour. I feel this album is my best body of work. It is important to me that my fans have the greatest show experience possible. On the Jackie tour, fans will get a chance to know me more intimately, jam to good music, and most importantly be entertained. I can not wait!" --- [R&B Star Ciara Brings Her “Jackie” Tour to Florida | Hotspots Magazine] ---
It’s been over ten years now since Ciara burst onto the R&B and pop music scene with her first album Goodies, and five albums later, fans nationwide are still going crazy over the “Queen of Dance.” On May 4, she released her sixth studio album, named Jackie, after her mother. This month she is kicking off a promotional tour for her new album, and she will be making two stops in Florida. She will perform her first Florida tour date in Miami Beach at The Fillmore Miami Beach at the Jackie Gleason Theater on Friday, May 15 at 8:30 p.m. The next night, she will be in Tampa at The Ritz Ybor, with her performance starting at 9 p.m. Fans fell in love with the Atlanta-based Ciara as soon as she released her debut album Goodies in 2004. The album went triple-platinum and spawned massive hit singles such as the chart-topping “Goodies” (featuring Petey Pablo) and the #2 chart successes “1, 2 Step” (featuring Missy Elliott) and “Oh” (featuring Ludacris). Her follow-up album, Ciara: The Evolution, was also very successful, and the singles “Get Up” and “Promise” became radio favorites and R&B chart hits. Her career high came in 2009 when she partnered with Justin Timberlake on the single “Love Sex Magic,” which hit the top ten on the pop charts and won Ciara her first Grammy Award. Her current album, Jackie, is her sixth studio album, and the lead single, “I Bet,” has already reached the top twenty on the R&B charts. Tickets are available for purchase right now online via Ticketmaster, Ciara’s website (click on “Live” at onlyciara.com), and the websites and box offices of the respective venues (fillmoremb.com, theritzybor.com). Ticket prices start at $29.50 (Miami Beach) and $27.50. Reserved seating is available for $45 (Miami Beach) and $61.65 (Tampa) per person; the reserved seating price for The Ritz Ybor in Tampa is in the VIP area on the second-floor balcony. For more information on Ciara, visit onlyciara.com. “Like” her on Facebook by going to facebook.com/ciaramusic and follow her on Twitter and Instagram @ciara.

--- [Review: Ciara's Jackie Tour works Tampa's Ritz Ybor crowd into a sweat] ---
Sixteen days after releasing her new album Jackie , Ciara brought her tour to the Ritz Ybor Saturday night and serenaded her diehards.A little more than two weeks seems like a tight turnaround if you consider the music business by its old model: release singles, release records, perform at award shows, and then tour. But artists have been proving for the past five years that the old rules do not apply, so Ciara opted to do things differently this time too.Shortly after releasing the album, Ciara allowed Yahoo to live-stream a performance of her Jackie Tour in Silver Spring, Md. The professionally shot and edited clip can still be found floating around on YouTube for all who are a little too short on cash to see the Atlanta Queen of Crunk 'n' B in the flesh. Go ahead. We'll wait .What you see there is exactly what fans got Saturday night, down to the "Gee whiz, you guys are great!" aww-shucks-isms she slipped in between songs to catch her breath.If you're a fan of Ciara's earlier albums, you'll hear the big singles you remember plus some unexpected covers. But the point of the Jackie Tour is to sell Jackie . So if you hadn't heard any of it before showing up, you got a crash course, with a whopping eight of the record's 11 tracks woven through the set.Even with that major spoiler, for fans, the show is a worthwhile experience, if only to see a perfect human specimen with your own eyes.You are not fitter than Ciara. Let's just get that out of the way.Watching her abs contract with each swing of her hips makes one wonder what would have become of her had she poured her teenaged energies into an Olympic sport instead of achieving pop stardom. Even after delivering a nine-pound baby a year ago, her arms and thighs and everything else are as enviable as when she burst onto the scene singing about her Goodies in 2004.She opened her 95-minute set with her album's eponymous track, declaring herself a "bad motherf-----" and kicked straight into the first of many dance routines that showcased her musicality, flexibility and stamina. Standouts from the Jackie songs included That's How I'm Feeling , a club banger featuring Pitbull and Missy Elliott , and Dance Like We're Making Love , another dance song that may seep into summer.Ciara made an effort to subvert expectations early with a cover of Sade 's Cherish the Day that fell amazingly flat even as fans whooped for their idol. The hypnotic and magnetic qualities of Sade's voice escape Ciara, but that's okay; it's not like Sade could be her in a twerk-off. We all have our talents.Her best vocal performances came when she got a chance to stop moving for a second and really focus on breathing and control. Body Party sent the crowd into a tizzy, especially when the band blended it with Joe 's Let's Make a Love Scene , turning the two songs into a super sexy chicken soup for the bedroom.A brief Michael Jackson tribute featured a super-smooth moonwalk as the live band played Billie Jean . Ballads I Bet and I Got You lacked the emotional attachment you want from songs so personal and raw. The way she smiled, you'd never know she broke up with her son's father and rushed headlong into single motherhood in the past year. There's no Mary J. Blige heartbreak for Ciara. Rawness isn't her skill set.Overall, the show was strong if not vocally impressive. CiCi is an entertainer through and through. If she can't sing you into submission, she'll expend enough sweat to make sure you came away seeing something new.It's still unclear if this take on music's new business model will work for her. There are worse ways to spend 95 minutes, though. Don't take my word for it. See it for yourself. -- Robbyn Mitchell
