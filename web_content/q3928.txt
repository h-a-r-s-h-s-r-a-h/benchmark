Cáceres (Spanish pronunciation: [ˈkaseɾes]) is a town and municipality in the Colombian department of Antioquia.
Cáceres is one of the oldest towns in Antioquia. Its history is linked to the exploitation of gold. The town was founded in 1576 by Captain Don Gaspar de Rodas, who arrived on the banks of the Cauca River, advancing on the right bank. He ordered the installation of 30 ranches with a ceremony and named the town San Martín de Cáceres.

It's a cool place with a long history! Contents Discovering Cáceres Cáceres is one of the oldest towns in the Antioquia region. It's known for its connection to gold mining. The town was officially started in 1576. How Cáceres Began Captain Don Gaspar de Rodas founded Cáceres. He arrived on the banks of the Cauca River. He chose a spot and set up 30 small houses or "ranches." After a lively ceremony, the new town was named San Martín de Cáceres. This marked the beginning of a new community. Where is Cáceres? Cáceres is located in the Antioquia Department of Colombia. It's part of the Bajo Cauca Antioquia subregion. This area is known for its natural beauty and rivers. The town is situated near the Cauca River. People and Size In 2015, about 37,806 people lived in Cáceres. It's a place where many families have lived for generations. The town and its surrounding area make up the municipality. History of Gold Mining Gold has played a big part in Cáceres's history. The early settlers were interested in finding gold. This activity helped the town grow and develop over time. Mining is still an important part of the region's economy. --- [CÁCERES – Corregimientos de Antioquia] ---
En el año 1576 se fundó Cáceres esto lo convierte en uno de los pueblos más antiguos de Antioquia y solo hasta 1903 fue su erección como municipio. Su historia está ligada a la explotación de oro y sus habitantes son, en buena medida, descendientes de los negros llegados desde Cartagena de Indias en los años de la Colonia. El municipio es considerado la «Capital Católica del Bajo Cauca». Entre sus atractivos turísticos se encuentran: Quebrada Noa – Restaurantes con mirador hacia el Río Cauca – Gran Reserva Natural Bajo Cauca – Nechí. Aproximadamente el 80% de su población vive en la zona rural. Es el municipio más extenso del Bajo Cauca. Y cuenta con 5 corregimientos en su territorio que son; Guarumo, Manizales, El Jardín Tamaná, Piamonte y Puerto Bélgica.
