--- [Fan Favorite Cast Members Of The Circle Season 2, Ranked] ---
The second season of Netflix's reality competition series The Circle is much more drama and conflict-ridden than season one. While the players in the first season surprised viewers by getting along, sharing intimate details of their personal lives, and forming the unlikeliest bonds, the cast of season two is more focused on winning at any cost. This means there's plenty of conflicts, passive-aggressive messages, gossip, and talking behind one another's backs. The fan-favorite characters of season two are, for the most part, those who try to stay out of the drama and spotlight, quietly being their effervescent selves while trying to win $100,000. Lisa (Lance Bass) Fans wanted to see a celebrity in season two and they got a celebrity…sort of. It was a slight miss having Lisa, personal assistant to Lance Bass, come on the show pretending to be Bass himself. With most of the players being in their 20s, many grew up long after the height of N'SYNC's fame. Thus, while they probably know Justin Timberlake, they don't know much about the boy band that he used to be in. So, while Lisa arrived armed with enough knowledge about Bass to be able to convince others she was the real deal, only a handful of players would even be able to call her out on inaccuracies anyway. Considering the show's viewership skews much younger than Bass' primary 40+ fanbase, it means Lisa isn't among the most popular in the cast. That said, as a new addition to an already established group of eight, she also hasn't had time to shine either. Terilisha First impressions are usually pretty important, but on a show like The Circle, they can be totally misleading. Such was the case with Terilisha, who started off as a favorite playing, not only among other players in the game but also with fans. But her penchant for conflict and attempts to throw Savannah under the bus began to show true colors that didn't sit well with fans. Both women were at fault for continuing a public rift. But fans don't like that Terilisha played too hard, too fast. She became so laser-focused on gameplay that her attempts to form bonds with others, like Courtney, were totally transparent and disingenuous. Jack (Emily) Jack's a tough one to peg. Viewers both love his confidence and insistence that he can fool anyone but are also annoyed by his arrogance. Unbeknownst to Jack, some of the women in the cast already suspect that he might not actually be who he purports to be. Jack is one of the best strategic players, always trying to be a fly on the wall while also staying one step removed from any drama or conflict. With that said, others notice his inability to take sides and have called him out (albeit privately) for his lack of backbone in the game. Bryant Sadly, Bryant wasn't popular among the cast, though considering he has the second-highest Instagram following, he came into the game already popular among fans. Ironically, it was his likeability that put a target on his back right out of the gate. Players wanted him gone before his too-nice nature made him a shoo-in for influencer. He would easily have formed tight friendships with everyone had he been given the chance to stay. Lee (River) Playing as a young, gay man who just went through a bad breakup, Lee was an instant fan favorite. As the first player to be much older than the others – a necessary inclusion to spice up the show - Lee tried his best to learn Internet lingo from his 20-year-old niece before coming on the show so he could pass himself off as a 24-year-old. Lee wears his heart on his sleeve, crying any time someone is blocked, whether aligned with them or not. His skill as a talented writer also comes in handy to compose messages that make him instantly relatable and likable to others, including fans. Deleesa (Trevor) Deleesa, who is playing as her husband and pretending to be a single father, has taken on the persona of a guy pretty well. She had to bite her tongue so as not to get in the middle of a girl fight. Every message she sends is calculated as she stops to make sure that she is saying what Trevor would say and not what Deleesa would say. Deleesa seems down-to-earth and focused on her family and winning the money for them. All of this makes her not only a top contender but also a fan favorite, both as herself and the guy she's pretending to be. Savannah Savannah was already pretty Instagram-famous before she came on the show with close to a quarter-million followers. So, she came in with a level of popularity already. However, despite initially being a favorite, her rift with Terilisha completely ruined her game and she fell to the bottom of the pack. That said, despite the fact that Savannah was the second person out, she still remains a fan favorite. Courtney Courtney's outgoing and energetic personality makes him instantly likable. It's no surprise given that he's a podcast and entertainment show host in real life. But his game started out flawed, as Savannah pointed out to him during her exit meet – that shocking meeting where each player gets to pick one person to meet in person before leaving the game for good. Nonetheless, his dialogue, infectious smile, and hilarious commentary, all likely stemming from his experience on television, make him totally entertaining to watch. Chloe Love her or hate her, Chloe brings a lot of fun and laughs to The Circle thanks to her bubbly personality. While she does like to instigate drama and is a serial flirt, there's something endearing about her. It's no surprise she was named an Influencer pretty early on. Aside from the fact that she has already proven herself to be entertaining via her time on another reality show, Too Hot to Handle, Chloe is playing a good game and doing her best to form bonds with everyone on different levels. And because of her fame from the other show, she came into The Circle as the most popular player already.


--- [The Circle (American TV series) season 2 - Wikipedia] ---
The second season of the American reality competition streaming series The Circle began on April 14, 2021, on Netflix, and concluded on May 5, 2021. The season was announced in March 2020 when Netflix renewed The Circle for a second and third season. Michelle Buteau returned as host. 
Like the previous season, players compete against each other to become the most popular, but do not actually meet their competitors. Instead, they communicate through a specially designed app and are able to portray themselves in any way they choose.
On May 5, 2021, the season was won by DeLeesa St. Agathe, who had played the game as her husband, Trevor, and won the US$100,000 prize that came along with it. Chloe Veitch was the runner-up and won the Fan Favorite award and US$10,000.

--- [Lee From 'The Circle' Season 2 On River, Books & Crying On TV] ---
Streaming Lee From The Circle Season 2 Is Happy He Cried Through The Show The fan favorite on life after the show, what the real River thinks, and if he’d return to reality TV. Before we hang up our Zoom call, Lee Swift asks for my Instagram handle. The 58-year-old Texan, who memorably had his niece give him an internet lingo education before appearing on Season 2 of Netflix’s social media competition The Circle, is embracing life online as a way to connect with his new fans — and the journalists who write about him. Despite the deception at the center of his strategy to catfish his fellow contestants as the 24-year-old free spirit “River,” Lee’s full-hearted earnestness has endeared the author to his fellow contestants and viewers alike. “We are playing a game, but you’re also connecting with people,” Lee tells Bustle. It explains why, at the moment when River was given the power to block someone from the game, he simply couldn’t take out his biggest competition because it would mean blocking a friend. “I kept telling myself, Think with your head, think with your head, this is a game — and my heart would take over,” he says. An unabashed empath, Lee says going with feelings over strategy was worth it. “I’ve learned a lot about myself, and I got friends forever with my other cast mates,” he says in a River-like display of positivity. Ahead of the Season 2 finale, Lee chats about life after the show, the real River, and what he was reading in the Circle while doing the hard work of being a catfish. You mentioned on the show that your nieces and nephews helped you learn internet lingo to prepare to play a 24-year-old. What did that education look like? It was actually the [niece] who is in college, because I thought that is likely to be the main age [of the cast]. We just virtually went through a whole bunch of things. The thing she made a mistake on is the basic stuff like YOLO — she thought I already knew it, so we didn’t even talk about it. We got deep into the weeds, but I should have got YOLO and FOMO, things that everybody should know that I didn’t! When did you decide to be a catfish? Through the casting process, I was kind of torn. But I ended up ultimately thinking hey, [the cast is] gonna be mainly young people, I think I can connect with them, I can create this character, which is kind of built from my own life experiences when I was younger. I got so much out of re-living being 24 that it was worth it to me. Seeing John — who appeared to be an older gay man — come in and be well received, did you have any second thoughts that you should have just gone in as yourself? I absolutely did think that. I really thought, why didn’t I come in [as myself], because everybody was gravitating to him. And I think John — you know, Lisa and Jack — had an advantage because we’d gotten rid of some catfish, so nobody was really catfish hunting anymore. I so gravitated to the character they created because it was kind of like me, you know? I’m curious about the real River. When did you decide you’d be using his photos, and what does he think about it now that the show is out? We’ve talked. He’s getting so many people following him — he’s loving it, to be honest. He has aspirations; he’s a very talented, wonderful young guy, and I want the best for him. I actually thought about going in as a girl. I had three sets of friends that I had put together [as potential profiles] — and also my niece, so four, I guess. I kind of kept looking at him, and as I was developing the character — his [real] name’s Doak — it was his photos that just spoke to me. What kinds of reactions have you been getting from your friends and family, and from strangers? Well, the friends and family, it’s constant mocking [laughs]. It’s all in fun. It’s like, “Do we have to get a limo for you now that you’re a celebrity?” And I go, “No, just a bowl of green M&Ms and a bottle of champagne.” But no, they’ve been so supportive and wonderful. As far as the public and the fans of the show, I’m just overwhelmed by it. And they’re all over the world. The thing that’s touching me the most is a lot of queer young people that are saying, “Thank you so much, you’re such a good example and you’re giving me hope that you can be happy and have a full life.” That means more than anything to me. The main thing I’ve seen on Twitter so far is people thinking your crying on the show is endearing. [Laughs] My god. My husband, because he knows me, was like, “Whatever you do, don’t cry.” What do I do? I cry on Episode 1. I’ve had some people be like, “How could you cry that fast?” Bryant and Savannah, I didn't know well. I did get a connection with Bryant right away; Savannah, we’ve made the connection after the show, to be honest. But I knew how bad I wanted to be in there, and I knew how bad they must be hurting [when they were blocked]. I’m an empathetic kind of person and it just came out. And I just don’t hold it in, and I’m not ashamed of that. Now that the show is out, have you asked Lisa to get that intro to Lance Bass? Yes. [Laughs] Have you spoken to him? She said we’re gonna work it out. [Lisa’s] a doll. I can’t wait to meet him. My husband and I are both fans. Was there anything that surprised you, watching the episodes back? For me, seeing my cast mates and their responses [they said out loud] that they weren’t typing was illuminating. Because you get what they say, but the feelings that they’re sharing in their apartments — and it was fun seeing their apartments, too — that was very illuminating. Is there a specific moment that you’re referring to? Probably the most revealing to me was how intense Savannah and Terilisha were in their first influencer chat. Both of them to me were much better players than I was. I think as far as strategy, what happened was they were so powerful everybody [thought], These are the two that we gotta get rid of, because they’re gonna win. As a writer and book lover, were you allowed to read once in the Circle? Did you bring books? We were allowed to bring in some books, and I did read some, but it’s so funny: People think there’s so much downtime. I don’t know if it was just me, but there was not a lot of downtime, we were busy chatting or doing games. You’re eating, or you’re getting ready for something. I read some, but not a lot, to be honest. I had Ricky Martin’s autobiography that I was reading. That’s amazing. I’m a big fan of his, I think he’s incredible. Has your perception of social media changed since you’ve been on the show? I think when I was a 13-year-old gay kid in West Texas and feeling so alone, so hopeless, how great it would have been to have Instagram, and Twitter, and TikTok, and be able to see, “Oh my gosh, I’m not by myself. There's other hearts like me.” I love it. Yes, are there things we need to make it better, for sure. But I think we can spread a lot of love through social media. Now that you’ve learned what FOMO means, what’s next for you? I have a book that just came out called Gabe’s Story. I’m kind of seeing where this goes. I feel myself still keeping my books going, but I kind of want to go back to screenplays and writing for television. I’ve been optioned, but I’ve never had anything made, so maybe this time something could get made! Would you ever go on another reality show? ….yes. [Laughs] None of the dating shows! I am a fan of reality shows. I just eat it up. I like Big Brother, I like Survivor, I like Too Hot To Handle, I liked Love Is Blind, oh my gosh… I love reality TV, I would definitely do it again. You wanna know my dream thing? It’s for The Circle to ask me back. This interview has been edited and condensed for clarity. This article was originally published on
