Loquatius Seelie is a changeling warlock/bard and is a news media personality for the Herald's Tome. He is played by Sam Riegel. Description [edit | edit source]Appearance [edit | edit source]As a changeling, Loquatius can alter his appearance at will, but his "default" form has dark-rimmed white eyes, and a shock of white hair that stands up. His skin is pale, and dark veins are visible through it, giving it an appearance almost like marble. The veins beneath the skin constantly move.[6] In his broadcasts, as himself, he wears a purple-lined gold jacket, and appears with a golden face and purple hair.[7] He also shifts his appearance during his ad reads.[8] Personality [edit | edit source]Loquatius appears confident and self-important, and enjoys being a celebrity.[9] Biography [edit | edit source]Background [edit | edit source]Loquatius is originally from the Feywild and was a member of the Seelie Court prior to living in Avalir.[10][11] It is likely that his warlock patron, an archfey, is the matriarch of his court, Lady Elmenore. He married Laerryn Coramar-Seelie seven years before to the events of Exandria Unlimited: Calamity, at the last Replenishment. The two have since divorced.[12] "Excelsior" (E3x01) [edit | edit source]Loquatius gave his morning broadcast at the Herald's Tome. He expressed concern to his assistant, Aria, about some broken crystal columns in Evenloft, which were preventing his broadcasts from going out to some, and went to see his ex-wife Laerryn to get them fixed.[13] He and Laerryn had a somewhat argumentative conversation in her quarters, but both of them expressed some wistfulness about their upcoming anniversary.[14] Later that evening, Loquatius arrived at the party at the Palazzo Por'co with a date, Bolo.[15] He spoke briefly to Purvan Suul.[16] "Bitterness and Dread" (E3x02) [edit | edit source]Loquatius realized, while looking at the body of the unidentified cleric of a Betrayer God, that the person had intended to complete a task at the party but had been killed by Cerrit before they finished.[17] Afterwards, he and Cerrit went to the Herald's Tome to look for more information on Vespin Chloras. Cerrit found crucial information on the Tree of Names, and that the files on Evandrin were missing. He asked Loquatius about the reporter, Elena Tuvaris, and Loquatius told him that she'd been let go, but did not reveal more.[18] "Blood and Shadow" (E3x03) [edit | edit source]Loquatius attempted to distract Milus Phren before the fight in Dawnsledge, but did not succeed.[19] He provided considerable support during the fight before Laerryn used Dimension Door to take him with her to the helm. At the helm, after he and Laerryn defended the eldritch battery, they spoke: Loquatius said that she should ask permission of the city before using the Astral Leywright.[20] Laerryn told him that he could go back to the Feywild, and that history would remember her, but Loquatius pointed out that history is fickle and written by people like him.[21] He also agreed with her that this was her life's focus, but that on focusing only on her work she had neglected him and their marriage.[22] After Laerryn directed Calum Staffwright to short the tithe, Loquatius warned her against it but also sent out a message to his contacts providing a general overview of the attacks on the city but keeping out much of the information.[23] He also recorded a crystal to be used in the case of his death in which he credited Laerryn for pointing out the threat to the city and said that he had stood in her way.[24] Loquatius, along with Laerryn, successfully resisted Zerxus's Zone of Truth when the party reconvened, though he did not speak much during that conversation.[25] When the Ring of Brass went to the Arboreal Calix, Loquatius was the first to notice the Ley-rudder stuck in the branches of the tree, and pointed it out.[26] When Nydas attempted to stop Laerryn from casting Blight, Loquatius used Fey Presence to attempt to frighten him, though it was canceled out by Zerxus's aura.[27] He then tried to pull Patia away from the tree, but instead began to also receive information from it. He saw Elmenore, who warned him to not let Patia find the names and who told him: "Something moves upon the face of your world."[11] The tree then lanced a branch through Loquatius, and he looked at Laerryn, scared of what she was doing, just before she cast Blight.[28] "Fire and Ruin" (E3x04) [edit | edit source]All of Loquatius's magic items other than the ether Laerryn had given him were destroyed when the Tree of Names exploded, and he bled out from his injuries.[29] He was resurrected by Zerxus shortly after, and Laerryn apologized to him.[30] After hearing the prophecy, he headed to the Herald's Tome station to broadcast and warn the city.[31] At the Herald's Tome, Aria helped him prepare for the broadcast, which he planned to send to both Avalir and Cathmoíra. They were interrupted by Loras, who teleported into the station and demanded he tell the people that all spell energy be used to preserve the city and Septarion.[32] Loquatius told Loras he would do just that, then cast Wall of Force to cordon him off and silence him.[33] Elmenore also at this time opened a portal for Loquatius, along with all members of the Seelie Court, so that they could escape back to the safety of the Feywild, per an ancient pact with the Nine Hells, but Loquatius declined, telling her he had to stay with Laerryn. Elmenore sadly agreed.[34] He addressed his broadcast to Toramunda, and told the people of the cities to evacuate, while urging the administrators to stay with the city as it fell, and used his changeling abilities to shift into the appearance of Eldamir the Wise to repeat the message.[35] He also recognized all of his colleagues in the Ring of Brass for their knowledge and bravery.[36] Loquatius returned to the Astral Leywright along with the rest of the party to protect Laerryn as she worked, and prevented her from falling unconscious with a held Healing Word. He also proposed to her during the fight.[37] As the city was destroyed after Laerryn successfully diverted the primordials Rau'shan and Ka'Mort, he and Laerryn stood in the room and confessed their love shortly before their deaths.[38] Post-ExU: Calamity [edit | edit source]Loquatius has reunited with Laerryn in the afterlife. Together they wander the Astral Plane looking for Evandrin.[39] Relationships [edit | edit source]Loquatius was formerly married to Laerryn. Description [edit | edit source]Laerryn is a pretty, dark-skinned elven woman. She is tall and lanky, and wears primarily gold clothing, sometimes with a purple cape. She has an angular face and gold eyes.[10] She is 100 to 150 years old in Exandria Unlimited: Calamity. Aabria Iyengar conceptualized this as the equivalent of being in her late 20s compared to the 40s that she perceived Patia Por'co (described as middle-aged) to be.[1] Laerryn is left-handed.[11] Personality [edit | edit source]Laerryn takes her role as a key maintainer of the infrastructure of the city solemnly, and is an ambitious person and a perfectionist. She frequently multitasks, and can be somewhat brusque and impatient.[12] She hates attending parties, preferring to make a brief appearance before leaving again to work.[13] She used to like parties and events, but recently became entirely preoccupied with her work, grieving for her friend Evandrin Alterra who vanished into the unknown in the aftermath of an experiment with the Astral Leywright.[14] Laerryn is unconcerned with others not understanding her project in the short-term and believes that others will understand in time and that "history will remember her fondly for making the road to the next big discoveries a little easier". She dedicates herself "quietly and dutifully" to protecting the Heart of Avalir and to realizing Avalir's mission of collecting knowledge and magic to share with the world as it traveling the leylines. She is disinterested in garnering immediate praise or accolades for her work, and her jealousy of the Magisterium is specifically over resources available to them. She sees herself as working to improve the lives of those in Avalir (especially her ex-husband Loquatius Seelie) and the future of Exandria. She considers her work to create a new path for Avalir as a gift "to the city and to history" rather a mandatory directive.[15] Aabria said that Laerryn's hobby is her work.[16] Biography [edit | edit source]Before Exandria Unlimited: Calamity [edit | edit source]Laerryn married Loquatius Seelie, whom she met through work,[14] seven years prior to the events of Exandria Unlimited: Calamity, at the last Replenishment.[17] She was single-mindedly focused on completing an Astral Leywright to "move a leyline to give Avalir a new road" into another Plane of Existence. She did not intend to create this path as a obligation to move the city across the planes or even to use this path immediately upon its creation. She hoped to offer it as a option and opportunity now available to the Navigator's Guild and to others throughout Exandria.[15][18] Her dedication to her work ultimately led to her divorce. She felt that their end of their relationship would give Loquatius the chance to be happy, the loneliness would allow her the ability to focus, and there would be time to fix their relationship after the Leywright was completed.[14] If she an Loquatius reconciled, she would have wanted to have at least three kids.[19] She is architect arcane at the Court of Working and a member of the Abjuration Guild, through which she holds the title of Hierophant Abjura.[20] "Excelsior" (E3x01) [edit | edit source]Laerryn worked intensely with her staff on the arcane engine of Avalir and the logistics of Avalir's descent to Cathmoíra. She then excitedly reviewed her own research into ley energies that were likely to change during the imminent celestial solstice.[21] She was interrupted by the arrival of Loquatius, and the two had an argumentative conversation but reminisced about their upcoming anniversary.[22] Later that evening, Laerryn attended the ball at the Palazzo Por'co, but wished to return to her research.[23] She investigated the arcane aspects of the strange bow that Cerrit brought.[24] "Bitterness and Dread" (E3x02) [edit | edit source]Laerryn hurried away from the party to work on her experiments in the Meridian Labyrinth. Nydas met her there with four taxmen, and she had one of them insert the bow of a solar into her project, the Astral Leywright.[25] She then used the Astral Leywright for its purpose: to transport a small machine, the Ley-Rudder, to another plane.[26] She and Nydas both celebrated, as they hope to redirect leylines and allow Avalir to travel to other planes so that they can become like the gods,[27] but she then noticed that this working caused the Arboreal Calix to draw a considerable amount of power.[28] Laerryn then went with Patia to the Magisterium in search of Micah Cormorant. They found his door locked, but Laerryn summoned a construct to break down the door.[29] The two wizards attacked and killed Magister Cormorant before searching his records and finding information about the Arboreal Calix and the original pact with the Gau Drashari, as well as evidence that Cormorant had been tampering with teleportation records.[30] At around 1 in the morning, Laerryn heard from her helmswoman that the descent to Cathmoíra was ready with no anticipated issues, but the communication ended in a choking noise. Laerryn called the rest of the Ring of Brass to go to the Meridian Labyrinth, but they were stopped by Lacrytia Hollow.[31] "Blood and Shadow" (E3x03) [edit | edit source]When questioned by Lacrytia, Laerryn told her she was heading to the helm.[32] During the ensuing fight, Laerryn notably used Reverse Gravity and Counterspelled a serious attack on Patia. She then used Dimension Door to take herself and Loquatius to the helm as the fight was still ongoing.[33] The two of them were able to secure one of the eldritch batteries under attack.[34] Laerryn and Loquatius then talk: he wanted to understand her plans, and said that she should get permission from all of Avalir. When she told him he could return to the Feywild, he said that he's happy here.[35]Calum then called her to confirm only one battery was destroyed and that there is some strange script in Primordial to decipher. He also gave her a status report on the energy in the city, and Laerryn told him to short the tithe to the Gau Drashari.[36] Loquatius was even more concerned for her, and told her history is subjective and unkind, but Laerryn continued with her plans.[37] The rest of the Ring of Brass convened at the helm. Laerryn resisted Zerxus's Zone of Truth.[38] As the party discussed their findings, Laerryn admitted that Evandrin's illness was likely due to her sending him - willingly - to the Celestial planes, and she angrily accused the druids of preventing her spells from working.[39] When the group realized that the city landing would complete Lacrytia and Vespin's plans, Cerrit realized that Laerryn was lying about not knowing how to stop it, and she told him that this is her only opportunity in 120 years to engage the Astral Leywright.[40] Cerrit left, and the rest of the Ring of Brass went to the Arboreal Calix.[41] Loquatius noticed the model of the Ley-Rudder stuck in the trees, and Laerryn tried but failed to pull it out with Mage Hand.[42] She decided the tree is what is preventing planar travel, and once she saw Evandrin stuck in the tree, began to cast Blight. Nydas tried to stop her, and a fight broke out.[43] Nydas was able to hold her for most of it, and Dweomer came to her aid, but eventually she broke free and cast Blight on the tree. It cracked in half, and Vespin, then Asmodeus, both emerged.[44] "Fire and Ruin" (E3x04) [edit | edit source]When the Tree of Names exploded, Laerryn was the only member of the Ring of Brass in the room to remain alive and conscious following the blast, though her Ring of Masks was destroyed.[45] She used Dimension Door to escape to a safe room within the Meridian Labyrinth, and watched the discussion Asmodeus had with Zerxus in Infernal via a feed from Dweomer.[46] She also telepathically messaged Cerrit, as the only other member still conscious.[47] When Zerxus returned to the room of the Arboreal Calix, she yelled at him through Dweomer to bring the other members back, then rejoined the group, along with Cerrit.[48] She apologized to Loquatius and scolded Zerxus, but urged the group to head to the Hall of Prophecy to learn what to do next.[49] She also asked Cerrit if his children were safe on the way.[50] At the Hall of Prophecy, after hearing Karwen's new prophecy that Exandria would be destroyed by the primordials Rau'shan and Ka'Mort should the Betrayer Gods awaken them, Laerryn realized that she could modify the Astral Leywright to instead move the primordials, rather than a ley line.[51] She ran back to the Leywright as the rest of the party gathered resources to help her and protect the population, listening to Loquatius's broadcast as she did so.[52] With the help of Patia's knowledge of the primordial's various names,[53] Loquatius's healing,[54] and the party protecting her from Vespin and the taxmen, Laerryn was able to successfully modify the Leywright; however, a release of energy devastating to the city could not be prevented.[55] During this fight, Loquatius proposed to her again, and the two of them stayed together in the room as the city burned.[56] Prior to her death, Laerryn pointed out the location of an exit from the Labyrinth to Cerrit so that he could escape and find his family,[57] and when he successfully did so, he contacted her over the telepathic bond, telling her "The Brass Ring endures."[58] Post-ExU: Calamity [edit | edit source]Laerryn has reunited with Quay in the afterlife and wanders the astral plane looking for Evandrin.[59] While investigating Gildhollow Tower, Ludinus Da'leth's home when he lived in Molaesmyr, Bells Hells found several of his old notes tracking solstices and arcane occurrences. In these notes he mentioned checking with a list of specialists that included Laerryn. Information from the Wrap Up and Twitter Spaces | Aabria Iyengar wanted to build Laerryn mechanically in a way to support her narrative journey. Once she knew that Laura Bailey and then Marisha Ray was building Patia Por'co, also an incredibly powerful wizard and "examples and paragons", they cooperated on their character's backstories together.[89]Mel Medarda was one of Aabria's inspirations for Laerryn.[90] Aabria initially wanted Laerryn to be a Graviturgy wizard, but upon realizing that dunamancy had not yet developed in the Age of Arcanum, she settled on the School of Abjuration, because it fit with Laerryn's personality as someone who "fundamentally wants to protect things" and to mitigate damage.[91] In the early abjuration builds, Aabria was considering giving Laerryn levels of Artificer to represent her engineering occupation, but Aabria's desire to improve her abjuration capabilities outweighed the Artificer option.[92] Aabria picked a high elf, because in her mind this race represents "hubris and inherent class distinction", which was the prominent climate among Age of Arcanum mages. Aabria also settled on playing a high elf because she wanted to create a character who obsessively chases a singular goal with incredible dedication and doesn't sleep, only temporarily resting in a trance. It is understandable to focus her everything on a project as a high elf who lives much longer and who lives through a decade "like a month-long crunch at the end of a big push".[93] Brennan let the players to roll twice for their ability scores and take the highest roll. Aabria rolled well for Laerryn, maxing Intelligence to boost her spellcasting modifier, and raising Constitution because her abjuration spells heavily rely on Laerryn's ability to maintain concentration. For this reason, she also picked up the War Caster feat. Aabria was proud that she chose the Eldritch Adept feat that let Laerryn learn an abjuration spell Mage Armor and cast it at will, which worked really well with her Arcane Ward, refilling it every time she cast Mage Armor.[94] Brennan let players choose all the magic items they wanted, but Aabria got distracted and forgot to take this opportunity, picking up only three items.[95] She also planned to combine the plus two mace and All-Purpose Tool into a single item and give it a special name, but did not get to it.[96] The idea for Laerryn and Loquatius being ex-spouses was proposed by Sam Riegel, the player for Loquatius.[97] While the cast was discussing their characters, Sam mentioned that he always wanted to play a character who was divorced from another member of the party, and Aabria Iyengar immediately volunteered to play his ex-wife.[98] By the finale of Exandria Unlimited: Calamity, they had not worked out anything about how Laerryn and Loquatius met beyond having met through work.[14] Appearances and mentions [edit | edit source]- Exandria Unlimited: Calamity - "Excelsior" (E3x01) (first appearance) - "Bitterness and Dread" (E3x02) - "Blood and Shadow" (E3x03) - "Fire and Ruin" (E3x04) - Campaign 3: Bells Hells - "The Sorrow of Molaesmyr" (3x57), mentioned only - "Escape From The Past" (3x58), mentioned only Quotations [edit | edit source]- "It's a mediocre mind that tries to replicate what's already been. Guest Party Members | Allies and Other Characters (Empire/Republic of Tal'Dorei) | Villains Guest Party Members | Allies and Other Characters (Dwendalian Empire | Xhorhas) | Villains The characters of Exandria Unlimited: Calamity. - Almighty Janitor: Downplayed; the Ring of Brass is comprised of a group of extremely influential people, but they are on paper much lower on the city's political totem pole than the Rings of Silver or Gold, who hold all the official political power. Even their name is intended to be a mocking reflection of this, according to Zerxus. They're nonetheless a group of level 14 mages and a Badass Normal, and while they may not run Avalir, they're the ones who keep it running. - Beyond the Impossible: In the final episode, the Ring of Brass do what even the Prime Deities were unable to do, and that is completely unmake two Primordial Titans. Of course, doing so resulted not only in the destruction of Avalir and Cathmoira, but also the entire continent of Domunas, leaving behind the Shattered Teeth of modern-day Exandria. - Contrasting Sequel Main Character: In comparison to the parties of the main campaigns as well as the Crown Keepers, who started out as a scrappy crew of misfits, each of the Ring of Brass are established figures with renowned careers and reputations. This is shown by their character levels as well - while most of the parties started out at Level 1-3note , the Ring of Brass are shown to be at Level 14. - Doomed by Canon: It's in the name of the show - the Calamity is imminent; no one's making it out unscathed. - Establishing Character Moment: - Zerxus wakes from a nightmare of the Calamity to come, in which he sees his dead husband and a younger form of his son, followed by Pelor curb-stomping Asmodeus—with Avalir as the curb—and he implores Pelor to stop. - Loquatius is all glitz and glamor as he broadcasts the morning news. He then has a Walk and Talk with his assistant and feigns modesty when met outside the office by a swarm of fans. - Laerryn is running the arcane engine that keeps Avalir aloft, dispensing with pleasantries with each interruption that demands her attention. When she receives astronomical estimates for a coming apogee, she quickly retires to her chambers to weep—only for her ex-husband Loquatius to appear at the door, and they snipe at each other until they remember their wedding at the last Replenishment. - Nydas is managing a multitude of preparations for the Replenishment at the Vault of the Golden Scythe—parades, inventories, meeting schoolchildren—all without missing a jovial beat...even when a Magister requests a brief audience with him and begins asking questions he shouldn't be far too comfortably. - Cerrit investigates a recreation of the recently-missing Archmage Vespin Chloras's sanctum, and spots irregularities very few would have noticed (with an Investigation check of 31). Sensing something very off, he asks the other investigator working the case to keep all further developments between the two of them. - Patia, with unfailing composure, meets with an Archmage of the Septarions beneath a statue of her grandfather, then talks hush-hush politics with his apprentice, expressing disdain when the topic of the gods comes up. - Hold the Line: The objective of the group's final battle is to hold off Vespin Chloras and his minions long enough for Laerryn to use the Astral Leywright to banish the Primordials.
