--- [Mohamed Zahafi - Wikipedia] ---
Mohamed Zahafi (born 1959) is a Moroccan middle distance runner who specialized in the 800 metres.
In 1983 he won bronze medals in both 800 and 1500 metres at the Maghreb Championships. Both races were won by Saïd Aouita. In the same year Zahafi competed at the 1983 World Championships, but without reaching the final. He also finished fifth in the 1500 metres at the 1983 Mediterranean Games.
He became Moroccan champion in the 800 metres in 1981, 1982 and 1983, and in the 1500 metres in 1983.
His personal best time was 1.46.38 minutes, achieved in June 1983 in Lausanne.

Mohamed Zahafi (born 1959) is a retired Moroccan middle-distance runner who specialized in the 800 metres and 1500 metres events.[1]His personal best in the 800 metres is 1:46.38, achieved in Lausanne on 30 June 1983, while his 1500 metres best is 3:39.92, set in Firenze on 13 June 1984.[1]Zahafi's notable achievements include winning bronze medals in both the 800 metres (1:50.0) and 1500 metres (3:41.0) at the 1983 Maghreb Championships in Casablanca.[2]He also secured a bronze in the 1500 metres (3:41.0) at the 1986 Maghreb Championships.[2]Internationally, Zahafi competed at the inaugural 1983 World Championships in Athletics in Helsinki, where he ran 1:49.43 in the 800 metres heats.[3]Additionally, he placed fifth in the 1500 metres with a time of 3:40.92 at the 1983 Mediterranean Games in Casablanca.[4] Early Life and Background Birth and Family Mohamed Zahafi was born in 1959 in Morocco.[1]Little is publicly known about Zahafi's family background or early personal life.
