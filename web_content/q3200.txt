Arthur Wallis Myers CBE (24 July 1878 - 17 June 1939) was a British tennis correspondent, editor, author and notable player. In Major tournaments he was a quarter finalist in the men's doubles at the 1908 Wimbledon Championships, and mixed doubles semi finalist at the World Hard Court Championships in 1914.

In Major tournaments he was a quarter finalist in the men's doubles at the 1908 Wimbledon Championships, and mixed doubles semi finalist at the World Hard Court Championships in 1914. He was active from 1903 to 1931 and contested 9 career finals, and won 1 singles title. Myers later became one of the leading tennis journalists of the first half of the 20th century. Tennis Career [edit]He played his first singles event at the 1903 Derbyshire Championships. He was a finalist at the Derbyshire Championships in 1908. He won his one and only singles title in 1909 at the Worthing Open against Albert Goward Goodwin. In 1912 he was a losing finalist at the French Riviera Championships where he lost to British international Roy Allen, and the same year a finalist at the Côte d'Azur Championships where he was beaten by French player Max Decugis. In 1913 he reached the finals of the Italian Riviera Championships in Sanremo where he lost to Roy Allen. In 1914 he was finalist at the Championships of Hyères where he lost to fellow compatriot Gordon Lowe. In 1922 he reached his final singles event at the Carlton Club de Cannes Championship where he lost a close match against Swiss player Charles Aeschlimann. He played his final singles event at the 1928 Cannes Carlton International. His last team event was as part of the International Club of Great Britain team in a match against the All-India Team in 1931. Myers was active mainly in doubles. In April 1906 he won the doubles title with Tony Wilding at the Barcelona International Lawn Tennis Competition. He won the Monte-Carlo Championships doubles event in 1910 and 1921 and the mixed doubles in 1909. Wallis Myers played in the mixed doubles at the 1914 World Hard Court Championships with Phyllis Satterthwaite, reaching the semi-finals, and teamed up with Norwegian American player Molla Mallory at the 1923 Monte Carlo tournament. In 1923 he won the doubles title of the South of France Championships. He was the captain of British tennis teams on tours undertaken in Europe, South Africa and India.
