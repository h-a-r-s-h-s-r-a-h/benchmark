--- [Dal Lake - Wikipedia] ---
Dal (Urdu pronunciation: [ɖəl] ; Kashmiri pronunciation: [ɖal]) is a freshwater lake in Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir in Indian-administered Kashmir. It is an urban lake, the second largest lake in Jammu and Kashmir, and the most visited place in Srinagar by tourists and locals. It is integral to tourism and recreation in the Kashmir Valley and is variously known as the "Lake of Flowers", "Jewel in the crown of Kashmir" or "Srinagar's Jewel". The lake is also an important source for commercial operations in fishing and water plant harvesting.
The shore line of the lake, about 15.5 kilometres (9.6 mi), is encompassed by a boulevard lined with Mughal era gardens, parks, houseboats and hotels. Scenic views of the lake can be witnessed from the shore line Mughal gardens, such as Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh built during the reign of Mughal Emperor Jahangir, and from houseboats cruising along the lake in the colourful shikaras. During the winter season, the temperature can sometimes reach as low as −11 °C (12 °F), freezing the lake.
The lake covers an area of 18 square kilometres (6.9 mi2) and is part of a natural wetland which covers 21.1 square kilometres (8.1 mi2), including its floating gardens. The floating gardens, known as "Raad" in Kashmiri, blossom with lotus flowers during July and August. The wetland is divided by causeways into four basins; Gagribal, Lokut Dal, Bod Dal and Nigeen (although Nigeen is also considered as an independent lake). Lokut Dal and Bod Dal each have an island in the centre, known as Rupa Lank (or Char Chinari) and Sona Lank respectively.
At present, the Dal and the Mughal gardens on its periphery are undergoing intensive restoration measures to fully address the serious eutrophication problems experienced by the lake. Massive investments of approximately US$275 million (₹ 11 billion) are being made by the Government of India to restore the lake to its original splendour.

Dal Lake is the shimmering jewel of Srinagar, often called the “Crown of Kashmir.” Spread over vast waters with the Zabarwan mountains in the backdrop, this freshwater lake is the heartbeat of the city. For centuries, poets, travelers, and kings have praised its beauty, and even today it remains the most iconic attraction of the Kashmir Valley. A trip here is never complete without a peaceful ride on its traditional Shikaras or a memorable stay in a houseboat. That’s why Dal Lake is considered an essential highlight in many Kashmir Tour Packages. Dal Lake has been at the center of Kashmir’s cultural and political history. During the Mughal era, emperors like Jahangir and Nur Jahan chose the lake’s surroundings as their summer retreat. This is where some of the grand Mughal gardens such as Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh were designed, reflecting a blend of Persian and Kashmiri styles. Beyond its royal associations, the lake represents the soul of Srinagar. The floating vegetable gardens, locally called “Rad,” are unique to the region and highlight the deep connection locals share with the water. For the Kashmiri people, Dal Lake is not just scenery; it is a way of life. Families live in houseboats, shopkeepers run floating markets, and fishermen continue age-old traditions. Its cultural and spiritual significance makes it more than a destination—it’s a living heritage. Dal Lake sits in the heart of Srinagar city, bordered by the mighty Zabarwan Range on one side and the Pir Panjal peaks in the distance. The lake spreads over an area of nearly 15–26 square kilometers, comprising multiple basins and channels. Its crystal-clear waters mirror the snow-capped mountains, creating postcard-perfect reflections at sunrise and sunset. Surrounding the lake are lush Mughal gardens, terraced lawns, and blooming Chinar trees. Each season paints a new picture—spring brings vibrant blossoms, autumn covers the lake with golden leaves, while in winter, parts of Dal Lake freeze, offering a surreal charm.
