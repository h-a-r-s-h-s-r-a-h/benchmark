--- [Thomas C. Hart - Wikipedia] ---
Thomas Charles Hart (June 12, 1877 – July 4, 1971) was a four-star admiral in the United States Navy, whose service extended from the Spanish–American War through World War II. Following his retirement from the navy, he served briefly as a United States Senator from Connecticut, becoming the highest-ranking military official ever to serve in Congress.

Supported by Baldwin Names Admiral Hart To Fill Maloney Term in Senate; Baldwin Names Admiral Hart To Fill Maloney Term in Senate February 9, 1945, Page 1Buy Reprints HARTFORD, Conn., Feb. 8- Appointment of Admiral Thomas Charles Hart, a registered Republican of Sharon, Conn., to be a United States Senator until November, 1946, was made this afternoon by Gov. Raymond E. Baldwin. Admiral Hart was Commander in Chief of the United States Asiatic Fleet at the time of Pearl Harbor. He is a member of the Navy's General Board. Governor Baldwin's appointment of Admiral Hart followed his signature on a bill empowering him to fill the Senate vacancy. Mr. Baldwin last Monday had said in a radio address that Admiral Hart was "his choice." Following the naming of the admiral, Governor Baldwin said: "Connecticut has in Admiral Hart an able, conscientious and patriotic American for its Senator in the Congress." Governor Baldwin telephoned Admiral Hart in Washington. He said he expects to confer with the admiral in Hartford next week after he is released from his present Navy Department duties. The appointing bill as permitted by the Seventeenth Amendment to the Constitution was a Senate substitute and provides that Admiral Hart's term will end as soon as the successful Senatorial candidate at the November, 1946, election is duly certified and qualified. The general understanding is that any interim appointee to succeed Democratic Senator Maloney, who died two years before the expiration of his elective term would not be a Senatorial candidate in 1946. In the State Senate the Democratic leader, Leon Riscassi of Hartford, who claimed “vindication" because Governor Baldwin had announced his probable appointee in advance, said Congresswoman Claire D. Luce's alleged statement that she suggested Admiral Hart's name to Governor Baldwin was almost the "kiss of death" because of a deluge of protests against anyone whom Mrs. Luce endorsed. Born June 12, 1877, in Genessee County, Michigan, Admiral Hart was appointed to the Naval Academy in 1893. He served as a commander of the Pacific Fleet submarine forces in the first World War, and was promoted to a rear admiral in 1919. He became Commander-in-Chief of the Asiatic Fleet on July 25, 1939. For his services President Roosevelt presented to him the gold star in place of a Second Distinguished Service Medal, which he won in the First World War. Admiral Thomas C. --- [Thomas Charles Hart :: Notable Graduates :: USNA] ---
Thomas Charles Hart Member of Congress Class of 1897- Senator - Connecticut(D), 1945-1946 Profile Thomas Charles Hart, a Senator from Connecticut, was born in Davidson, Genesee County, Mich. on June 12, 1877. He attended various public schools in Michigan and graduated from the United States Naval Academy, Annapolis, Md., in 1897. He served in the Regular Navy from graduation until 1945. He was then placed on the inactive list as an admiral (retired). His naval career covered service afloat during the Spanish-American War and both World Wars. Upon retirement, he settled in Sharon, Connecticut. He was appointed as a Republican to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Francis T. Maloney and served from February 15, 1945, to November 5, 1946. He was not a candidate for election to the vacancy in 1946. He resided in Sharon, Connecticut, until his death there on July 4, 1971, at which point he was interred in Arlington National Cemetery, Fort Myer, Va.
