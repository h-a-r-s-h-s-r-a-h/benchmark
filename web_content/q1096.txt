--- [Shastri Nagar metro station] ---
Shastri Nagar metro station is an elevated station on the Red Line (Line 1) of the Delhi Metro network, situated in the Shastri Nagar neighborhood of North Delhi, India, with the postal code 110052.[1] Opened on 3 October 2003, it features two side platforms and provides convenient access to the nearby Sarai Rohilla railway station, facilitating connectivity for commuters in the densely populated northern suburbs.[2][1]The station operates daily from approximately 5:30 AM to 11:30 PM, accommodating the Red Line's route spanning 33.48 km from Rithala to Shaheed Sthal (New Bus Adda), with 29 stations in total.[1][3] It was originally named Vivekanandapuri upon inauguration and renamed Shastri Nagar to reflect the local area, reflecting the Delhi Metro Rail Corporation's (DMRC) early expansion efforts in Phase I of the network.[1] The station's two entry/exit gates enhance accessibility: Gate 1 leads to the Government Boys Senior Secondary School and Sarai Rohilla railway station, while Gate 2 connects directly to the Shastri Nagar residential and commercial zones.[1]Key facilities at Shastri Nagar include available parking, escalators, elevators for divyangjan (persons with disabilities), and modern automated ticketing systems, making it a user-friendly hub for daily commuters, students heading to Delhi University North Campus, and travelers linking to broader rail services via Ring Road.[1] Its strategic location has boosted local real estate and economic activity, with the station handling significant footfall as part of the Red Line's role in alleviating traffic congestion in North Delhi.[4] Overall, Shastri Nagar exemplifies the DMRC's commitment to efficient urban transit, contributing to the metro's status as one of India's busiest rapid transit systems.[3] Overview Location and Accessibility Shastri Nagar metro station is situated at coordinates 28°40′12.48″N 77°10′54.43″E in North Delhi.[5] Its full address is E2/202, Shri Balaji Medical Ltd Road, Block C, Subhadra Colony, Shastri Nagar, New Delhi, Delhi 110052.[6]The station lies between Inderlok and Pratap Nagar stations on the Delhi Metro Red Line, providing seamless connectivity to these adjacent stops.[1] It is approximately 1 km from Sarai Rohilla Railway Station, a walkable distance that facilitates intermodal transfers for commuters arriving by train.[7] Local bus routes, including DTC services such as 108, 166, 181, and 39, connect directly to the station, enhancing access from surrounding areas.[8] The station is also near major roads like the Ring Road, supporting easy vehicular approach.Shastri Nagar is a residential neighborhood in North Delhi, characterized by a mix of housing, educational institutions, and commercial spaces.[9] It features several schools, such as Maharaja Agarsain Public School and DAV Public School, along with nearby markets including those in Kamla Nagar and Karol Bagh for daily shopping needs.[10] The area includes hospitals, banks, and other amenities, serving a diverse local population.[11]For accessibility, the station provides ramps at entry and exit points, as well as elevators and escalators to accommodate passengers with reduced mobility.[1] Parking facilities are available on-site for private vehicles.[12] History The Shastri Nagar metro station, originally planned under the name Vivekanandapuri, was renamed prior to its opening to reflect the surrounding locality of Shastri Nagar, which honors former Indian Prime Minister Lal Bahadur Shastri. This change addressed local demands for alignment with established neighborhood nomenclature, as highlighted in community appeals during the planning phase.[13][14]As part of Delhi Metro's Phase I, construction of the station began alongside the broader project in 1998, with specific work on the elevated structure progressing in the early 2000s. The station, an elevated facility with two side platforms, formed a key segment of the Red Line's westward extension from Tis Hazari to Inderlok, covering 4.87 km and including four stations. Engineering efforts focused on minimizing disruptions in the densely populated North Delhi area, though specific challenges like pillar erection over arterial roads were typical of Phase I elevated viaducts. The section, including Shastri Nagar, officially opened on 3 October 2003, marking a significant expansion of the network just months after the initial Red Line segment's launch. Inaugural operations commenced with standard gauge trains, serving initial passenger flows from nearby residential and institutional zones.[15][1]Post-opening, the station underwent renovations as part of a comprehensive DMRC initiative starting in July 2019 to upgrade 21 Red Line stations between Dilshad Garden and Rithala. By March 2021, infrastructure enhancements at Shastri Nagar included improved facades, modernized electrical systems, and enhanced passenger amenities to boost efficiency and aesthetics. No major incidents have been recorded specific to the station through 2025, with ongoing maintenance ensuring operational reliability.[16] Infrastructure Station Layout Shastri Nagar metro station is an elevated structure equipped with two side platforms and two tracks, designed to facilitate efficient passenger flow on the Red Line of the Delhi Metro.[1][6]The station follows a multi-level configuration typical of elevated Delhi Metro facilities: platforms are situated on Level 2 (L2), the concourse level—including fare control, ticket counters, and access points—on Level 1 (L1), and the ground level (G) handles street-level entrances and exits.[6]Platform assignments direct passenger movement effectively; Platform 1 accommodates trains bound for Rithala, with Inderlok as the subsequent station, while Platform 2 handles services toward Shaheed Sthal, followed by Pratap Nagar.[1][17]Electrification at the station utilizes a 25 kV 50 Hz AC overhead catenary system, standard for the Delhi Metro network to power train operations reliably.[18] Facilities and Amenities Shastri Nagar Metro Station, an elevated facility on the Delhi Metro's Red Line, provides essential amenities to support passenger convenience and safety. The station includes public toilets available for use by commuters, along with drinking water facilities and small convenience stores for basic needs.[19] Waiting areas are designated within the paid and unpaid zones to allow passengers to rest during waits, complemented by clear signage and information desks staffed by personnel for queries and assistance.[1]For banking services, ATMs from Punjab National Bank and State Bank of India are located inside the station premises, enabling cash withdrawals without leaving the facility.[6][20] Ticketing options encompass automatic vending machines for single-journey tokens, smart card recharges at counters and machines, and integration of contactless payments via the National Common Mobility Card (NCMC), UPI-based QR code tickets through apps like Paytm, PhonePe, and Amazon Pay, as well as WhatsApp booking.[21][22][23]Security features include an extensive CCTV network covering entry points, platforms, and concourses, with over 24,000 functional cameras across the Delhi Metro system as of 2025, monitored in real-time. Help points and emergency communication buttons are installed throughout the station for immediate assistance, supported by Central Industrial Security Force (CISF) personnel.[24][25] As of 2025, no major new passenger amenities like station-wide Wi-Fi or mobile charging stations have been added specifically at Shastri Nagar, though EV charging facilities with 10 connectors are available near the station for electric vehicle users.[26] Operations Line and Services Shastri Nagar metro station is integrated into the Delhi Metro's Red Line (Line 1), which spans 34.55 kilometers from Rithala in northwest Delhi to Shaheed Sthal (New Bus Adda) in Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh, serving 29 stations in total.[27][28] This line provides key interchanges at stations such as Kashmere Gate (for Yellow and Violet Lines), Welcome (for Pink Line), Inderlok (for Green Line), and Netaji Subhash Place (for Pink Line).The station operates as part of the Red Line's daily schedule, with services running from 5:15 AM to 11:22 PM on weekdays and Saturdays, and from 7:00 AM to 11:00 PM on Sundays and holidays, as of the latest 2025 data.[1]Train frequency on the Red Line, which includes Shastri Nagar, varies by section and time of day; in the Dilshad Garden to Rithala segment, peak hours (weekdays) see trains every 3 minutes 30 seconds, while off-peak hours have intervals of 4 minutes 10 seconds, with slight adjustments on weekends.[29] No major frequency updates have been implemented on this line as of September 2025.[29]For inter-line connections, passengers at Shastri Nagar can access the Green Line via the adjacent Inderlok station, one stop away toward Rithala. The Yellow Line can be accessed via interchange at Kashmere Gate.[1] Passenger Usage In January 2015, Shastri Nagar metro station recorded an average daily ridership of 17,006 passengers, translating to approximately 527,195 passengers per month.Ridership at the station has followed the broader recovery and growth trends of the Delhi Metro network post-pandemic, with overall daily journeys rising from 4.09 million in 2022 to 5.56 million in 2023, driven by urban expansion in North Delhi and improved connectivity.[30] By 2024, the network achieved record highs, including 7.867 million journeys on a single day in November.The station plays a key role in the local transport network by linking residential neighborhoods in North Delhi, such as Shastri Nagar and adjacent areas, to employment hubs like Connaught Place and commercial districts, as well as educational institutions including the Government Boys Senior Secondary School.[4] This connectivity has spurred economic impacts, including a 90% increase in rental demand since 2018 and property appreciation of 2.8% annually, with average rates reaching ₹5,450 per square foot, fostering retail growth and job opportunities in the vicinity.[31]Peak usage occurs during morning and evening rush hours, primarily serving a diverse commuter base of students heading to nearby schools and universities like Delhi University, and professionals commuting to central business areas.[4] To address potential overcrowding, the station employs measures such as CCTV surveillance, dedicated security personnel, and infrastructure enhancements like expanded platforms and multiple entry/exit gates with escalators and lifts for smoother flow.[31]
