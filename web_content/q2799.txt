Carla Hall (born May 12, 1964) is an American chef, television personality, and former model.
She appeared in the fifth and eighth seasons of Top Chef, Bravo's cooking competition show. She was a co-host on The Chew, a one-hour talk show centered on food from all angles, which premiered on ABC in September 2011.

Felicia Perretti History and Background Carla was born in Nashville, Tennessee, and grew up surrounded by soul food and Southern cuisine. After graduating from Hillsboro High School, Carla packed her bags for Washington, DC, and attended the prestigious Howard University, where she earned an accounting degree. She became a Certified Public Accountant shortly after graduation, but after a couple of years behind a desk, she decided corporate America wasn’t her calling. She pivoted from crunching numbers to strutting catwalks and spent her mid-20s as a working model in Paris, Milan and London. She enjoyed diverse cuisine during her modeling years, but ultimately, she yearned for the flavors of home. In an interview with Jessica B. Harris for Garden & Gun, Carla shares, “Paris ended up being the bridge between what I knew I didn’t want to do and what I eventually wanted to do. “I was homesick. And it was wanting that connection to home that got me into cooking.” She returned to the States and started her culinary journey by going door to door selling sandwiches to local offices and salons. After hustling for five years, Carla decided to pursue formal culinary education at L’Academie de Cuisine in Maryland. She was 30 years old at the time. After graduation, Carla’s culinary chops led her to snag the sous chef position at the Henley Park Hotel in Washington before working as executive chef at The Washington Club and The State Plaza Hotel. She eventually opened a Maryland-based catering company, Alchemy Caterers, in 2001 before making her big television break in 2008 on Bravo’s Top Chef: New York. She captivated audiences with her warm, quirky personality and impressed big names like Jacques Pépin and Emeril Lagasse with her standout dishes. She returned to compete in Top Chef: All-Stars Season 8 and earned the coveted title of “Fan Favorite.” In 2011, Carla worked as one of the five co-hosts on ABC’s The Chew. She was nominated for a Daytime Emmy Award three times while on The Chew and ultimately brought home the trophy for Outstanding Informative Talk Show Host in 2015. She remained in her role there until its final season in 2018. She began working with Food Network in 2015, serving as a judge on Halloween Baking Championship Season 1. From there, Carla became a friendly face on Food Network as the host and judge of several shows. Family Life Carla lives in Washington, DC, with her husband of nearly 20 years, Matthew Lyons.
