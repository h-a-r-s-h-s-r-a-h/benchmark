--- [Rare (Selena Gomez album) - Wikipedia] ---
Rare is the third studio album by American singer Selena Gomez, released by Interscope Records on January 10, 2020. As the executive producer, she worked with many producers, including Ian Kirkpatrick, Jason Evigan, Mattman & Robin, Sir Nolan, Simon Says, The Monsters & Strangerz and David Pramik. Described by Gomez as her "diary from the past few years", Rare is a midtempo pop and dance-pop record, taking cues from electronic, Latin pop and R&B styles. Lyrically, the album explores themes of self-love, self-empowerment, self-acceptance, and self-worth. Guest features on Rare are from singer 6lack and rapper Kid Cudi.
The album was promoted by two singles prior to its release: The lead single "Lose You to Love Me" was released on October 23, 2019. The song topped the Billboard Hot 100 and became Gomez's first number-one single in the United States. "Look at Her Now" was released a day after the lead single, peaking at number 27 on the chart. Coinciding with the album release, the title track was made the third single, peaking at number 30 on the Hot 100. Rare received positive reviews from music critics, who praised its production and cohesiveness, with many calling it Gomez's best album to date. The album debuted at number one in Argentina, Australia, Belgium, Canada, Estonia, Lithuania, Mexico, Norway, Portugal, Scotland, and the United States. Rare has since been certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) for sales of over 1 million equivalent units in the United States.
A Target-exclusive edition of Rare additionally includes five of Gomez's standalone singles released in 2017–2018: "Bad Liar", "Fetish" featuring Gucci Mane, "It Ain't Me" with Kygo, "Back to You", and "Wolves" with Marshmello. The vinyl edition of Rare adds "Feel Me" as a bonus track. A deluxe edition with three new tracks, including the third single "Boyfriend", was released on April 9, 2020.

Rare is the third studio album from American recording artist Selena Gomez, released on January 10, 2020 via Interscope Records. The album marks her second release from Interscope Records following 2015’s Revival. On June 10th, 2019, Gomez confirmed the album is “finally done.” On June 12th, 2019, she said the following about the album in an interview on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon: I have to do a few finishing things with it but I’m just relieved. It took me four years now to even feel at a good place with this album. And it’s just because I had such huge moments that happened in my life personally that ‘how was I going to capture that?’ and ‘how was I going to actually feel good about what I was saying?’ So, I just kept going, and I’m relieved. She revealed in the same interview that her music will always contain a “sense of strong pop.” However, with this album she explored more with electric and acoustic guitar and a more soulful sound: It all kind of hits different places that I feel like is my lane of music. On December 12th, 2019, Gomez announced the album’s title, track-list, and cover art via her social media. The Target bonus edition of the album will also feature Gomez’s previous hit releases: “Bad Liar” “Fetish,” “It Ain’t Me,” “Back to You,” and “Wolves.” The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart with 112,000 equivalent album units earned in the U.S. in the week ending Jan. 16, according to Nielsen Music. The album marks her third solo album to peak at number one. On December 12, 2019, Selena took to social media to announce the title, cover art, and tracklist of the album. She captioned her post: Can’t believe I’m revealing the art and title for my new album Rare, out January 10th. It’s the most honest music I’ve ever made and I can’t wait for you to hear my heart. Yes! It was revealed by Selena in a January 8, 2019, interview with People that Taylor Swift has heard a few songs prior to the album’s release: I remember Taylor [Swift] said when I played her some of the new songs, “I feel like I’m seeing who you were before [you were struggling with some mental health issues].” That makes me happy. I like feeling like that girl again. In a tweet shared by Spotify: Honestly, this album was kind of a nightmare to do it, but in the best way possible. Because it evolved each year, and then something would happen to me and it wouldn’t be very drastic so that it would take me somewhere else. And then all of a sudden I found this healing, and I saw something bigger then what I thought that I was, and I think that also added the whole end of the album. So you can really kind of hear sparatic all of the music notions and the track-list is based on that. Um… I feel like you can hear where I’m at emotionally each song. I don’t feel I performed them all the same way, they all meant something to me. Um, it was a long process but I do have to say that it was so worth it! Becaues I truly think this is my best album.
