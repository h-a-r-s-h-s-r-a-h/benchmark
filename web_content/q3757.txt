Major Disaster (Paul Booker) is a fictional character appearing in American comic books published by DC Comics. He started out as an enemy of Green Lantern and the Justice League before reforming and becoming a superhero.

Major Disaster Major Disaster (real name Paul Booker ) is a former antagonist from the DC Comics universe, who ended up becoming a reluctant anti-hero. He debuted in Green Lantern (volume 2) #43. Biography [ ] Paul Booker was Green Lantern's former friend and confidant. Accidentally activating a hidden control panel, he discovered Kalmaku's casebook on Green Lantern. Using this, he found the secret identities of both Green Lantern and the Flash. Armed with his new powers, he set out to kill the crime fighters, but was defeated and was thought to have died in the ensuing explosion. In the original BATMAN AND THE OUTSIDERS series,a terrorist organization paid him to attack Station Markovia--The Outsiders headquarters in an oil facility at sea;in international waters. A strong explosive charge became trappped INSIDE Disaster's Force Field,causing a massive explosion that destroyed the facility.The Outsiders survived,Disaster's fate was unknown. Metamorpho also referred to him as "Minor Disaster",which he didn't like very much.However, the Injustice League were given a chance by Maxwell Lord, the then-chief of the JLI. They somehow managed to stop a terrorist attack and were subsequently sent to the South Pole to form the Justice League Antarctica, along with other nuisances G'nort and the Scarlet Skier. The team was short lived as Disaster destroyed their embassy whilst defeating a waddle of killer penguins. The team, though lacking an embassy, stayed together, acting as bodyguards for Maxwell Lord when he was in a gunshot-induced coma. They also joined the American and European Leagues, the team called the Conglomerate, and the intergalactic bounty hunter Lobo in pitched battle against Despero. Major Disaster, in an effort to stop Despero, turned The team then received an offer from the government to work on their Suicide Squad. Major Disaster agreed, even "volunteering" Multi-Man against his will. They were sent to a small island off the coast of Iceland, to deal with a terrorist situation and an out-of-control genetic experiment. Lifeless, explosive genetic constructs who looked like kids killed Big Sir. The rest of the 'children' were shrunk by Multi-Man, who had that power in his current incarnation. Multi-Man was shot through the head soon after, but in the past he has come back from worse. Unfortunately, Clock King, who had no such powers, was shot repeatedly. In the resulting chaos, Major Disaster perceived that Cluemaster saved his life at least twice, before also being shot dead. Cluemaster survived his injuries, but with multiple scars. Disaster stayed with the Squad until Superman gave him a chance to prove his heroic worth in defeating a number of Superman's foes. Booker answered a general call for superheroes by Oracle. Dozens gathered, all to stop the Secret Society of Super Villains from attacking Metropolis. The confrontation, known as the Battle of Metropolis, led to many fatalities on both sides. A character that resembles Major Disaster perished; in an interview DC editor-in-chief Dan DiDio named the character killed as Boss Moxie. Booker, however, has not subsequently appeared since Infinite Crisis #7. However, an artist's rendering of a Black Lantern Major Disaster appeared in the Blackest Night: Director's Cut one-shot. Powers and Abilities [ ] Major Disaster's powers originally came from a set of weapons he used to cause natural disasters. Over time, exposure to these weapons made the powers internalize and now Disaster can cause chaos at will. His deal with Neron greatly enhanced his powers, enabling him to see the strands of chaos around him. The disasters he can now control theoretically range from being able to command asteroid showers without breaking a sweat to causing heart attacks, nose bleeds, and even making people trip up. He appears to have no real limitations, though he lacks fine control now. He has devised a force field for himself, using his powers to redirect attacks, though he seems not to use it much. While Major Disaster is adept at creating chaos, it takes him great effort to stop disasters, whether his own or natural. --- [Major Disaster (disambiguation) | DC Database | Fandom] ---
Major Disaster is a super-villain and enemy to Green Lantern with the ability to cause chaos and natural disasters. This involves probability manipulation. His career began as a cheap criminal who hired scientists to develop high-tech weapons, although he later internalized their effects and gained super-powers. Later in his career he became a hero when Maxwell Lord invited him to join the Justice League. He has been a member of the Army of Crime, the Injustice League, the Justice League of America, Justice League Antarctica, Justice League Elite, SKULL and the Suicide Squad. Eventually he was killed by Superboy-Prime during Infinite Crisis at the Battle of Metropolis. Major Disaster was created by Gardner Fox and Gil Kane, first appearing in Green Lantern (Volume 2) #43. --- [Major Disaster (Character) - Comic Vine] ---
Origin The son of an orthodontist, Paul Booker was a small time crook with a police record. On the run from the law he found an unlocked apartment window, and slipped inside to hide from the pursuing police. By chance, he had chosen the home of Thomas Kalmaku, the former friend and confidant of Green Lantern ( Hal Jordan). Furthermore he accidentally activated a control for a hidden panel, revealing Thomas' Green Lantern casebook. Upon reading the volume, Booker discovered the secrets behind both Green Lantern and the Flash ( Barry Allen). Using the knowledge to his advantage, Booker adopted the costumed identity of Major Disaster. Creation Major Disaster is a DC character created by Gardner Fox and Gil Kane. His first appearance was in Green Lantern #43. Character Evolution Major Disaster went from being a petty thief to a formidable for of many superheroes, at first setting his sights on Green Lantern and the Flash but focusing more on the former due to his first incarceration. When he regenerated after a blast which should have killed him, he no longer relied on sophisticated devices and went further to achieve more power at the cost of his soul. After assisting the Justice League in warding off a natural disaster which would have killed people on the island he was at, Major Disaster attempted to reform himself but failed, only truly succeeding when Batman trusted him enough to replace the JLA should something happen to them on a mission. Unfortunately, Major Disaster was killed only to be resurrected as a Black Lantern and ultimately defeated. Major Story Arcs Major Disaster immediately hired a group of brilliant criminal scientists to fashion devices that could create disasters. He then set about to expose the secret identities of the two heroes, but they quickly overcame him, accidentally causing an explosion in the process that seemingly killed Disaster. His device had scattered his atoms around the room due to the explosions and months later they coalesced, returning him to normal. Wanting to prevent it from happening again, his group of scientists managed to create a device which would protect Disaster from any destruction he created. Seeking revenge against Green Lantern, Disaster used the knowledge of GL's Hal Jordan persona but was defeated a second time. However, Disaster tried to reveal Jordan's secret to the police but discovered the power ring commanded him to be unable to say Jordan's name aloud. Disaster tried to internalise his disaster causing power as it slowly destroying him. He tried to transfer the increasing power surplus into a super hero, choosing Superman but he failed and Superman imprisoned him. Months later, Disaster sought revenge against the emerald gladiator and was frustrated to find John Stewart now wielding the ring. Disaster nearly destroyed portions of Los Angeles before being driven seemingly insane by John Stewart, who made Disaster think that everyone was actually Green Lantern. Leader of a version of the Injustice League, Major Disaster later joined his fellow teammates as members of the Justice League of Antarctica, which proved to be short lived. He would later return to villainy until he was recruited into the Suicide Squad, teamed with his fellow 'Leaguers' once again. He was believed to be the sole survivor of this mission until both Cluemaster and Multi-Man showed up alive. Disaster would later join the JLA in a group Batman placed together in the unforeseen circumstance that the core team became incapacitated and didn't return from Atlantis . He would later join the splinter group Justice League Elite. Major Disaster was last seen alive during Infinite Crisis during the Battle of Metropolis where Superboy-Prime snapped his neck and killed him. Re-animated, he joined the Black Lantern Corps during the Blackest Night but was defeated by Superboy-Prime who destroyed him and his fellow Black Lanterns by cycling powerful emotions through the use of a black ring. New 52 Superman/Wonder Woman Major Disaster battles Superman and Wonder Woman and adds the two heavy weights to his rogues gallery. Dawn of DC Green Lantern Major Disaster has received some Angorian alien tech and is wreaking havoc on downtown Los Angeles. That is until Flash and Green Lantern show up and overpower the powered-up villain. Powers and Abilities Booker originally relied on sophisticated equipment created by rogue scientists that could cause natural disasters but over time he 'internalized' the power they generated and no longer required them. During Underworld Unleashed Major Disaster made a deal with Neron, increasing his powers at the cost of his soul. He became able to see and control the causal strands of chaos around him and at this power level he could do little things like causing heart attacks. Disaster easily created earthquakes as well as tsunami waves and meteor showers but had a difficult time battling against them when called on to do so.
