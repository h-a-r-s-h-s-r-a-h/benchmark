The International 2019 (TI9) was the ninth iteration of The International, an annual Dota 2 world championship esports tournament. Hosted by Valve, the game's developer, the tournament followed a year-long series of awarding qualifying points, known as the Dota Pro Circuit (DPC), with the top 12 ranking teams being directly invited to the tournament, which took place in August 2019 at the Mercedes-Benz Arena in Shanghai. In addition, six more teams earned invites through regional qualifiers played in July 2019. The grand finals took place between Team Liquid and OG, who had respectively won the International's 2017 and 2018 events. There, OG defeated Team Liquid 3–1 in the best-of-five series to become the first-ever repeat champion of an International.
As with every International from 2013 onwards, the prize pool was crowdfunded by the Dota 2 community via its battle pass feature, with the total at over US$34 million. In addition, various related activities were also held during the event, such as an all-star game and cosplay and submitted short film contests. The event had over a million concurrent viewers during the grand finals on the livestreaming platform Twitch.

The International 2019 was the ninth iteration of Dota 2's flagship annual championship. Hosted by Valve Corporation, it took place at the Mercedes-Benz Arena in Shanghai, China, being the first to be celebrated in Asia. The tournament began on 3 July 2019 with the Open Qualifiers and closed on 25 August 2019 with the Grand Finals, where OG successfully defended their title by defeating 2017 champions Team Liquid 3-1, becoming the first team to win two championships. With a prize pool of over US$34.3 million, The International 2019 was at the time the largest tournament to ever take place in eSports, breaking the record briefly held by the Fortnite World Cup 2019, which awarded $30 million. It was eventually surpassed by The International 10. The majority of the money awarded during the event is collected through several in-game items released by Valve, including the The International 2019 Battle Pass, a digital tournament pass and features bundle tied to the event that included many activities and cosmetic items. 2019 marks the fifth consecutive year that The International's prize pool outgrows its previous-year record. However, it is the first year the record is held by a non-Dota 2 tournament, albeit briefly. The International 2019 became the third consecutive event won by a European team. The event also saw several new records being set, and some old records being broken. OG's back-to-back victory is the first-ever in the event's history. Their win also made Europe the region with the most tournament wins, with 4 titles. The team also became the first one to return to the Grand Finals, and win a second time, with the original champion roster. Natus Vincere, Newbee and Team Liquid had experienced roster changes before they reached the Grand Finals again. OG also became the first champion since Natus Vincere to make it to the Grand Finals the following year. Team Liquid became the first team to run through the entire Lower Bracket, with the most number of series won during the Main Stage. Their victory against PSG.LGD in the Lower Bracket final meant that two previous champions would meet in the Grand Finals, killing the chances of a new team winning the championship. The International 2019 saw an increase in the number of previous champions attending the event. Natus Vincere and Alliance came back after two years without qualifying. Only two champions did not make it: 2012 winners Invictus Gaming, who fell during the regional qualifiers, and 2016 winners Wings Gaming, who disbanded shortly after winning the championship. In the Group Stage, teams are divided into two groups of nine and compete in a best-of-two round robin tournament. The top four teams (in green) advance to the upper bracket of the main event, the lower four teams (in yellow) advance to the lower brackets, and the team in last place (in red) is eliminated. The Main Event is a double-elimination tournament with the top four teams from each group starting in the upper bracket, whilst the bottom four teams start in the lower bracket. The International 2019 Background Announcement and Development Valve announced The International 2019, the ninth edition of its annual Dota 2 championship, on April 26, 2019, confirming the event dates as August 20 through 25 in Shanghai, China.[9] This iteration marked a significant evolution in the tournament's structure, building on previous years by integrating it as the culminating event of the 2018-2019 Dota Pro Circuit (DPC) season, the second season of Valve's structured professional league introduced in 2017 (with the inaugural season being 2017-2018) to organize global competition through a points-based system.[10][11] The DPC emphasized regional representation and consistent qualification pathways, with points accumulated from majors and minors determining direct invites, thereby streamlining the path to The International compared to earlier open qualifiers and wildcards.[12] On May 7, 2019, Valve detailed further developments via an official blog post on the Dota 2 website, launching the Battle Pass 2019 as the primary crowdfunding mechanism.[13] This digital compendium allowed players to purchase levels and earn rewards, with 25% of all sales directly contributing to the event's prize pool, a model refined from prior years to boost funding while introducing new in-game features like the Wrath of the Mo'rokai game mode and terrain customizations.[14] The announcement highlighted the event's role in concluding the DPC season, noting that only one final round of majors and minors remained to finalize qualification standings.[13] Preparatory developments centered on the DPC's five majors, which served as key qualifiers and tested evolving formats such as flexible rosters and linked minor-to-major pipelines.[10] Tournaments like the Stockholm Major in March 2019 exemplified this, awarding substantial DPC points to top performers and fostering a competitive ecosystem leading into The International.[15] These events not only built hype but also refined Valve's approach to global esports governance, ensuring The International 2019 represented the pinnacle of a season-long narrative. The Battle Pass sales rapidly elevated the prize pool beyond $30 million, underscoring the community's role in scaling the event's impact.[14]Location and Schedule The International 2019 was hosted at the Mercedes-Benz Arena in Shanghai, China, marking the first occasion the tournament was held in the country.[4] This multi-purpose indoor arena, located in the Pudong New Area, provided a state-of-the-art venue for the event, with a seating capacity of 18,000 spectators.[16] The arena's configuration for the tournament included a custom main stage setup adorned with Dota 2-themed elements to immerse attendees in the event's lore.[1] The tournament schedule spanned from August 15 to August 25, 2019, without a traditional play-in or wildcard stage; instead, all 18 qualified teams proceeded directly to the group stage.[4] The group stage took place over four consecutive days, August 15–18, at a separate closed-door location in Shanghai, with matches beginning daily at 10:00 China Standard Time (CST) and continuing in multiple sessions to accommodate the round-robin format across two groups.[17][2] Following the group stage, August 19 served as an off-day, allowing teams time for travel, recovery, and preparation before the playoffs commenced.[4] The playoffs, or main event, unfolded from August 20 to 25 at the Mercedes-Benz Arena, featuring a double-elimination bracket with varying match lengths (best-of-one for the initial lower bracket round, best-of-three for most others, and best-of-five for the grand finals).[18] Daily sessions typically started around 11:00 CST early in the week, shifting to 10:00 CST later, with multiple matches per day to advance through the bracket; no additional off-days were scheduled during this phase to maintain event momentum, culminating in the grand finals on August 25.[1] This structure ensured a compact yet intensive competition timeline, balancing high-stakes gameplay with logistical needs for international participants.[4]Qualification Dota Pro Circuit Invites The Dota Pro Circuit (DPC) was established by Valve in 2017 for the 2017–2018 season as the structured professional league for Dota 2.[19] For the 2018–2019 season, which determined qualifications for The International 2019, it comprised five majors and five minors designed to award points based on team performance.[12] These tournaments provided a global competitive pathway, with minors featuring eight teams and majors 16 teams, ensuring broad participation while prioritizing high-level play.[10] All qualifying teams earned points regardless of final placement, promoting consistent merit across the season.[20] Direct invites to The International 2019 were granted to the top 12 teams in the final DPC points rankings, establishing this as the elite qualification route ahead of open regional events.[21] The system emphasized accumulated performance, with majors distributing 15,000 points total—awarding 4,950 to the winner and decreasing progressively (e.g., 3,000 for second place, 2,100 for third)—while minors offered 500 points overall to reward top finishers on a smaller scale.[22] This structure favored teams with sustained success, as points carried over the entire season without reset. For instance, Team Secret led the standings with 14,400 points, securing the top invite.[22] On July 1, 2019, Valve officially revealed the 12 direct invitees based on these rankings, including prominent teams such as OG, PSG.LGD, and Evil Geniuses.[23] The list encompassed: Team Secret, Virtus.pro, Vici Gaming, Evil Geniuses, Team Liquid, PSG.LGD, OG, Fnatic, Ninjas in Pyjamas, TNC Predator, Alliance, and Keen Gaming.[21] This points-driven selection highlighted the DPC's role in identifying the season's strongest contenders, with the remaining six spots determined via regional qualifiers.[2]Regional Qualifiers The regional qualifiers for The International 2019 provided six additional slots to the tournament, one for each of the six designated regions: China, Europe, Southeast Asia, North America, South America, and the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS). These closed qualifiers followed open qualifiers and were designed to ensure diverse regional representation, with teams that had already secured direct invites through the Dota Pro Circuit ineligible to participate, preventing overlaps.[4][24] The qualifiers took place online from July 7 to July 14, 2019, immediately after the open qualifiers concluded on July 6. Each regional event featured 8 teams, consisting of a mix of directly invited teams and teams that advanced from open qualifiers; the number of open-qualifier slots varied by region, competing in a best-of-one round-robin group stage where the top four advanced to a double-elimination playoff bracket. Playoff matches were best-of-three, except for the grand finals, which were best-of-five, determining the regional representative. This format allowed for competitive depth while efficiently selecting a winner within a tight schedule.[25][26][27] The winners were Royal Never Give Up from China, who defeated CDEC Gaming 3-2 in the grand final; Mineski from Southeast Asia, defeating Team Jinesbrus 3-2; Chaos Esports Club from Europe, securing victory over The Final Tribe 3-0; Natus Vincere from CIS, topping Winstrike Team 3-0; Infamous from South America, beating paiN Gaming 3-0; and Forward Gaming from North America, who won 3-1 against J.Storm. However, Forward Gaming's organization disbanded shortly after qualifying on July 21, 2019, leading to their roster being signed by Newbee, who competed under the Newbee banner at the event while retaining the North American slot.[25][26][27][28][29][30][31] This qualification pathway highlighted emerging talent from outside the professional circuit, fostering global competition and allowing underdog teams to challenge established DPC invitees at The International.Teams Participating Teams The International 2019 featured 18 professional Dota 2 teams, comprising 12 invited through the Dota Pro Circuit (DPC) points system and 6 qualified through regional qualifiers.[4] These teams represented a diverse global field, with the largest contingent from Europe (6 teams), followed by China (4), Southeast Asia (3), the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS, 2), North America (2), and South America (1).[4] The invited teams included established powerhouses based on their DPC performances during the 2018-2019 season.
