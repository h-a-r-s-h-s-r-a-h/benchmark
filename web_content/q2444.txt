--- [Ronald R. Blanck - Wikipedia] ---
Lt. Gen. (Ret.) Ronald Ray Blanck, D.O. (born October 8, 1941) was the 39th Surgeon General of the United States Army, from 1996 to 2000.  He is a Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (D.O.) and is the only such physician ever appointed Surgeon General of the Army. He was also president of the University of North Texas Health Science Center at Fort Worth from 2000 to 2006. He is the former chairman of the board of regents of the Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences.

--- [Lancaster County native, Army's newest 3-star general, sworn in as top ...] ---
Ronald R. Blanck wants all soldiers to be healthy soldiers. As the country’s newest three-star general and head of the U.S. Army’s medical division, Blanck has responsibility for the health and well-being of military personnel and beneficiaries. "We’re working very hard to make sure our soldiers stay healthy," Blanck, who was born in Manheim and raised in Ephrata, said during a recent telephone interview. "Deploying healthy forces is priority No. 1." Blanck received his third star and was sworn in as the 39th Surgeon General of the Army during a ceremony in Washington, D.C., last week. The promotion was conducted by General Dennis Reimer, Chief of Staff of the Army and Blanck’s immediate superior. Following a White House announcement on Sept. 9, Blanck was confirmed for the post by the Senate on Sept. 19. He is one of only about 30 three-star generals in the Army, one of fewer than 75 in the U.S. Armed Forces. Public affairs officer Virginia Stephanakis said there is only one three-star general in the Army’s medical department. "The surgeon general is the top doc," she said Monday. Blanck said he’s approaching the new post "with great excitement and optimism and not a little bit of apprehension, given the tremendous scope and the challenges we face." The job requires Blanck to serve as chief medical adviser to the Army’s chief of staff, and to oversee medical care, training, research and development, and other functions of the Army’s medical wing. The Army medical division comprises 50,000 medical personnel, he said. "We have literally millions of beneficiaries who are eligible for our care," he added. A 1959 graduate of Ephrata High School, Blanck earned a bachelor of science degree from Juniata College in 1963 and received his doctorate in osteopathy from Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine in 1967. He interned at the former Lancaster Osteopathic Hospital, now the Community Hospital of Lancaster, in 1967-68. He was drafted into the Army in 1968 and sent to Vietnam as a general medical officer. He began his military career as battalion surgeon with the 5th Battalion, 22nd Artillery. Blanck oversaw more than 20,000 doctors, nurses and technicians in the Persian Gulf War as the Army Surgeon General’s chief of professional services. He was installed as commander of the 1,000-bed Walter Reed Army Medical Center in 1992. A 1986 graduate of the Army War College in Carlisle, Blanck has also headed Army hospitals in Frankfurt and Berlin, Germany. In Berlin, he cared for former Nazi leader Rudolf Hess, and he declared Hess dead after he hanged himself at Spandau Prison in 1987 at the age of 93. His awards and decorations include the Defense Superior Service Medal, the Legion of Merit, the Bronze Star Medal and the Meritorious Service Medal (1st Oak Leaf Cluster). Blanck and his wife, Donna, live in Washington, D.C. Although his new position also gives him command of the U.S. Army Medical Command, headquartered at Fort Sam Houston, Texas, Blanck said he plans to remain in Washington. The Medical Command operates eight medical centers, nearly 30 community hospitals and various research and teaching institutions worldwide. The Army medical corps is exploring "different ways of providing health care," Blanck noted. "I really think we can serve as a model for society as we’re serving the soldier."


--- [Lt. Gen. Ronald Ray Blanck, D.O. | auroramri] ---
Lt. Gen. Ronald Ray Blanck, D.O. Chairman and Senior Partner of Martin, Blanck & Associates Dr. Blanck currently serves as chairman and senior partner of Martin, Blanck & Associates which is a leading healthcare consulting company. Dr. Blanck received his Bachelor of Science from Juniata College in 1963, and a Doctorate of Osteopathic Medicine from the Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine in 1967. He served for 32 years (1968-2000) in the US Army, culminating with his selection as the Army Surgeon General. Throughout his Army career, Dr. Blanck held a variety of administrative, academic and clinical positions, including: commander of Walter Reed Army Medical Center and North Atlantic Regional Medical Command; Director of Professional Services and chief of Medical Corps Affairs in the Office of the Surgeon General; Chief of the Department of Medicine at Brooke Army Medical Center; and service as a Battalion Surgeon during the Vietnam War. Dr. Blanck joined the University of North Texas Health Science Center in 2000 after his retirement from the U.S. Army and served as president until 2006. He has also held a variety of academic appointments at USU, the University of Texas Health Science Center at San Antonio, Howard University, George Washington University and Georgetown University. Dr. Blanck’s military honors include Distinguished Service Medals, the Defense Superior Service Medal, the Legion of Merit, the Bronze Star Medal, Meritorious Service Medal and Army Commendation Medals. In 2000, the American Medical Association honored Dr. Blanck with its highest award for government officials, the Dr. Nathan Davis Award. He is a master and past governor of the American College of Physicians. Additionally, he is a member of the American Medical Association, American Osteopathic Association and the Association of Military Surgeons of the United States. He serves as Chairman of the Education Commission on Foreign Medical Graduates. Dr. Blanck also joined the boards of Delsite, Inc, Noblis, Inc, Trauma Cure, Inc and The Annapolis Center for Science-Based Public Policy.
