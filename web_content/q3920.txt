"The River" is a poignant track by the Canadian punk band Nomeansno, featured on their 1993 album "Why Do They Call Me Mr. Happy?" The song delves into the complexities of life, using the metaphor of a river to illustrate the struggles and existential dilemmas faced by individuals. The song encapsulates the themes of despair, helplessness, and the inevitability of fate, portraying life as a turbulent river where one must navigate through challenges and confront their own limitations. The lyrics of "The River" unfold like a narrative of introspection and existential contemplation. The river serves as a powerful metaphor for life itself, representing the unpredictable currents and the overwhelming nature of existence. The opening lines introduce the listener to the concept of being 'thrown' into life, echoing Jean-Paul Sartre's philosophy of existentialism, where individuals find themselves in a world that is indifferent to their struggles. This imagery sets the tone for the song, suggesting that life is a journey fraught with challenges that one must navigate alone. As the song progresses, the speaker reflects on their own failings and the inability to connect with others. The lyrics convey a deep sense of sorrow and resignation, as the speaker grapples with the realization that they cannot save others from their own despair. This theme of helplessness is further emphasized through the imagery of drowning, which symbolizes the overwhelming nature of life's challenges. The river becomes a place of isolation, where the speaker feels adrift and unable to reach out for help. The repeated refrain of drifting away reinforces the feeling of loss and surrender to the flow of life. It highlights the inevitability of fate and the futility of trying to control one's circumstances. The somber mood of the song is palpable, as the speaker confronts the harsh realities of existence and the weight of their past mistakes. The call for help, juxtaposed with the acknowledgment of their own limitations, creates a poignant tension that resonates throughout the track. Ultimately, "The River" serves as a reflection on the human condition, exploring the themes of despair, isolation, and the struggle against the current of life. The river, as a metaphor, encapsulates the journey of existence, where individuals must confront their own demons and navigate the turbulent waters of their experiences. The song leaves the listener with a sense of introspection, urging them to reflect on their own journey and the inevitability of facing life's challenges head-on.


--- [Why Do They Call Me Mr. Happy?] ---
Why Do They Call Me Mr. Happy? is the sixth full-length album by Vancouver punk rock group Nomeansno. Released in 1993, it is their second album recorded by the original two-piece lineup of brothers Rob and John Wright following Mama (1982), and first after the departure of longtime guitarist Andy Kerr. Here the band mostly replaced its hardcore punk sound with slower songs influenced by heavy metal and progressive rock.  The album was well-received by critics and praised for its balance of heaviness and subtlety, showcasing the dynamics of the band in its original incarnation.
