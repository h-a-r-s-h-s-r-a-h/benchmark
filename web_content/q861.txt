--- [Saurav Ghosal bags Squash Player of the Year award - Times of India] ---
MUMBAI: Current World No 14 Saurav Ghosal has been named Squash Player of the Year at the third Mahindra Scorpio Times of India Sports Awards (TOISA) powered by All-Out on Monday. The 31-year-old was adjudged the top Indian squash player of 2017 by the esteemed TOISA jury comprising Abhinav Bindra, Bhaichung Bhutia, Devendra Jhajharia, Reeth Abraham and Gautam Gambhir along with the Chief Editor of Times Internet Ltd, Rajesh Kalra. The other nominees were Harinder Pal Sandhu, Joshna Chinappa and Mahesh Mangoankar. While Sandhu won more titles last year, what swayed the jury's votes was the fact that Ghosal participated in events where the level of competition was stronger. The experienced squash player lifted his seventh international title by beating Swiss Nicolas Mueller 11-6, 11-8, 11-8 to win the $50,000 JSW-CCI International Squash Circuit, and won the National Squash Championship. He also reached the final of the Hong Kong FC International having stunned top seed Annie Hu in the semi-finals and the semi-finals of the Montreal Open and Wimbledon Club Squash Squared Open. Ghosal also made it to the third round of the PSA World Championships, the only Indian to move past round one. Sandhu, ranked 49th in the world, enjoyed his best year as a professional squash player, winning four successive PSA World Tour titles from May to July 2017 - the Malaysian Tour Leg 9, the Nissan Makati Open, the South Australian Open and the Victorian Open. The World No 17 Chinappa was also consistent last year, winning the Asian Championship by overcoming Dipika Pallikal Karthik, with whom she paired up with to claim bronze at the WSF World Doubles Championship. She also reached the quarter-finals of the Women's World Squash Championship where she lost to second seed Camille Serme. The 23-year-old Mangaonkar, ranked 64th in men's singles, was runner-up at the New Zealand International Classic squash tournament and the North of Scotland Open and registered India's only win of their 1-2 loss to Malaysia at the WSF World Team Men's Championship in Marseilles.
