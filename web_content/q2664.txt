Pachavita is a town and municipality in the Neira Province, part of the Colombian department of Boyacá. The urban centre is situated on the Altiplano Cundiboyacense at an altitude of 1,985 metres (6,512 ft) and at a distance of 85 kilometres (53 mi) from the department capital Tunja. The municipality borders Chinavita in the north, Tenza and La Capilla in the south, Garagoa in the east and Úmbita in the west.

Pachavita shares its borders with other towns: Chinavita to the north, Tenza and La Capilla to the south, Garagoa to the east, and Úmbita to the west. Contents What Does the Name Pachavita Mean? The name Pachavita comes from the Chibcha language, which was spoken by the native people of this area. The word pacha means "man" or "lord," and vita means "point," "summit," or "peak." So, Pachavita can mean "Peak of the man." Another possible meaning is "Proud chief." A Look Back in Time: Pachavita's History Before the Spanish conquistadores arrived in Colombia, the area where Pachavita is now was part of a large group of native communities called the Muisca Confederation. The Muisca people had different leaders, and the zaque (a powerful ruler) of Hunza was in charge of the Pachavita area. The modern town of Pachavita was officially founded on November 17, 1716. How Pachavita Makes a Living: Economy The main ways people in Pachavita earn money are through agriculture (farming) and dairy farming. Farmers grow many different crops, including yuca, maize (corn), bananas, arracacha, beans, lulo, and cucumbers. They also produce eggs, peas, and cheese. See also In Spanish: Pachavita para niños

Last modified: 2021-08-26 by klaus-michael schneider Keywords: boyaca | pachavita | Links: FOTW homepage | search | disclaimer and copyright | write us | mirrors See also: Pachavita is a municipality in the Colombian Department of Boyacá, part of the the subregion of the Neira Province. It was founded on 17 November 1796. The flag of Pachavita is 2:3 in ratio, vertically divided green-white-green and is charged with a tree ("chizo"). According to the description, the tree should have nine branches for the nine "veredas" of this municipality, but it isn't seen as such in the image. The flag was adopted by the municipal council's decree No. 012 of 24 November 1982 (full text of the decree is in the municipal web site) and it was designed by José Luis Huertas. Source: municipal web site Dov Gutterman, 18 September 2008 Relevant parts of the Decree (translated, skipping the administrative prescriptions): "Article 2. The dimensions and colours shall be as follows: Stripes: The flag shall be made of three vertical stripes: The first, vert (green) shall cover one third of the flag, the second, central, white shall also cover one third of the flag. The third remaining part shall also be made of vert (green). a) In the middle of the white part shall be placed the design of a tree, representing a "chizo"*, the emblematic tree of our municipality and from the main trunk of which radiate nine branches representing the nine rural hamlets ("veredas")** prescribed by the territorial division. b) The length of the flag shall be the vertical dimension added with a third part and the same dimensions as for the national flag shall be adopted. Colours: a) Argent (white), means the requirement to protect the orphans and their future to preserve peace [...] b) Vert (green), means the requirement to help the workers and peasants who demonstrate faith, sincere friendship, service, repect and hope in better times, outlined by the beauty and fertility of their fields. Article 3. It is recommended to the administration, educational bodies and other bodies of the community to hoist the municipal flag on all national and local holidays." José Luis Huertas signed ("firmado") the Decrees prescribing the flag and coat of arms, but is not explicitely listed as the designer of the symbols. * "chizo", a.k.a "chizo zapote", is the sapodilla, Manilkara zapota. ** Aguaquiña, Buenavista, Centro, Guacal, Hatogrande, Llanogrande, Pie de Peña, Sacaneca and Soaquira. Ivan Sache, 29 September 2008 image from municipal web site
