--- [Squash(sport) - Wikipedia] ---
Squash, sometimes called squash rackets, is a racket sport played by two (singles) or four players (doubles) in a four-walled court with a small, hollow, rubber ball. The players alternate striking the ball with their rackets, directing it onto the playable surfaces of the four walls of the court. The object of the game is to hit the ball in such a way that the opponent is not able to play a valid return. There are about 20 million people who play squash regularly worldwide in over 185 countries. The governing body of squash, World Squash, is recognized by the International Olympic Committee, and the sport will be included in the Olympic Games, starting in the 2028 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles. The Professional Squash Association (PSA) organizes the pro tour.

--- [AsianSquashChampionships2025: Velavan Senthilkumarwins...] ---
Asian Squash Championships 2025: Velavan Senthilkumar bags bronze medal after semi-final loss This was the Indian squash player’s second successive medal at the continental showpiece, having won silver at the preceding edition two years ago. India’s Velavan Senthilkumar settled for a bronze medal at the Asian Squash Championships 2025 after losing to second seed Alex Lau Tsz Kwan of Hong Kong China in the semi-finals in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia on Friday. The 45th-ranked Indian squash player, who had not dropped a game en route to the final four, went down 3-1 (9-11, 11-13, 11-5, 6-11) to the world No. 41 Alex Lau in a hard-fought encounter. Despite the defeat, the result confirmed Velavan Senthilkumar’s second consecutive medal at the continental showpiece. Both losing semi-finalists are awarded bronze at the continental showpiece. In the previous edition in 2023, Velavan Senthilkumar returned with a silver medal after losing to Malaysian squash player Ng Eain Yow in the final. Senthilkumar earlier breezed past his opponents in straight games, beating the People’s Republic of China’s Li Haizhen, Japan’s Tomotaka Endo and Malaysia’s Joachim Chuah in the lead-up to the semi-finals. Senthilkumar, who was handed a bye in the opening round, was the only Indian squash player to make it past the quarter-finals in Kuala Lumpur. Diwarkar Singh and Rahul Baitha bowed out in the men’s round of 32, while Suraj Chand reached the pre-quarterfinals. In the women’s draw, Pooja Arthi Raghu, Akanksha Salunkhe and Shameena Riaz all suffered second-round exits. Saurav Ghosal remains the only Indian man to win a gold medal at the Asian Individual Squash Championships, having clinched the title in 2019. Joshna Chinappa won back-to-back women’s crowns in 2017 and 2019. India’s teenage star Anahat Singh did not compete in the individual Asian Championships this year, but will feature in the doubles tournament scheduled to begin next week.
