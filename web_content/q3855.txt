3T (Things Take Time) is Youngsta’s debut album, it took him a while to put this project together hence the title “Things Take Time”. On the album, Youngsta narrates, through himself and his grandfather, the history and lived experiences of a Cape Colored person. The colored identity has always been complicated, his grandfather attempts to provide some clarity on “Kleurling”. In South Africa, the Colored population has always been stuck between the Black population and the White population. It has been this way since the time of colonialism, it continued into Apartheid and is still evident today. The Colored population, like the White population, are in the minority in the country. However, they are in the majority in the Western Cape and many argue, including Youngsta’s grandfather, that the Colored folk is indigenous to the Western Cape, as the Khoi and San were. Youngsta’s message is that after decades of ill-treatment and displacement, due to colonialism and apartheid, the Colored population are starting to carve out a meaningful existence. However, the community should remember that “Things Take Time” and therefore the Colored population in South Africa has a long way to go. This shit is a bit more serious than the other music I’ve released. I’m turning over a new chapter in my career. I’m no longer just going to make fun, party music. We are not driven to learn about our history, because it’s painful. There are things going on that shouldn’t be happening in 2019. I wanted to understand and break this cycle. The only way I was going to understand the cycle was to go back into the past. I waited five years to release this album. I made 30 mixtapes in the interim but I waited five years, wondering if people are going to understand and respect it. Now we’re at the point where things are so bad in SA, I don’t care if they understand, people need to hear this.
