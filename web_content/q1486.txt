--- [Searing Totem - Wowpedia - Your wiki guide to the World of Warcraft] ---
Summons a Fire Totem with 5 health at the feet of the caster for 60 sec that repeatedly attacks an enemy within 0 to 25 yards for (20% of spell power) Fire damage. When dropped, the Searing Totem will randomly select an enemy within 25 yards of it and continuously attack that target until A.) the Searing Totem expires, B.) the target leaves its 20-yard radius, or C.) the target dies. If B or C occur, the totem will randomly choose another target in range and begin attacking it. If there are no targets within range, it will do nothing until an enemy comes in range. Also note that the totem will only engage hostile targets on its own; it will ignore neutral targets, unless you engage them first. Reliable source of extra damage, and is useful in situations where the other damage totem ([Magma Totem]) isn't and/or when the [Fire Elemental Totem] is unfeasible. It is useful primarily in single-target, prolonged fights (e.g. boss fights). You should avoid using it in situations where it could accidentally hit a target that shouldn't otherwise be hit (such as something that has been CC'd). Very useful in PvP for an extra bit of consistent damage. Most players will ignore the totem, knowing it will go away when you die and not wanting to take the time to target it. Always useful in a boss fight, assuming the boss doesn't have adds which get CC'd. Most of the time, if you drop the totem while you are in melee, it will start out attacking the target you have selected. This may be a function of closeness, or may be a function of who you have selected at the time. It is suspected that it simply picks the closest target when it doesn't have one. Limitations[] The totem of has only 5 hp, making it trivial to destroy. Dangerous to use when it can break a CC effect, or when it may pull an as-yet-unpulled group of creatures. Trivia[] Searing Totem is one of the four totems that have a chance to be summoned by a Shaman's "Totemic Call" Hero Power in Hearthstone. Patch 6.0.2 (2014-10-14): Searing Bolt now has a 2-second cast time (up from 1.5 seconds), but casts faster with Haste, and its damage has been increased by 120%. Patch 5.0.4 (2012-08-28): No longer prefer to target enemies that are afflicted by Flame Shock or Stormstrike effects. Patch 3.0.8 (2009-01-20): Searing Totem (Rank 4) now does the proper damage for its rank.

--- [It has been almost six years since Searing Totem has been removed from ...] ---
It has been almost six years since Searing Totem has been removed from WoW, yet it's still displayed in shaman character selection Searing totem and other shaman abilities were removed in the Legion pre-patch (7.0.3) on July 19th, 2016. This was a small part of a slew of drastic changes to each class in WoW. Many have debated over the years as to whether these changes, which have paved the way for how most specs play today, have had a net benefit or detriment to the game. I, for one, am of the boat that improving the gameplay style of a class should take precedence over the roleplay aspects of the class. However, I believe that it's a strange thing to have one of the core identities of a class present in character selection, but not present in the actual game itself. As an Enhancement Shaman player in BFA, there was a tier 2 talent I could take called "Totem Mastery" which would summon a fire totem along with three other totems. I rarely used this personally as another option in that tier was typically stronger. Outside of that talent, I did not have access to any fire totems from Legion's pre-patch to now. Searing Totem almost came back in Shadowlands but was removed before release with the following explanation, "Searing Totem was brought back in Shadowlands with the intention of Totems being a cool and iconic part of the Shaman class, but without significant redesign to the button to give it mechanical interaction and feeling impactful to press, it wound up feeling like just another cooldown you had to manage that didn’t have any excitement behind it". I'm happy that they chose to prioritize gameplay here, but I wish they kept trying to rework the ability to make it fit smoothly into a shaman's rotation. As it appears Searing Totem won't be coming back, why not make the totem in character selection a water totem, given how Healing Stream Totem is a baseline spell for all shamans? At least that way, I won't be baited into thinking I have access to a removed ability.
