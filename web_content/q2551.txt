[Perkin Prize for Organic Chemistry - Wikipedia]
The Perkin Prize for Organic Chemistry is a prestigious award established in 2008 by the Royal Society of Chemistry for sustained originality and achievement in research in any area of organic chemistry. The prize is named after Sir William Henry Perkin (1838-1907), inventor of the first aniline dye, and is awarded on a biennial basis.
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perkin_Prize_for_Organic_Chemistry

[2017 Royal Society of Chemistry Perkin Prize for Organic Chemistry]
2017 Royal Society of Chemistry Perkin Prize for Organic Chemistry - Awarded for pioneering contributions to the synthesis and applications of complex catenanes, rotaxanes and molecular knots that underpin the field of artificial molecular machines.
