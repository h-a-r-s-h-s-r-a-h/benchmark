[Cochiquito Volcanic Group - Wikipedia]
The Cochiquito Volcanic Group is a small volcanic group of volcanoes north of the town of Buta Ranquil in Argentina. The main peak is Volcán Cochiquito, a stratovolcano of estimated Pleistocene – Holocene age. There are eight satellite cones in the volcanic field, including Volcán Sillanegra (a pyroclastic cone complex) and Volcán Ranquil del Sur (a small stratovolcano).

Last updated: February 2026 Argentina 36.77 S, 69.82 W summit elevation 1945 m (Volcán Sillanegra) Stratovolcanoes Geology and Volcanology The Cochiquito volcanic group consists of several small stratovolcanoes in northwestern Argentina, located north of Buta Ranquil. The highest point is Volcán Sillanegra at 1945 m, followed by Cochiquito stratovolcano at 1435 m and Volcán Ranquil del Sur. The group is part of the Andean back-arc volcanism and shows no evidence of fumarolic, thermal, or seismic activity in modern times. No historic eruptions are recorded. The volcanoes are monitored indirectly through regional networks by relevant authorities. The Cochiquito group is of interest for understanding small-scale stratovolcanoes and back-arc volcanism in the central Andes. Features of Cochiquito volcanic group Cochiquito stratovolcano 1435 m.
