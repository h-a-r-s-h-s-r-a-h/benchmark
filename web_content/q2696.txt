--- [P-Model - Wikipedia] ---
P-Model (also typeset as P-MODEL and P. Model) was a Japanese electronic rock band started in 1979 by members of the defunct progressive rock band Mandrake. The band has experienced many lineup revisions over the years but frontman Susumu Hirasawa was always at the helm of operations. P-Model officially disbanded in 2000, although many of its members continue to release solo albums and collaborate with each other on different projects.
Hirasawa has since released work under the name "Kaku P-Model" (核P-Model; lit. 'nucleus of P-Model'), effectively a solo revival of the band.

--- [r/SusumuHirasawa on Reddit: A breakdown of ‘big body’ — 10th album by P-Model] ---
A breakdown of ‘big body’ — 10th album by P-Model I've never done anything like this, so if you feel like this 'essay' isn't cohesive enough, that's probably because this is. I decided to make this post as a reply to u/oMINDSPINo, who was interested in learning more about big body concept and the context around the album, and instead of making a private reply / answering him in the thread that will probably no one will check in the near future, I thought of making a separate post, so more people will chime in with their opinions or pieces of knowledge. I'll start with the general overview of the album following by analyses of the songs, quoting lyrics and translation available on either Hirapedia and / or phonon-belt journal, which is, to my knowledge, the only sources, apart from the album's Wikipedia article, available in English. big body strongly follows P-MODEL, self-titled 9th album by the band, in its sci-fi inspired lyrics and techno-pop oriented sound. But with a catch! Where P-MODEL was just a bunch of tracks compiled together without any great concept (which, at the time, was already unusual for Hirasawa) and a relatively loose running theme (sci-fi (SPEED TUBE, GRID, GO AMIGO), rapidly evolving computer technologies (2D OR NOT 2D, STONE AGE), and self-awareness of tech (WIRE SELF, ERROR OF THE UNIVERSE), big body expands on these themes whilst also offering a unified concept roughly based on 1953 science fiction novel by Theodore Sturgeon More Than Human. The main work for the album started right after concluding the tour in support of P-MODEL, with BIG FOOT being debuted on 7th of December, 1992 and BURNING BRAIN on the gig day later. There was a recording of 7/12 concert on niconico with Hirasawa's MC before BIG FOOT, but it seems unavailable now, it would be nice if someone has it somewhere. In the February issue of Pico Entertainment magazine in 1993, shortly before the release of the album, Hirasawa said in an interview that he tried to give each individual member 'more space', since he was aware that it would be the last album by this lineup. The robotic humanoid figure featured on the cover and jacket of CD and LP is called ビッグ・ボディくん (BIG BODY-kun) and was by Hirasawa on his AMIGA computer. The words Nsetn · · · · Oirao Ivook written on the cover mean «kono ato revisION» (Revision after this), alluding to the fact that in November of that year a change of members would be announced. According to Akiyama, he wrote his two songs (JOURNEY THROUGH YOUR BODY, BURNING BRAIN) and BURNING BRAIN was the first song to be included in the upcoming album, however, JOURNEY THROUGH YOUR BODY was chopped in half (4 minutes instead of 8), removing long intro and Akiyama's vocals due to the close resemblance to YMO's Technodon's material. If you want to hear the song with the original lyrics, it is on the live album PAUSE. The full instrumental version was also available in 2016 for attendees of CROSS concert (Akiyama / Yoshikazu Takahashi's duo). Unlike Hirasawa, main inspiration for Akiyama's lyrics, especially JOURNEY THROUGH YOUR BODY, comes from the 1966 American film called Fantastic Voyage, which plot follows submarine crew that is reduced to microscopic size and journeyed into the body of an injured scientist to repair damage to his [burning] brain. Crammed in between two Akiyama's songs is 幼形成熟BOX (Neoteny Box), the only song by Kotobuki, and it is speculated to be a rework of 2D OR NOT 2D before ‘Hirasawa's treatment’, with original vocal melody and faster tempo. Lyrics are somewhat cryptic, at least for me, (I personally have no idea whatトランス トランスバケット バケット(trance, trance (or transformer, transformer), baguette, baguette (or bucket, bucket)) means and how they can be incorporated into the larger concept, but hey, if Hirasawa approved 'em they must be good, right? Now to Hirasawa's songs which form the main core of the album (70% of the output is his solo effort, to be exact) and their connection with the More Than Human, and bits of unnecessary trivia, as always. CLUSTER: the opening song on the album, starts with what Hirasawa arguably does best — long and loud HAYAAA, lyrically it is probably about the second part of More Than Human, where people with strong psychic abilities (telepathy, exceptional technology knowledge, teleportation, etc...) form a cluster (or, to put it simply, a team) which will later be called Homo Gestalt (ring a bell, huh?). Why not the first part of the book where they initially form their team? There's a line ‘SHE'S A CLUSTER‘ and a major female character with psychic abilities was introduced only in the second part called ‘Baby is Three’. I will try to spoil as little of the book's plot as possible because it is, firstly, quite short and secondly, is so much more than what Hirasawa utilised for his lyrics in big body. CHEVRON: Wikipedia entry for the album states that the title was inspired by the French car manufacturer Citroёn's logo "double chevron mark", because at the time, Hirasawa owned Citroёn XM and Xantia. I don't know how much of it is actually true and whether it was confirmed by Hirasawa himself at some point, but hey, that's a nice fact. Despite the probably automobile-inspired title, lyrics are about molecular biology, specifically the transition of DNA into RNA with electrons and translation of RNA into proteins in human's (species's (?)) systems. Here's the great translation on Hirapedia. BIIIG EYE: the ‘I'M SCREAMING IN THE LENS’ line was inspired by the Gene Kelly's performance of Singin' in the Rain on the film of the same name (confirmed by Hirasawa himself in a recent Tweet). The live version is somewhat slower and can be found on PAUSE live album. Lyrically this song's got nothing to do with the big body concept but features a number of Asian places (Taiwan, Tokyo, Hong Kong). Interestingly enough, there is now a building called BIG-I (pronounced BIG EYE) in Kōriyama, Fukushima Prefecture, but it was completed in 2001, long after the release of the album. BIG FOOT: there is little to go on with this song, it's not about Sasquatch, although Hirasawa doesn't mind if you think it is, it doesn't have anything to do with More Than Human, there is no mention of anything closely resembling content of the song in the book, and it's understandable because the book was written in 1953 long before the advancement of computer technologies... and, personally, I've always assumed that the lyrics of BIG FOOT describe the computer (or some kind of artificial intelligence) fetch-executing commands given by some kind of User, especially the 'STEP TO STOCK // STEP TO STACK TO STOP TO STOCK' and 「SPEED 倍 SPEED」(SPEED INCREASING SPEED) lines that sound like machine executing programs and Big Foot might be the username of the person that controls the machine but never appears to it, like some Japanese lines probably allude to (聴こえてる 憩える声 / I can hear the calming voice, ただオーラを微動だにせず届かせた / [Your] aura reaches me even without slightest movement). But this is my theory and you're free to disagree and offer your takes! 時間等曲率漏斗館へようこそ(Welcome to the House of Chrono-Synclastic Infundibulum): the context of this song is as hard to understand as to pronounce its name. I think this one again doesn't have anything with the overall concept of the album and concentrates on describing the mix of technological and divine and difference between them (mentioning of rationality, metaphysical and divine revelations / samadhi) and their coexistence in Japan's society and how everything's moving in loop. (again, this is my interpretation and it'd be nice if you offer some of yours) BINARY GHOST: this song was covered by Hajime Fukuma shortly after he joined the band, lyrically, again, this probably has nothing to do with More Than Human, with Hirasawa singing only BINARY GHOST over and over again whilst AMIGA voice says something indecipherable. My take is that this song again teases with computer intelligence and self-consciousness of tech, but I have no concrete proofs to expand on this theory HOMO GESTALT: the final song on the album, features a lot of Greek prefixes and finally enriches the album's lore and its connection with More Than Human, here's a nice translation. Both the book and the song describe the next step of human evolution (a gestalt organism), «psychic evolution instead of physical» where people connect their minds (their psychic abilities as well) into one large consciousness. The lyrics of the song, I believe, also add a physical elements to the Gestalt as well as intertwines it with CHEVRON describing DNA working and things like that. A fun fact: this song was voted for the rework by Hirasawa for KANGENSHUGI 8760 HOURS (凝集する過去 還弦主義 8760時間) project where he would rerecord P-Model's songs in his new, ‘orchestral‘ style, but was quickly rejected because of its lyrical resemblance to WIRE SELF which had been decided to be included earlier. Make of it what you will but perhaps some lyrics can be better understood if put into context with P-MODEL.
