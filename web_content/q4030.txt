--- [Arboletes- Wikipedia] ---
Arboletes is a town and municipality in the subregion of Urabá in Antioquia Department, Colombia.
The municipality is bounded by the Caribbean Sea to the north, the department of Córdoba to the north and east, the municipality of San Pedro de Urabá to the south and west, and the municipalities of Turbo and Necoclí. The town is 502 km from the city of Medellín, capital of the department of Antioquia. The municipality covers an area of 710 km².
Arboletes has 8 rural townships (corregimientos): El Carmelo, La Trinidad, Naranjitas, El Guadual, La Candelaria, Buenos Aires, Las Platas and Pajillal.

Located at the northernmost tip of Colombia’s Antioquia department, Arboletes is one of the best-kept secrets of the Colombian Caribbean. This coastal town features a therapeutic mud volcano, pristine beaches, ecological trails, and warm, welcoming locals. Ideal for ecotourism, relaxation, and reconnecting with nature, it’s a paradise for adventurers seeking unique experiences. Arboletes sits on the shores of the Caribbean Sea near the Cordoba border and boasts a distinctly Caribbean culture, cuisine, and climate. With its tropical landscapes and laid-back atmosphere, it’s the perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Places to visit in Arboletes, Colombia Arboletes Mud Volcano The mud volcano is the town’s main attraction and one of the few of its kind in Colombia. It’s a natural geological phenomenon where warm, thick mud bubbles up from beneath the earth’s surface. Rich in minerals, the mud is believed to have therapeutic properties for skin conditions, rheumatism, and muscle stress. Visitors can effortlessly float on its dense surface, then walk a few meters to the Caribbean Sea to rinse off. There are rustic changing rooms and showers on-site. How to get to Arboletes There are several ways to reach this destination from Colombia’s main cities: Getting there: From Medellín: The most common route is by road. The journey takes between 9 and 11 hours, and you can catch buses from the Terminal del Norte. Companies like Sotracor and Rápido Ochoa operate this route. You can also rent a car to enjoy the scenic drive through towns such as Santa Rosa de Osos, Yarumal, and Montería. From Montería (Córdoba): This is the closest city with an airport. The drive to Arboletes takes approximately 3 hours. From Cartagena or Santa Marta: You can get there by land, following the coastal route through Sincelejo and Montería, then taking a final connection to Arboletes. This is ideal if you’re exploring the northern coast of Colombia. Local cuisine The gastronomy of Arboletes reflects the rich culinary traditions of the Colombian Caribbean. Don’t miss out on trying fried fish with coconut rice and patacón (fried green plantain) — a regional classic. Also recommended are the many fried street snacks like empanadas, arepa de huevo (egg-filled corn cake), and arepa dulce (sweet corn cake). Tips for visiting Arboletes The best time to visit Arboletes is between December and March, when the weather is dry and sunny — perfect for enjoying its beaches and natural landscapes. The climate is tropical and warm year-round, so be sure to pack light clothing, a hat, sunscreen, and insect repellent. While Arboletes is generally a peaceful and safe town, it’s wise to avoid walking alone on deserted beaches at night for added safety. Although Arboletes is still relatively unknown to international tourists, it continues to surprise visitors with its authenticity, natural beauty, and the hospitality of its people.
