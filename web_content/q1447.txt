--- [Snatch Game - Wikipedia] ---
Snatch Game is a comedy challenge recurring across the Drag Race television franchise and a fixture of the reality competition series. Since the second season of the original American RuPaul's Drag Race series in 2010, the challenge has returned for every subsequent season. Typically arranged as a parody of Match Game (known as Blankety Blank in the UK, Blankety Blanks in Australia, and Jogo dos Pontinhos in Brazil), the challenge is a test of the contestants' skills at celebrity impersonation and improvisational comedy.
The challenge similarly recurs on various spin-offs, including All Stars and Secret Celebrity Drag Race, as well as the international adaptations for Thai, British, Canadian, Dutch, Australian-New Zealand, Spanish, Italian, French, Philippine, Belgian, Swedish, Mexican, Brazilian, and German audiences. 
Usually occurring midway through each Drag Race season, Snatch Game is widely considered among the most important and memorable challenges of the show and, in RuPaul's own words, separates "the basic bitches from the fierce-ass queens." Winners of the show are often amongst the top performers in the Snatch Game, though there are a few exceptions.
The drag queen contestants typically impersonate women, though several contestants choose male celebrities with sufficiently flamboyant public images to fit a drag aesthetic. Several contestants have chosen to impersonate other people directly associated with the show, such as other past or present contestants, Michelle Visage, or RuPaul. Queens cannot choose copyrighted characters, although some celebrity portrayals have been closely based on a specific screen performance.
The challenge often relies on special celebrity guests to participate in game play, who frequently double as the episode's guest judges. Other seasons feature the show's regular production team—Michelle Visage, Ross Mathews, Carson Kressley, Rhys Nicholson, or members of the Pit Crew—in lieu of outside guests.
Ginger Minj has won Snatch Game three times, the most of any queen in the franchise. Eight other queens have won Snatch Game twice: BenDeLaCreme, Baga Chipz, Jinkx Monsoon, Trinity the Tuck, Jimbo, Hannah Conda, Gottmik and Punani. Three queens have been eliminated twice for their Snatch Game performances: Gia Gunn, Onyx Unleashed and Cynthia Lee Fontaine.

--- ['Drag Race': Snatch Game winners and the celebs they impersonated] ---
When it comes to making RuPaul laugh, these queens understand the assignment. For several years, RuPaul's Drag Race queens have described the Snatch Game as the challenge that separates frontrunners from "filler queens." That long-standing perception has been falling flat over the years. In season 11, for instance, the top 2 queens of the entire season were the two worst performers in the Snatch Game and even had to lip sync for their lives that week — which led to a double shantay. There was also the disastrous Snatch Game of season 14, in which seven of the eight remaining queens bombed and prompted the next episode to be a Lip Sync Smackdown that could settle which of them would actually be eliminated. Yet, most Drag Race fans do remember how each queen did in the Snatch Game, and can often hold onto that perception of them based on how well or how poorly they did in the challenge. Generally speaking, losing the Snatch Game isn't a good look, while winning that challenge does propel a queen to be considered a real contender in the season. With the exception of Drag Race season 1 and All Stars 1, every single season of the original U.S. series has featured a Snatch Game. So, without further ado, let's take a trip down memory lane for an overview of Snatch Game winners on Drag Race and the celebrities they've impersonated. Tatianna as Britney Spears (Season 2) Tatianna impersonates Britney Spears in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 2. Logo TV Season 2 had the first-ever Snatch Game of the entire franchise, which meant that no one had any idea, or example, or blueprint for how to do well in the challenge. In a surprising turn of events, Tatianna's impersonation of Britney Spears was funny and believable, which gave her the edge to win the first Snatch Game of all time. Stacy Layne Matthews as Mo'Nique (Season 3) Stacy Layne Matthews as Mo'Nique in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 3. Logo TV Many queens didn't see Stacy Layne Matthews as a real contender throughout season 3, but her Snatch Game as Mo'Nique (in the style of Precious) was brilliant and really resonated with the judges. Chad Michaels as Cher (Season 4) Chad Michaels as Cher in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 4. Logo TV Chad Michaels walked into Drag Race season 4 as an absolute legend for being the best Cher impersonator in the world. The stakes were absurdly high for Chad to do well in the Snatch Game… But no need to fear! The queen still delivered a pitch-perfect Cher and rightfully won the challenge. Jinkx Monsoon as Little Edie (Season 5) Jinkx Monsoon as Little Edie in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 5. Logo TV The blueprint for how to play the Snatch Game on Drag Race was set by Jinkx Monsoon in season 5 with her impersonation of Little Edie (of Grey Gardens fame). Not only did Jinkx win, but she also re-invented the Snatch Game and showed queens how to do well in that challenge for several seasons to come. BenDeLaCreme as Maggie Smith (Season 6) BenDeLaCreme as Maggie Smith in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 6. Logo TV The Snatch Game of season 6 featured a fierce fight between BenDeLaCreme's Maggie Smith (in the style of Downton Abbey) and Bianca Del Rio's Judge Judy, but DeLa came out victorious in the end by surprising the judges with an unusual choice that turned out to be hilarious. Kennedy Davenport as Little Richard (Season 7 – a tie) Kennedy Davenport as Little Richard in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 7. Logo TV The first and only Snatch Game tie in a flagship season of Drag Race took place in season 7. Kennedy Davenport was one of the winners of that challenge after delivering an incredible impersonation of flamboyant musician Little Richard. It's also worth noting that this was the first-ever male character in the Snatch Game, which meant that the odds were stacked against Kennedy from the start. In any case, she went for it, and we couldn't stop laughing. Ginger Minj as Adele (Season 7 — a tie) Ginger Minj as Adele in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 7. Logo TV Ginger Minj, as Adele, was the other Snatch Game winner of season 7. Just like Kennedy Davenport's Little Richard, Ginger was incredibly funny and consistent throughout the entire challenge. This was a well-deserved tie — and not a cop-out from the judges — when taken into consideration how well both queens did in the episode. Bob the Drag Queen as Uzo Aduba and Carol Channing (Season 8) Bob the Drag Queen as Uzo Aduba and Carol Channing in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 8. Logo TV Bob the Drag Queen had not one, but two Snatch Game impersonations in season 8… And they were both incredible. Bob first did Uzo Aduba (in the style of her character from Orange Is the New Black), and then changed clothes to impersonate Carol Channing. To the surprise of literally no one, Bob won this Snatch Game, and it wasn't even close. Alexis Michelle as Liza Minnelli (Season 9) Alexis Michelle as Liza Minnelli in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 9. VH1 RuPaul had been waiting for someone to do Liza Minnelli in the Snatch Game for many, many years. Thankfully, Alexis Michelle finally gave Ru the full Liza With a "Z" experience in season 9. Though she faced some tough competition from other queens, Alexis still won the Snatch Game with her performance of the iconic Liza Minnelli. Aquaria as Melania Trump (Season 10) Aquaria as Melania Trump in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 10. VH1 As a lewk queen who struggled with public speaking sometimes, Aquaria wasn't the kind of contestant that viewers were watching out for in the Snatch Game. So imagine everyone's surprise when Aquaria's impersonation of Melania Trump in season 10 was literally hilarious and absolutely earned her the win. Silky Nutmeg Ganache as Ts Madison (Season 11) Silky Nutmeg Ganache as Ts Madison in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 11. VH1 Some people claim that Silky Nutmeg Ganache's Snatch Game performance as Ts Madison was just "Silky being Silky," but let's be honest: A lot of queens fumbled this challenge, and Silky was able to consistently make Ru and viewers laugh while staying in character. All things considered, Silky did win this Snatch Game fair and square. Gigi Goode as Maria the Robot (Season 12) Gigi Goode as Maria the Robot in the Snatch Game of RuPaul's Drag Race season 12. VH1 It had already been groundbreaking when queens started to play male celebrities in the Snatch Game. Notably, Kennedy Davenport's Little Richard impersonation was the first-ever male character to win the challenge. | Snatch Game | |||||||||||||||||| |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---| The Snatch Game is a Maxi Challenge where the contestants showcase their best celebrity impersonations in a game show setting. Originally devised as a satire of the classic TV show, Match Game (Blankety Blank in the UK), the challenge has evolved to parody other popular game shows, though maintaining the same objective: having the funniest and most accurate impersonation. When the snatch game occurs varies from season to season, however in most main seasons the Snatch Game usually occurs around episode 6 or 7, usually with 7 or 8 contestants participating. Rules of Snatch Game[] - The host shouts a random question from a deck of cards to one of the guest stars. The contestants must listen to the question and fill in the blank with their own answer. - The contestants' answers must match the one from the guest star in order to gain points. - If a contestant gets a correct, matching answer, the guest star gets a point. - At the end of the game, the points do not actually have an effect on judging, since the contestants are judged by their impersonation and comedy.
