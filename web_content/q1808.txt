--- [¿De qué habló Gustavo Petro con Alain Berset, presidente de Suiza?] ---
El presidente de Colombia, Gustavo Petro, recibió este jueves en Bogotá con honores militares a su homólogo suizo, Alain Berset, quien visita el país andino para conocer de primera mano los temas relacionados con la paz y el apoyo que le da a ellos la nación europea. La ceremonia tuvo lugar en la Plaza de Armas de la Casa de Nariño, sede del Ejecutivo colombiano, donde militares del Batallón Guardia Presidencial rindieron honores al gobernante suizo. Berset llegó a Colombia el miércoles y visitó los departamentos de Valle del Cauca y de Antioquia para conocer proyectos de promoción de la paz y de la ayuda humanitaria en las localidades de Buenaventura y de Dabeiba. Igualmente, estuvo en Medellín, donde visitó la turística Comuna 13. El jefe de Estado Suiza también se reunió este jueves con Rodrigo Londoño, presidente del partido Comunes, surgido de la desmovilización de las FARC, y el Representante Especial del Secretario General de la ONU en Colombia, Carlos Ruiz Massieu. Tras el encuentro entre los mandatarios de Colombia y Suiza, el presidente Gustavo Petro destacó la cooperación internacional por parte del país europeo, principalmente en materia de paz. "Suiza ha sido un gran aliado de la Paz del país. Exploramos lo que se viene convirtiendo en un debate al interior de Colombia, la creencia de que el Gobierno solo puede dialogar con organizaciones con estatus político, lo cual choca con el Derecho Internacional Humanitario", señaló el presidente colombiano, quien también se refirió a la posibilidad de inversión en los territorios golpeados por los cultivos ilícitos. "La idea es que la inversión de Suiza en Colombia pueda articularse a nuestros esfuerzos por salir de los cultivos ilícitos en algunas regiones del país. Que regiones con economías ilícitas se reconviertan en territorios con economías lícitas", manifestó. En Bogotá, Berset tiene previsto también entrevistarse con los principales responsables de las negociaciones de paz entre el Gobierno y el Ejército de Liberación Nacional (ELN), dado que Suiza, junto a España, Alemania y Suecia, participa en este proceso como "países acompañantes".


--- [Presidency of Gustavo Petro] ---
Gustavo Petro's term as the 34th president of Colombia began with his inauguration on 7 August 2022. Petro, who previously served as mayor of Bogotá, took office after his victory in the 2022 presidential election over the anti-corruption leader Rodolfo Hernández. Petro took office under the political shadow of his predecessor, amid an economic crisis and increased political polarization.
At the time of his election, Petro was a 62-year-old member of Congress; his victory has been attributed to public anger at the political class over years of corruption in politics, economic recession, the COVID-19 health crisis and a rise in violent crime. The crisis the country was in was caused, among other factors, by the weakness of the prices of raw materials; the events revealed underlying weaknesses in the economy including poor infrastructure, excessive bureaucracy, an inefficient tax system, and corruption. 




--- [President of Switzerland Visits Colombia to Support the Peace Process ...] ---
Swiss President Alain Berset is on an official visit to Colombia. The purpose is to show support for the peace talks with the ELN (National Liberation Army) and the Colombian government’s “Total Peace” project. The visit began on August 7, and after touring the country for several days, Berset is meeting with President Gustavo Petro in Bogota today. Results of Humanitarian Cooperation The Swiss president’s official visit comes within the framework of Switzerland’s support for the peace plans developed by the Colombian government. In this context, humanitarian cooperation and the ongoing dialogues with the ELN set the stage for the meeting between Berset and his Colombian counterpart, Gustavo Petro. Switzerland is one of the accompanying countries in the dialogue process that Colombia maintains with the ELN guerrilla. Along with Spain, Germany, and Sweden, the Swiss form the group of accompanying states, a typology for peace dialogues that Colombia initiated 25 years ago. Berset has stated that Colombia is a priority country in Switzerland’s plan to promote humanitarian cooperation agreements. Over the last two days, the Swiss president has toured Colombian territory. During visits to Buenaventura and Dabeiba (Antioquia), Alain Berset engaged with various social players to understand firsthand how the internal conflict is affecting the country. In Buenaventura, he gained insights into the lives of communities in the Colombian Pacific, during a visit with Carlos Ruiz Massieu, the UN Secretary-General’s Special Representative in Colombia. The two high-ranking representatives had the opportunity to speak with the mothers of young victims of the Punta del Este massacre in 2005. In Dabeiba (Antioquia), Berset interacted with a reintegration project for ex-combatants, involving his country. There, Berset and Ruiz Massieu had the chance to observe progress in reconciliation between victims and signatories of the peace agreement. The President of Switzerland supports the “Total Peace” project Following this journey through Valle del Cauca and Antioquia, Alain Berset arrives in Bogotá where he will hold talks with Colombian President Gustavo Petro. The Swiss leader will discuss progress in the implementation of the 2016 peace agreements with the former FARC (Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia). Within the context of dialogues and Colombia’s “Total Peace” project, Berset is scheduled to meet with the key figures in the discussions between the government and the ELN. Likewise, according to official sources, both presidents will also address topics related to trade, bilateral cooperation, and actions against climate change. Finally, the signing of an agreement is foreseen, through which Switzerland will digitally archive a copy of the documentation from the Colombian Truth Commission.
