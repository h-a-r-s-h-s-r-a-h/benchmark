--- [Somnath Bharti - Wikipedia] ---
Somnath Bharti (born 10 May 1974) is an Indian politician and lawyer. 

As a member of the Aam Aadmi Party, Bharti served as a Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA) of Delhi from Malviya Nagar constituency. He has previously served as minister of Law, Tourism, Administrative Reforms, Art & Culture in the Government of Delhi, from December 2013 to February 2014 in the first Arvind Kejriwal government.
He is a practicing lawyer in the Supreme Court of India and Delhi High Court. He had represented the Malviya Nagar constituency in the Delhi Legislative Assembly from 2013 till 2025.

Activism In June 2012, Bharti was involved in a campaign against the alleged interference of the then Minister of Human Resources and Development, Kapil Sibal, in the Joint Entrance Examination process for admission to Indian Institutes of Technology. Political career Bharti was the Aam Aadmi Party candidate for the Malviya Nagar constituency in the Delhi state assembly elections, 2013. Bharti won the seat, defeating Arti Mehra of the Bharatiya Janata Party and the incumbent Kiran Walia of the Indian National Congress. Walia had won the seat in 1999, 2003, and 2008 and had been education minister in the earlier government, while Mehra, who has represented the neighbouring Hauz Khas constituency for many years, had been Mayor of Delhi in 2007-2009. In contrast, Bharti was new to politics. Bharti was briefly Minister of Law, Tourism, Administrative Reforms, Art & Culture in the Government of Delhi. He was appointed on 28 December 2013 and left office on 14 February 2014 when the government of which he was a part resigned due a failure to enact a Jan Lokpal bill. In January 2014, during his time in office, Bharti revealed the identity of a Danish tourist who had been raped, despite such identification being illegal in India. The government's resignation, led by Arvind Kejriwal, pre-empted a personal resignation by Bharti in response to accusations of vigilantism. Some residents in the Khirkee Extension area of Delhi had complained to Bharti about a drug and prostitution racket allegedly being run by African nationals. In January 2014, he and some AAP workers, together with television camera crews, visited the area. Bharti and his supporters asked the police to raid the homes rented by Africans. When the police refused to do so, citing a lack of a warrant, a group led by Bharti allegedly caught four women and forced them to give urine tests. The AAP supported Bharti, calling the women's allegations false and stating that his actions were not racist and that residents had long complained of criminal activities by Africans in the area. Kejriwal demanded suspension of the police officers who had refused to conduct the raid. A court directed the police to lodge a First Information Report against the mob, and the Delhi Commission for Women also opened an investigation into the matter. Kejriwal's government had come under increasing political pressure to act against Bharti in the hours preceding its resignation. An independent judicial enquiry conducted on behalf of the Lieutenant-Governor of Delhi announced on 28 February 2014 that the police had been correct not to accede to Bharti's demands and that Bharti should not have then taken the law into his own hands by leading the raid. The police investigation into the events in not expected to report until 26 March 2014. Domestic abuse allegations On 10 September 2015, Bharti was arrested on a domestic abuse case after his wife alleged he beat her for a prolonged period. A non-bailable warrant was also issued against him by a Delhi High Court. The Delhi High Court rejected his anticipatory bail plea and he finally surrendered at the Dwarka Police Station late at night on 29 September 2015 under directions from the Supreme Court. Bharti obtained conditional bail, whereas his dog was exonerated.

Somnath Bharti is an Indian lawyer and politician who represented the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) as a member of the Delhi Legislative Assembly for the Malviya Nagar constituency from 2013 until his defeat in the 2025 state elections.[1][2] A founding member of AAP, Bharti entered politics after a career in information technology consulting and legal practice focused on public interest litigation.[1][3]Bharti, an alumnus of the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi and the University of Delhi's Faculty of Law, secured three consecutive victories in Malviya Nagar assembly elections, establishing himself as a key AAP figure in South Delhi.[3][4] He briefly served as Delhi's Minister of Law during AAP's short-lived government in 2013–2014 and later as Minister of Law and Justice under Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal.[5] In 2024, he made his debut in national politics by contesting the Lok Sabha seat from New Delhi but lost to Bharatiya Janata Party candidate Bansuri Swaraj by a margin of approximately 78,000 votes.[6][7]Bharti's political career has been marked by notable controversies, including a 2014 incident as law minister where he mobilized residents to raid a neighborhood suspected of illegal activities, leading to allegations of vigilantism and mistreatment of African women residents.[8] In 2015, his wife accused him of domestic violence, a case that involved even their pet dog and was eventually settled through mediation in 2018.[8][9] More recently, in early 2025, Bharatiya Janata Party spokesperson Sambit Patra alleged Bharti's involvement in influencing a land-grabbing probe in Jaitpur, presenting an audio clip as evidence, though Bharti denied the claims.[10][11] These events have drawn scrutiny to his approach to governance and personal conduct. Early life and education Family background and upbringing Somnath Bharti was born on 10 May 1974 in Hisua, Nawada district, Bihar, to Sitaram Bharti, a spiritual wanderer, and Manorama Rani Bharti, a school teacher.[3][12]He was the eldest son and second of three children, raised in the semi-urban environment of Hisua Bazar alongside an elder sister, Divya Stuti, who became a classical music teacher, and a younger brother, Loknath Bharti, a software engineer.[3][12]Bharti's family life centered on his father's spiritual pursuits, which influenced household dynamics and led Bharti to begin practicing meditation as a child—a habit he maintains daily.[3] From an early age, he showed concern for disadvantaged children in his community, often gifting them clothes from his own possessions.[3][12] Academic qualifications Somnath Bharti earned a Master of Science degree in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi, in 1997.[13][1] This postgraduate qualification followed his initial schooling in Bihar, where he completed primary education before relocating to Delhi for advanced studies.[14]Bharti subsequently pursued legal education, obtaining a Bachelor of Laws (LLB) from Law Centre-II, Faculty of Law, University of Delhi, in 2008.[13][1][14] These credentials supported his transition into legal practice after earlier roles in academia and consulting.[15] Pre-political career Academic and consulting roles Following his completion of an M.Sc. in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi in 1997, Bharti worked in the coaching sector as a mathematics teacher, tutoring students preparing for IIT entrance examinations.[12][3][16] This role aligned with his academic background and involved direct instruction in advanced mathematical concepts relevant to engineering admissions.[1]Bharti subsequently shifted to information technology consulting, leveraging his technical education in a professional capacity before pursuing legal studies and practice.[16][17][18] Specific details on consulting engagements remain limited in public records, though reports describe it as a successful phase preceding his activism.[3] Legal practice Somnath Bharti enrolled as an advocate in 2009 after completing his law degree from the University of Delhi.[16] He established the law firm Bharti & Associates, which provides services in civil, criminal, corporate law, and intellectual property matters.[19][3] Much of Bharti's practice emphasized public interest litigation, where he pursued cases addressing social injustices and advocated on behalf of marginalized groups by filing petitions to highlight and challenge systemic issues.[3][15] In 2009, Bharti's legal efforts contributed to pressuring the government to partially retract interference in an unspecified matter, as he later claimed in Supreme Court proceedings.[17] His professional record, however, included disciplinary issues; while defending a client in a corruption case, a Patiala House Court in 2013 indicted him for evidence tampering and rebuked his actions as highly objectionable and unethical.[20][21] Activism and political entry Anti-corruption initiatives Somnath Bharti became involved in anti-corruption activism through participation in the India Against Corruption (IAC) movement, which began in 2011 under Arvind Kejriwal's leadership to demand the creation of a robust Lokpal institution for probing high-level graft.[22] Bharti, who first met Kejriwal around 2010 amid early transparency campaigns, aligned with the group's efforts to expose systemic corruption via public protests and advocacy for legislative reforms like the Jan Lokpal Bill.[23] The movement mobilized mass demonstrations, including the high-profile 2011 fast by Anna Hazare, though Bharti's role centered on supporting Kejriwal's push for political action post-2012 split from the apolitical IAC core.[24]As a Supreme Court advocate prior to AAP's founding, Bharti handled legal matters tied to corruption allegations, including representing figures challenging official misconduct, which he framed as part of broader accountability drives.[25] His activism emphasized rooting out black money and institutional nexuses enabling graft, aligning with IAC's focus on empowering citizens through information rights and judicial oversight.[26] This pre-political engagement positioned Bharti as a proponent of direct confrontation against entrenched corruption, influencing his later transition to electoral politics.[17] Joining the Aam Aadmi Party Somnath Bharti became a member of the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) at its inception on November 26, 2012, following the formation of the party by Arvind Kejriwal and associates after the split from the India Against Corruption (IAC) campaign.[22] His involvement stemmed from prior participation in the IAC movement, which he joined after teaching children in East Delhi slums and aligning with anti-corruption activism led by Kejriwal.[22][24] Bharti first encountered Kejriwal around 2010 during the early stages of transparency activism that evolved into IAC protests against alleged corruption in government. This engagement positioned him among the early supporters who transitioned to AAP, contributing to its foundational emphasis on fighting corruption through political means rather than solely street protests.[22] As an inaugural AAP member, Bharti leveraged his background as a lawyer to support the party's legal and organizational setup, including representing Kejriwal in corruption-related cases.[25] His rapid integration facilitated his nomination as the AAP candidate for the Malviya Nagar constituency in the December 2013 Delhi Legislative Assembly elections, where he secured victory.[22] Political career Service as MLA and minister Somnath Bharti was elected to the Delhi Legislative Assembly from the Malviya Nagar constituency in the December 2013 elections as a candidate of the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP), securing victory with 57,982 votes against the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) incumbent.[14][1] On December 28, 2013, following AAP's formation of a minority government under Chief MinisterArvind Kejriwal, Bharti was inducted into the cabinet as Minister of Law, Tourism, Administrative Reforms, Art and Culture.[22][4] His tenure in these roles lasted approximately 49 days, concluding with the government's resignation on February 14, 2014, amid disputes over governance priorities including the Jan Lokpal Bill.[1] During this period, Bharti outlined intentions to strengthen judicial independence and streamline administrative processes, though the brevity of the government's term limited implementation.[18]Bharti was re-elected from Malviya Nagar in the February 2015 Delhi Assembly elections, defeating the BJP candidate by a margin of 2,677 votes amid AAP's landslide victory.[1] He served his second term as a backbench MLA, focusing on constituency-level development such as infrastructure improvements and public service delivery, without securing a cabinet position in the expanded AAP government formed that month.[27] In the February 2020 elections, he secured a third consecutive term, winning by 20,640 votes over the BJP's Kanak Verma, reflecting sustained voter support in a constituency spanning middle-class and resettlement areas.[1][28] As MLA during this term, Bharti emphasized transforming Malviya Nagar into a "model constituency" through targeted local interventions, including sanitation drives and resident welfare enhancements, though quantifiable outcomes remained tied to broader AAP governance metrics.[27]Bharti's service as MLA extended until the end of the 2020-2025 assembly term, during which he participated in legislative debates on Delhi's administrative autonomy and urban issues, while maintaining an active role in AAP's opposition to central government interventions in local affairs.[5] He did not hold ministerial office in subsequent AAP governments led by Kejriwal from 2015 onward.[29] In the February 2025 Delhi Assembly elections, Bharti sought a fourth term but lost to BJP's Satish Upadhyay by approximately 3,000 votes, marking the end of his legislative service.[2] Key legislative positions and initiatives During his tenure as Minister of Law, Justice, and Legislative Affairs in the short-lived Arvind Kejriwal government from December 2013 to February 2014, Somnath Bharti prioritized anti-corruption legislation, spearheading efforts to enact the Jan Lokpal Bill, which sought to create an independent body for probing high-level graft cases with provisions for swift investigations and penalties. The bill's rushed introduction drew criticism from opposition parties Congress and BJP for bypassing procedural norms and lacking stakeholder consultation, ultimately failing to pass and contributing to the government's resignation on 14 February 2014.[30][31]As a Member of the Legislative Assembly (MLA) from Malviya Nagar since 2013, Bharti has advanced resolutions challenging central government interventions in Delhi's governance. On 27 May 2015, during a special session, he moved a private member's resolution declaring the Ministry of Home Affairs' notification—granting the lieutenant governor control over civil services—as unconstitutional and invalid, urging the assembly to disregard it in defense of the National Capital Territory's executive autonomy.[32][33] The resolution aligned with Aam Aadmi Party's broader campaign for full statehood, highlighting ongoing jurisdictional disputes under Article 239AA of the Constitution.Bharti has consistently supported assembly resolutions opposing central policies perceived as infringing on local priorities. In January 2017, he proposed a motion strongly condemning Delhi Police and paramilitary actions against students protesting fee hikes at universities, framing it as an assault on democratic rights and calling for accountability.[34] Similarly, in December 2020, he participated in efforts to pass a resolution against the central farm laws, including tearing copies of the legislation in the house to symbolize rejection, while advocating compensation for protesting farmers who died.[35] In July 2021, the assembly under his party's majority adopted a resolution he backed against the appointment of Rakesh Asthana as Delhi Police Commissioner, citing conflicts of interest.[36]Bharti has publicly criticized the central government's handling of Delhi's legislative agenda, noting in October 2021 that approximately 40 bills passed by the assembly—spanning areas like health, education, and anti-corruption—remained pending approval, including re-referred versions of the Jan Lokpal Bill returned in 2016.[37][38] His positions reflect Aam Aadmi Party's emphasis on decentralizing power and expediting judicial and administrative reforms, though many initiatives faced delays due to lieutenant governor referrals and Supreme Court oversight on Delhi's limited legislative domain. 2024 Lok Sabha candidacy Somnath Bharti was nominated by the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) as its candidate for the New Delhi Lok Sabha constituency in the 2024 Indian general election.[39][40] The AAP contested four seats in Delhi as part of the INDIA alliance seat-sharing agreement with the Indian National Congress, which fielded candidates in the remaining three constituencies.[41] Bharti's candidacy leveraged his incumbency as the AAP MLA from Malviya Nagar, a segment within New Delhi, emphasizing local governance achievements in education and health.[42]Polling in Delhi occurred on 25 May 2024 during the sixth phase of the elections.[7] Bharti campaigned aggressively, criticizing the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) on issues like unemployment and inflation while promising continuity of AAP's welfare schemes at the national level. On 1 June 2024, he publicly vowed to shave his head if Narendra Modi secured a third term as Prime Minister, framing it as confidence in opposition gains.[43]Results were declared on 4 June 2024, with Bharti securing 374,815 votes against BJP candidate Bansuri Swaraj's 453,185, resulting in a defeat by a margin of 78,370 votes.[7][41][44] Following the loss, Bharti refused to honor his shaving pledge, stating that Modi's victory lacked an absolute majority and relied on coalition support.[45]On 20 July 2024, Bharti filed an election petition in the Delhi High Court challenging Swaraj's election, alleging corrupt practices including undue influence, vote manipulation, and inflammatory speeches by Swaraj and BJP leaders like Nirmala Sitharaman that violated the Representation of the People Act, 1951.[46][47] He sought Swaraj's disqualification and a fresh poll. The court issued notices to Swaraj and the Election Commission on 14 August 2024, with hearings ongoing as of late 2024.[48][49] Electoral history Delhi Assembly elections Somnath Bharti first contested the Malviya Nagar constituency in the 2013 Delhi Legislative Assembly election as the Aam Aadmi Party candidate, securing victory and defeating the incumbent Bharatiya Janata Party MLA.[50] He retained the seat in the 2015 election, polling 51,196 votes against the Bharatiya Janata Party candidate's 35,299 votes.[51]In the 2020 Delhi Assembly election, Bharti won re-election by a margin of 18,144 votes over the Bharatiya Janata Party's Shailendra Singh Moti, receiving 52,043 votes.[52][51] This victory marked his third consecutive term as the Member of the Legislative Assembly from Malviya Nagar.[53]Bharti sought a fourth term in the 2025 Delhi Legislative Assembly election held on 5 February 2025 but lost to the Bharatiya Janata Party's Satish Upadhyay by a margin of over 2,000 votes.[54] Upadhyay's win ended AAP's hold on the constituency since 2013.[2] Lok Sabha elections Somnath Bharti contested the 2024 Indian general election from the New Delhi Lok Sabha constituency as the candidate of the Aam Aadmi Party (AAP), marking his first attempt at a parliamentary seat.[4] The polling occurred on 25 May 2024, with vote counting and results announced on 4 June 2024.[7]Bharti faced Bansuri Swaraj of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), who secured victory with 453,185 votes.[41] Bharti received 374,815 votes, finishing second and losing by a margin of 78,370 votes.[41][7] hesitated citing lack of warrants, he and his group proceeded to surround and enter at least one building, confronting the occupants.[59][60]The raid escalated into confrontation when police arrived; Bharti urged his supporters to block the officers from leaving until arrests were made, leading to scuffles.[61] Several African women inside the flats alleged that the mob, led by Bharti, forcibly entered their residences, molested them by pulling at clothing and making indecent demands (such as to strip for inspection), assaulted one Ugandan woman by slapping her, and threatened violence while 323 (voluntarily causing hurt), Section 506 (criminal intimidation), Section 153A (promoting enmity between groups), Section 147 (rioting), and others for wrongful confinement and outraging party.[81] Personal life Marriage and family dynamics Somnath Bharti married Lipika Mitra on December 10, 2010, after meeting through a matrimonial website.[82][83] The couple has two children: a son named Somansh Bharti and a daughter named Somalika Bharti.[82]The marriage deteriorated soon after its inception, with reports of ongoing discord attributed to Bharti's demanding schedule and temperament.[84] Lipika Mitra accused Bharti of subjecting her and the children to mental, physical, and verbal abuse, including assaults and harassment, leading her to file a domestic violence complaint with the Delhi police and the Delhi Commission for Women in June 2015.[85][86] She alleged specific incidents such as Bharti unleashing their pet dog on her and forcing an abortion, while living separately in Dwarka with the children for several years prior.[87][83]Bharti denied the allegations, claiming they were politically motivated and asserting that family matters were internal, with audio evidence purportedly refuting the claims.[88][89] Mitra refused Supreme Court mediation in October 2015, expressing intent to pursue legal separation for the children's future.[90][91] In May 2019, the Delhi High Court quashed the FIR against Bharti, noting that he and Mitra had amicably settled the matrimonial dispute out of court.[73] The couple remains separated, with no public reports of reconciliation as of recent accounts.[1] Public persona and ideology Somnath Bharti projects a public persona as a combative activist and grassrootspolitician, characterized by aggressive interventions in local issues and a willingness to challenge authorities directly, often disregarding conventional protocols. As an IIT Delhi alumnus and lawyer who transitioned into politics, he has been described as ambitious and over-zealous, with a penchant for high-profile actions such as midnight raids targeting suspected drug and prostitution rackets in his Malviya Nagar constituency in January 2014, which drew both praise for vigilance and criticism for vigilantism.[16] This confrontational style, coupled with involvement in over a dozen controversies including arrests in 2015 and 2016, has earned him the label of Aam Aadmi Party's (AAP) "original bad boy," yet he maintains favor with party leadership, Early Life of Somnath Bharti Somnath Bharti was born on 10 May, 1974. He spent his childhood at Hisua Bazar in Nawada district of Bihar, about 100 km from the state capital Patna. He additionally served IIT Delhi Alumni Association as its Secretary for 2007-08 and 2011-12 and as IIT Delhi Senator in 2008 and later as the president in 2012-13. Political Career of Somnath Bharti In 2009, Somnath Bharti led the movement against the abeyance of sentencing of Vikram Buddhi, an Indian PhD student at Purdue University who was confined by the United States ascendant entities in 2006 for writing against the former President of US, George Bush. Ultimately, the US equity system was forced to promulgate a sentencing within three months, after it had been in abeyance for over three years. Being a lawyer, Bharti devoted a plethora of time in the redressal of grievances by filing Public Interest Litigations (PILs) and fighting the battles for helpless, poor and illiterate class of people. In 2010-2013, he came in the limelight for fighting the rights of home-schooled children and subscribers of alternate edification system in view of the binding provision of the Right of children to free and compulsory education Act. In response to his PILs, the ministry of Human Resources and Development (HRD), through an affidavit, clarified that they are not against home-schooling. Bharti was additionally involved in a campaign against the alleged interference of the then Minister of HRD, Kapil Sibal, in the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) process for admission to IIT in June 2012. Later, the government partially withdrew the proposal. Being an activist, he also raised the issue of two deaths caused by contaminated drinking water in the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) campus in 2013. In the 2013 Delhi state assembly elections, Bharti contested from the Malviya Nagar constituency on AAP ticket and was elected as a member of Legislative Assembly, and got the cabinet berth. During his time in office, Bharti revealed the identity of a Danish tourist who had been raped, despite the fact that revealing a rape victim's identity is illicit in India. During the same tenure, another act of his sparked off a major controversy when he ordered Delhi police to raid an area called Khirki village of South Delhi on grounds of illegal drug trafficking. The Police refused to abide by his orders as it did not work under Delhi government's supervision but Central government. This was followed by major protests by the party and the CM Arvind Kejriwal to bring the Delhi Police under Delhi government. On 14 February, 2014, Bharti resigned along with his other colleagues in the government led by Kejriwal, over the failure to enact a Jan Lokpal (Citizen's Ombudsman) bill.
