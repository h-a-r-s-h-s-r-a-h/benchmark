Krishi Vaniki- other species Yojana This scheme has been implemented since 2012-13 in Bihar to encourage plantation of other species. For instance, Teak, Mahogany, Beechwood, Rosewood and Khair. Mukhya Mantri Niji Paudhshala-Poplar ETP yojana This scheme aims to set up nurseries with quality plants belonging to different species through entrepreneurs and farmers. Har Parisar Hara Parisar Yojana Due to the shortage of forest land in Bihar, this scheme was launched to increase both the purity and greenery in the environment. Greening of Urban spaces The prime objective of this scheme is to promote projects related to landscaping of urban parks and green spaces in the urban areas. Jal- Jeevan Hariyali Campaign Due to climate change and global warming, the government of Bihar is focusing on managing water, forests and other natural resources to reduce the risks of environmental degradation. --- ['State forest cover up by 6.99sq km' - Times of India] ---
PATNA: The state’s forest cover increased by ‘meagre’ 6.99 square kilometres in the last two years, as per the India State of Forest Report (ISFR), 2019 released by Union minister Prakash Javadekar on Monday. Besides, tree cover in Bihar has decreased by 260 square km as compared to the previous ISFR report in 2017. The ISFR is a biennial publication of Forest Survey of India (FSI) under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEF&CC). It gives a comprehensive assessment of the inventory of forest, tree cover, bamboo resources and carbon stock among other aspects related to forests, green cover and biodiversity. Apart from reduction in tree cover, the ISFR stated that a total of 1,131.60 hectares of forest land in Bihar was diverted for non-forestry purposes between January 1, 2015 and February 5, 2019 under the Forest Conservation Act, 1980 (MoEF&CC, 2019). Against the diversion of forest land for non-forestry purposes, the state government has communicated to the FSI that a total of 56,560 hectares of plantations have been raised during the last two years (2017-19). The ISFR report further stated that based on the interpretation of IRS Resourcesat-2 LISS III satellite data of the period between October 2017 and December 2018, the forest cover in the state is 7,305.99 sq km, which is 7.76% of the state’s total geographical area. In terms of forest canopy density classes, Bihar has 333.13 sq km under ‘very dense forest’ (VDF), 3,280.32 sq km under ‘Moderately Dense Forest’ (MDF) and 3,692.54 sq km under ‘Open Forest’ (OF). Forest cover in the state has increased by 6.99 square km as compared to the previous ISFR assessment in 2017. The ISFR report attributed the net positive change of 6.99 square km observed in forest cover to plantations and conservation practices. The top three districts with the maximum increase in forest cover in the last two years were Banka (20.73%), Muzaffarpur (19.82%) and East Champaran (8.86%). The districts with maximum decrease in forest cover included Rohtas (-33.77%), Gaya (-14.69%) and Kaimur (-14.61%). The report further stated that the total carbon stock of forests in Bihar is 55.24 million tonnes, which is 0.78% of the total forest carbon in the country. The carbon stock of forests is the amount of carbon sequestered from the atmosphere and stored in forest ecosystem, mainly within living biomass and soil. The state government, on the other hand, has been claiming 15% green cover in Bihar and it’s target is to increase it to 17% by the end of 2020. A senior official of state environment, forests and climate change department told TOI on the condition of anonymity that the reason behind lower forest cover cited by ISFR is frequent change in the criteria for the same while making assessment for the report. “We had conducted two surveys in 2017 — one by the FSI itself and the other by Bihar Remote Sensing Application Centre. The report of both the surveys had pegged forest cover in the state at 14.5%. However, we have observed since 2011 that the criteria for identifying forest cover in ISFR keeps changing every time. Thus, we do not take much notice of ISFR and go by our own surveys conducted by third party agencies based on most advanced technologies,” he said.
