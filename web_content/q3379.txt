--- [Lawrence Francis Kramer - Wikipedia] ---
Lawrence Francis "Pat" Kramer Jr. (February 24, 1933 – August 24, 2023) was an American politician who was the Mayor of Paterson, New Jersey from 1967 to 1972 and 1975 until 1982.
Kramer ran for Governor of New Jersey in the Republican primary in 1981. He came in second to future Governor Thomas Kean.

Let your community know Let the family know you are thinking of them Hear your loved one's obituary Kramer, Jr., Lawrence Francis, “Pat”, age 90, of Wyckoff, formerly of Paterson, on Thursday, August 24, 2023. Born and raised in Paterson, Pat had lived there most of his life before moving to Wyckoff. In 1965, Pat commenced his career as General Manager of Lawrence F. Kramer Company in Paterson. He started his political career in 1966 when he was elected Mayor of Paterson, serving from 1967-1972 and then again from 1975 to 1982. During the hiatus from the Mayoral office, Pat served on Governor William Cahill’s cabinet as the Commissioner of Community Affairs for the State of New Jersey. He was true leader in the Paterson community. As mayor, he led the effort to designate the Great Falls of Paterson as a National Historic Landmark in 1976. Pat continued his efforts after his time in office and worked to have the Falls designated a National Historic Park in 2009. After his public service, Pat began as the Executive Vice President of V. Paulius & Associates in Allendale. He was a parishioner of St. Therese R.C. Church in Paterson and then St. Elizabeth’s R.C. Church in Wyckoff. --- [Pat Kramer, Longtime Paterson Mayor, Dies At 90 - Daily Voice] ---
Kramer grew up on East 42nd Street and 18th Avenue, and attended what is now John F. Kennedy High School. After graduating from Fairleigh Dickinson University, Kramer ran on the Republican ticket for mayor, and was elected in 1966. Kramer's first term of mayor was from 1967 to 1972, before he was asked to head up the Commission of Public Affairs. Following that stint, he ran for mayor again, serving from 1975 to 1982. He also ran for governor in 1981, losing in the Republican primary. As mayor, Kramer worked to preserve the Great Falls Historic District, including getting then-President Gerald Ford to pay a visit. Tributes poured in for Kramer following his passing. Pat "was a brilliant advisor, collaborator and friend," Congressman Bill Pascrell said. "I have met great leaders in all walks of life.
