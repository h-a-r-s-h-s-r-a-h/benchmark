Koichi Mizushima (水島 公一, Mizushima Kōichi; born January 30, 1941) is a Japanese materials scientist best known for discovering lithium cobalt oxide (LiCoO₂) as a high-voltage cathode material that enabled the development of practical lithium-ion batteries.[1][2]Mizushima earned his doctorate in physics and conducted postdoctoral research at the University of Tokyo in 1978 before joining John B. Goodenough's research group at the University of Oxford as a visiting scientist.[1] There, as part of a collaborative effort to create safer, high-energy-density rechargeable batteries, he shifted focus from lithium metal and sulfide-based materials—plagued by safety issues like explosions—to metal oxide alternatives.[1] Mizushima's innovative approach involved identifying oxides from which lithium ions could be reversibly extracted rather than absorbed, leading to his 1980 publication demonstrating that LiCoO₂ provided nearly twice the voltage of prior batteries while maintaining stability.[2][1]This breakthrough, achieved by thinning electrodes to about 100 microns to improve current flow, resulted in the first lithium-ion battery prototype free of metallic lithium, offering enhanced safety without sacrificing energy density.[1] Mizushima's work at Toshiba Corporation, where he spent much of his career, directly contributed to the commercialization of lithium-ion technology, powering portable electronics like laptops and smartphones, as well as advancing applications in electric vehicles and renewable energy storage.[3][1] In recognition of these impacts, he received the 2016 NIMS Award from Japan's National Institute for Materials Science for his foundational role in environmental and energy materials research.[3] Mizushima continues as a specially appointed Executive Fellow at Toshiba Research Consulting Corporation, applying his expertise in solid-state physics, magnetism, and battery innovation.[1][4] Early Life and Education Birth and Early Influences Koichi Mizushima was born on January 30, 1941, in Tokyo, Japan.[5]Details regarding his family background, including parental professions or siblings, remain limited in available records, with no specific influences documented in public sources.
