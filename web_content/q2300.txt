--- [Balen Shah - Wikipedia] ---
Balendra Shah (born 27 April 1990), more commonly known as Balen, is a Nepali politician, structural engineer and former rapper who served as the 15th mayor of Kathmandu from 30 May 2022 until his resignation on 18 January 2026. He was the first independent candidate to be elected as mayor of Kathmandu.
Shah has been a part of the Nepalese hip-hop industry since 2012. In the 2022 local election, Shah was elected mayor, defeating Nepali Congress candidate Srijana Singh and CPN (UML) candidate and former Kathmandu mayor Keshav Sthapit.
As mayor, Shah's tenure included improvements to waste management and road traffic control but was also highly controversial for his high-handed approach to complex issues like squatter resettlement, demolition of illegally built private structures, and his administration's persecution of street vendors. In January 2026, Shah resigned as mayor of Kathmandu and joined the Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP) to contest the 2026 general elections.

Balendra Shah (Balen) Biography Balendra Shah, commonly known as “Balen Shah”, is a prominent young leader in Nepal, a structural engineer, and a hip-hop artist (rapper). In Nepal’s 2022 local elections (B.S. 2079), he defeated candidates from established political parties and was elected as an independent candidate to the post of Mayor of Kathmandu Metropolitan City (KMC). Despite having no prior political or administrative experience, his technical knowledge, clear vision, and support from the younger generation led to this victory, which is widely viewed as a “paradigm shift” in Nepal’s parliamentary and local politics. Personal Details - Full Name: Balendra Shah (बालेन्द्र साह) - Common Name: Balen (बालेन) - Date of Birth: 27 April 1990 (B.S. 2047 Baisakh 14) - Place of Birth: Nardevi, Kathmandu - Nationality: Nepali - Ancestral Home: Ekdara Rural Municipality, Mahottari (Madhesh Province) - Primary Fields/Profession: Politics, Civil Engineering, Music - Current Position: Mayor, Kathmandu Metropolitan City (Term: 30 May 2022 to present) - Educational Qualification: Master of Technology (Structural Engineering) Table of Content - Balendra Shah (Balen) Biography - Personal Details - Childhood and Background - Education and Qualifications - Early Career: Engineering and Music - Political Rise and the 2079 Election - Major Contributions and Initiatives as Mayor - Controversies and Criticisms - Personal Life - Legacy and Historical Impact - Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - Sources and Verification Note Childhood and Background Balendra Shah was born in the historic Nardevi area of Kathmandu. His father, Dr. Ramnarayan Shah, is an Ayurvedic physician, and his mother is Dhruvadevi Shah. His family background is linked to Ekdara Rural Municipality in Mahottari district, although his upbringing and education took place in the Kathmandu Valley. Born into a middle-class family, Shah showed an early interest in science and technology. At the same time, he was drawn to poetry writing and music. His childhood was spent around Nardevi and Tinkune, giving him exposure to both Kathmandu’s traditional urban lifestyle and modern influences. Education and Qualifications Balendra Shah’s academic journey is considered technically strong and successful. - School Education: Completed school-level education (SLC) from V.S. Niketan Higher Secondary School, Kathmandu. - Higher Secondary Education: Completed 10+2 (Science) from Himalayan WhiteHouse International College. - Bachelor’s Degree: Completed a bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering from Acme Engineering College, Kathmandu. - Master’s Degree: Earned a postgraduate degree (Master of Technology) in Structural Engineering from Visvesvaraya Technological University (VTU), Karnataka, India. This academic background provided him expertise in urban infrastructure development, earthquake-resistant structures, and urban planning. Early Career: Engineering and Music Before entering politics, Balendra Shah was active in two fields: engineering and music. a) Engineering Career After the 2015 earthquake (B.S. 2072), Shah actively participated in reconstruction work. He worked as a consultant with various national and international institutions. His expertise was applied particularly in building safety assessment and correcting technical errors in the map approval process. He also worked on development and construction projects in different parts of Nepal in collaboration with an institution from the Netherlands. b) Musical Journey A key reason Balendra Shah became widely known among the younger generation as “Balen” was his rap music. From 2013, he gained attention in the Nepali hip-hop scene through the rap battle program known as Raw Barz, which was initiated by “Yama Buddha” (Yama Buddha). - Academic and Social Message: His songs often reflected social distortions, frustration with politics, and a sense of patriotism. - Notable Songs: “Sadak Balak,” “Balidan,” “Gorkhali Takma,” among others. However, after becoming active in politics, he has taken a break from musical performances. Political Rise and the 2079 Election Balendra Shah’s political rise is considered an unexpected and significant event in Nepal’s history. He ran as an independent candidate in Kathmandu, a city widely seen as a stronghold of Nepal’s major established parties—Nepali Congress and CPN (UML). - Candidacy Preparation: He had been preparing his candidacy for around two years before the election. - Election Symbol: “Lauro” (Walking Stick). He described this symbol as “support for the elderly and a weapon to strike corruption.” - Campaign Strategy: Instead of large rallies and mass meetings, he prioritized a social media campaign and grassroots door-to-door campaigning. Election Result In the vote count held in May 2022 (B.S. 2079 Jestha), Balendra Shah won by a large margin. - Votes Received: 61,767 - Closest Rivals: Sirjana Singh (Nepali Congress) – 38,341 votes, and Keshav Sthapit (CPN UML) – 38,117 votes. Major Contributions and Initiatives as Mayor After assuming office on B.S. 2079 Jestha 16 (30 May 2022), Balendra Shah introduced major changes in Kathmandu Metropolitan City’s administrative working style. His tenure is noted for a “no-nonsense” and “action-oriented” leadership style. He has focused on policy strictness and a regulatory role in physical infrastructure to make traditional, weak administration more efficient. His key contributions and initiatives can be categorized as follows. a) Urban Management and Regulation One of Balendra Shah’s most visible and widely discussed moves has been maintaining “urban order.” To regulate uncontrolled urbanization in the Kathmandu Valley, he adopted a zero-tolerance policy on full compliance with the building code and map approval requirements. - Basement and Parking Reform: A common practice was for commercial buildings to show underground parking in approved plans but rent the space in practice as shops, warehouses, or restaurants. Under Balen’s leadership, metropolitan police and dozers were used to remove illegal structures in dozens of complexes and restore those spaces as parking areas. This helped reduce on-street parking pressure on major roads such as New Baneshwor, Durbar Marg, and Koteshwor. - Hoarding Board and Visual Pollution Control: A strict policy was implemented to remove large hoarding boards installed against standards and to allow only those that complied with specified size and tax requirements. - Wire Management: In coordination with the Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA) and internet service providers, efforts were intensified to remove unnecessary hanging wires (unused optical fibers/cables) from roads. b) Structural Reforms in Education Balen Shah’s “Shikshama Sip” program is presented as a model initiative practiced by a local level in Nepal. He carried out wide interventions to improve the quality of 89 community schools in Kathmandu. - Book Free Friday: To move beyond textbook-only learning and teach life skills, additional activities were made mandatory every Friday. Under this, students receive practical training such as agriculture, mobile repair, house wiring, culinary arts, traditional music, and robotics. - 10% Scholarship in Private Schools: In line with provisions in the Education Act, private schools were strictly required to provide scholarships to 10% of their total students—targeting disadvantaged and talented students. In B.S. 2080, the metropolis itself conducted examinations and arranged free study in private schools for more than 2,300 students based on merit. For transparency, the names of schools were made public through the website. - Ending Party Politics in Schools: With the conclusion that educational quality declines when teachers are active political party workers, he instructed teachers to renounce party membership. Party-related programs inside school premises were prohibited. c) Access and Transparency in Healthcare For the first time, the metropolis played a strict regulatory role in the service delivery of hospitals within the Kathmandu metropolitan area. - Mandatory 10% Free Beds: Under the Health Institution Operation Standards, 2077, Clause 70 (gha), private and community hospitals must allocate 10% of beds free of cost for disadvantaged citizens. After Balen warned that hospitals failing to comply could face renewal stoppage and closure, major hospitals in Kathmandu—including Kantipur, Norvic, and Medicare—were compelled to disclose details and provide free services. - Free Blood Service: An agreement was made with the Nepal Red Cross Society so that metropolitan residents would not be charged when blood is needed. - Pharmacy Monitoring: Hundreds of pharmacies and polyclinics operating against standards and selling expired medicines were sealed and brought under legal action. d) Cultural Revival and Tourism In Balen’s vision, Kathmandu is prioritized not only as a modern city but also to be re-established as a “cultural city.” - Nepal Bhasa (Newari Language) and Ranjana Script: Along with encouraging Nepal Bhasa teaching in the local curriculum, a policy was adopted to promote the use of Ranjana script on signboards. - Heritage Conservation and Indra Jatra Diplomacy: Encroachments were removed from historic stone spouts (hitis), falchas, and dabalis, and reconstruction work began. He invited mayors and guests from various countries to observe Kathmandu’s renowned Indra Jatra (Yenya Purnima), initiating a practice of cultural diplomacy. - Heritage Identification through Technology: QR codes were placed in heritage areas including Hanuman Dhoka Durbar Square to provide visitors with audio/video information about the sites. E) Governance and Digital Administration He has made extensive use of technology to reduce administrative hassles. - Transparency and Live Meetings: For the first time in Nepal’s history, a practice was introduced to broadcast municipal executive meetings live through television and social media. This ensured the public’s right to know what their representatives are saying and how decisions are made. - Infrastructure Ambulance: An “infrastructure ambulance” team was launched for urgent tasks such as filling road potholes and repairing sewer systems. - Labor Bank: By collecting details of unemployed youth and skilled human resources, the concept of a “Shram Bank” (Labor Bank) was implemented to provide employment in metropolitan projects. - Call Center 1180: The 24-hour toll-free number (1180) was made effective for receiving complaints, with follow-up on submitted issues. Controversies and Criticisms Despite positive impacts, some of Balen Shah’s working style and decisions have not been free of controversy. Critics have described him in some cases as “authoritarian” and a “strict administrator.” Thapathali Squatter Settlement Incident In B.S. 2079 Mangsir, metropolitan police used force with dozers to remove a squatter settlement that had encroached on the Bagmati riverbank. During clashes between locals and police, more than 30 municipal police personnel were injured. - Core of the Controversy: The Human Rights Commission and urban planners criticized the use of force during winter without alternative housing arrangements (without a rehabilitation plan), calling it inhumane. Balen’s side argued that riverbank land is public property and cannot be seized. Street Vendor Eviction To make the city pedestrian-friendly, he completely prohibited small shops, pushcarts, and corn roasters on sidewalks. He was criticized on social media for taking away the livelihoods of the poor. Some activists demanded time zoning or designated locations for vendors, but the metropolis did not move away from its sidewalk trade-free policy. “I will set Singhadurbar on fire” statement Claiming that the federal government was not cooperating in metropolitan work (especially in waste management and police coordination), he posted an angry statement on Facebook in B.S. 2080 Bhadau, including: “From today, Singhadurbar’s garbage will not be collected… If the country truly needs cleaning, its leaders should be dumped in Sisdol.” and “If the metropolis vehicle is stopped, I will set Singhadurbar on fire.” - Reaction: The statement drew widespread criticism for being “apolitical” and “provocative.” However, supporters viewed it as rebellion against the center’s dismissive attitude. A few days later, Singhadurbar’s waste collection resumed, and the Supreme Court also ordered that waste collection should not be stopped. Ban on the Hindi film “Adipurush” After issuing an ultimatum to correct a dialogue in the Indian film “Adipurush” that described Janaki (Sita) as “India’s daughter,” he halted the screening of all Hindi films in Kathmandu. The Ministry of Communication and Information Technology and the film association stated that a local level does not have authority to stop a film that has passed censorship. After Patan High Court issued an interim order not to obstruct the screening of films that have already passed censorship, the dispute turned into a legal battle. Personal Life Although very outspoken in public life, Balendra Shah follows a policy of keeping his private life confidential and protected from media. - Family Relationships: His father, Dr. Ramnarayan Shah, is an Ayurvedic physician, and his mother, Dhruvadevi Shah, is a homemaker. Because he was born to a Madhesi-origin father and a hill-origin mother, his background is presented as representing Nepal’s inclusive character, which he takes pride in by saying, “I myself am Nepal.” - Marriage: Balen is married to Sabina Kafle, a public health professional. Sabina’s “behind the scenes” role is considered decisive in Balen’s election campaign (digital marketing and strategy building). - Child: Toward the end of 2023 (around B.S. 2080 Poush), it became public that Balendra Shah and Sabina Kafle had their first child, a daughter. To maintain privacy, he did not make a formal announcement or publish photos, although this news has been confirmed in various public programs and media reports. - Interests and Nature: He is fond of coffee and likes using technical gadgets. Although he tends to make tough decisions, he is known among close people as soft-spoken and a good listener. Legacy and Historical Impact Balendra Shah’s tenure (still ongoing) is already being seen as a turning point in Nepal’s contemporary politics. Political analysts assess his impact in three main dimensions. a) The “Balen Effect” His victory broke the traditional arrogance of established major political parties that winning is guaranteed once a ticket is secured. After his rise, the emergence of the Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP) and the victories of Ravi Lamichhane and many independent and new faces in the B.S. 2079 House of Representatives election are considered an extension of the “Balen Effect.” This also encouraged youth with a “no vote” mindset to participate in voting. b) Assertion of Local Autonomy He practically demonstrated how to exercise the authority given to local levels by the Constitution (Schedule 8). His style of asserting metropolitan jurisdiction without fear in dealings with the federal government and ministries (such as the Department of Roads and the Electricity Authority) has inspired other municipal heads to become more active (example: coordination and influence with Dharan Mayor Harka Sampang). c) Standards in Service Delivery and Accountability By conducting live executive meetings, he established a transparency standard. The systems introduced in infrastructure building, scholarship distribution, and health services are seen as having a strong possibility of becoming institutional reforms that future mayors may not be able to reverse. Kathmandu: Independent candidate Balendra Shah has been elected mayor of the Kathmandu Metropolitan City by getting 61,767 votes, according to the District Election Office, Kathmandu. Ishwor Man Dangol of CPN (Unified Socialist) won the election as the ward chair in ward no. 12 of the city. Among 300,242 voters, a total of 190,962 voters had cast their votes in the Kathmandu Metropolitan City, the District Election Office said. Newly-elected mayor Shah obtained his Master’s in Structural Engineering from India and has also earned his fame as a rapper. Mayor Shah has stated to begin his works for the development of infrastructures, roads, schools, hospital management and waste management.
