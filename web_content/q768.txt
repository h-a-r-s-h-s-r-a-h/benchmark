--- [Meaning of 退屈しのぎ (Taikutsu Shinogi) by きのこ帝国 (Kinoko Teikoku)] ---
"退屈しのぎ (Taikutsu Shinogi)" is a song by the Japanese band きのこ帝国 (Kinoko Teikoku), featured on their album "渦になる (Uzu ni Naru)" released in 2013. The title translates to "staving off boredom," capturing the essence of the song's themes. The song delves into feelings of existential boredom, emotional numbness, and the passage of time, reflecting on the nature of happiness and the persistence of memories. The lyrics of "退屈しのぎ (Taikutsu Shinogi)" paint a vivid picture of a life marked by stagnation and introspection. The opening lines, "生ぬるい惰性で生活を 綻ばす" (Living in a lukewarm inertia), set the tone for the song, suggesting a sense of lethargy and emotional dullness. The narrator finds themselves in a cluttered room, likened to a trash can, where time seems to crawl as they wait for it to pass. This imagery evokes a feeling of entrapment, as the narrator is caught in a cycle of monotony, merely existing rather than truly living. As the song progresses, the narrator reflects on sleepless nights, listening to their own breathing, which emphasizes a profound sense of isolation. The line "許せない言葉もやるせない思いも いずれは薄れて忘れてゆくだろう" (Unforgivable words and helpless thoughts will eventually fade and be forgotten) suggests a resignation to the pain of past experiences, acknowledging that both positive and negative emotions are transient. This contemplation leads to a deeper inquiry: "憎しみより深い幸福はあるのかい" (Is there happiness deeper than hatred?). This question encapsulates the struggle to find meaning and joy amidst suffering, highlighting the complexity of human emotions. The repeated motif of "平行線の延長戦が屈折した精神性" (The extension of parallel lines represents a distorted mentality) serves as a powerful metaphor for the narrator's mental state. It suggests that their thoughts and experiences are entangled in a way that feels unchangeable, much like parallel lines that never intersect. This imagery reinforces the idea of habitual routines that trap the narrator in a cycle of despair, as they recount their daily life as a story that cannot be erased. The song's melancholic tone is further accentuated by the lines that describe a sudden shift to a nostalgic narrative, where the narrator listens to someone recounting their past. The mention of a "冴えないノンフィクションの結末" (dull non-fiction ending) reflects a sense of disappointment in reality, as the coldness of the other person's hand symbolizes emotional detachment. The final lines, where the narrator's breath mixes with a voice that begins to sing, suggest a glimmer of hope or connection amidst the pervasive numbness, yet it remains tinged with sadness. Overall, the song captures the essence of existential reflection, the struggle with inner demons, and the search for meaning in a seemingly monotonous existence.

--- [Kinoko Teikoku - Wikipedia] ---
Kinoko Teikoku (きのこ帝国; literally "Mushroom Empire") was a Japanese shoegaze/dream pop band.
Their first EP, Long Good Bye, peaked in the Oricon Albums Chart at #56 on the week of December 4, 2013. Their 2016 album Ai no Yukue peaked at #19 on Oricon Album Chart on December 14, 2016.
Time Lapse was their last and most successful album, released on September 12, 2018. It peaked #14 on Oricon Album Chart. On May 27, 2019, the band announced they will be suspending activity.
The group was composed of Satō Chiaki (佐藤千亜妃) as vocals and guitar, A-chan (あーちゃん) on the guitar and keyboards, Shigeaki Taniguchi on the bass, and Kon Nishimura on drums.
