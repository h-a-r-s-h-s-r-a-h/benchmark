--- [Jamia Masjid, Srinagar - Wikipedia] ---
The Jamia Masjid (Kashmiri: بٔڑ مٔشیٖد) is a Friday mosque located at Nowhatta in the Old City of Srinagar, in the Indian-administered union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The mosque was commissioned by Sultan Sikandar in 1394 CE and completed in 1402 CE, at the behest of Mir Mohammad Hamadani, the son of Mir Sayyid Ali Hamadani, and is regarded as one of the most important mosques in Kashmir. It is located in a central zone in the religio-political life of Srinagar, and is headed by the mirwaiz of Kashmir.
The mosque has been the site of anti-Indian protests over generations, and has become a platform for people to debate and discuss the politics of the Kashmir conflict.


--- [Khanqah-e-Moula - Wikipedia] ---
The Khanqah-e-Moula, also known as Shah-e-Hamadan Masjid and the Khanqah, is a Sunni mosque located in the Old City of Srinagar, in the Indian-administered union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. Situated on the right bank of the river Jhelum between the Fateh Kadal and Zaina Kadal bridges, it was built in 1395 CE, and was commissioned by Sultan Sikandar in memory of Mir Sayyid Ali Hamadani. It is held to be the first khanqah — mosques associated with specific saints — in the Kashmir Valley. It is one of the best examples of Kashmiri wooden architecture, and is decorated with papier mache. The mosque structure is a Monument of National Importance.

Jamia Masjid, Jammu And Kashmir Jamia Masjid is one of the most beautiful and historically significant mosques in Jammu and Kashmir. Located in the heart of Srinagar’s old city at Nowhatta, this grand mosque is over 600 years old and stands as a living symbol of Islamic culture, spirituality, and Kashmiri craftsmanship. Known for its peaceful ambiance, magnificent wooden structure, and 378 deodar pillars, Jamia Masjid is not just a religious site but also a heritage treasure of India. Once you step inside the mosque courtyard, the noise of the busy markets fades away and a divine calm surrounds you. Whether you are a pilgrim seeking spiritual connection or a traveler exploring architectural wonders, Jamia Masjid leaves a lasting impression with its simplicity, strength, and serenity. History of Jamia Masjid, Srinagar The foundation of Jamia Masjid was laid in 1398 AD by Sultan Sikandar Shah Kashmiri, father of the famous ruler Sultan Zain-ul-Abidin. The massive mosque was completed in 1402 AD, establishing it as one of the earliest monumental Islamic structures in the Kashmir Valley. Over the centuries, the mosque has faced numerous hardships and disasters. It has been damaged by fire three times in its long history. The first fire occurred in 1479, following which Sultan Hasan Shah initiated restoration, which was later completed by his successor Ibrahim Magre in 1503. The second major fire in 1620 during Emperor Jahangir’s rule destroyed a large portion of Srinagar, including Jamia Masjid. Malik Haider, a well-known historian, supervised the reconstruction work which took nearly 17 years. The third devastating fire happened in 1674 during Aurangzeb's reign. When informed of the tragedy, he famously asked whether the chinar trees were safe, stating that mosques could be rebuilt but centuries-old trees could not be replaced. He then ordered complete restoration and the mosque was rebuilt within three years. The final major restoration was carried out under Maharaja Pratap Singh, who ensured the mosque’s structure remained intact and preserved for future generations. Architecture of Jamia Masjid Jamia Masjid is a fine example of Indo-Saracenic architecture blended beautifully with traditional Kashmiri wooden construction. Unlike most Mughal mosques built in marble, Jamia Masjid primarily uses deodar wood, locally available and durable against harsh winters. The highlight of the mosque is its 378 massive wooden pillars that support the entire structure. Each pillar is made from a single piece of deodar wood, symbolizing strength and craftsmanship. The mosque courtyard is spacious and can accommodate thousands of worshippers at once. In the center lies a beautiful water fountain used for ablution (wudu). The inner hall reflects simplicity and elegance, with wooden ceilings, arched doorways, and traditional Kashmiri detailing. The entire atmosphere inside the mosque offers complete peace, making it a perfect retreat for both devotees and visitors. Religious Significance Jamia Masjid is the main congregational mosque of Srinagar and plays a vital role in the religious life of the Muslim community in Kashmir. Thousands gather every Friday for Jumma Namaz, and during festivals like Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha, the mosque witnesses massive gatherings. Beyond prayers, the mosque functions as a center for religious teachings, community discussions, and spiritual awakening. Its long history of survival through disasters makes it a symbol of faith and resilience. Best Time to Visit Jamia Masjid The best time to visit Jamia Masjid is between March to October when the weather remains pleasant. During winter, snowfall in Srinagar enhances the beauty of the structure but travel may become challenging. Friday afternoons and festival days can be very crowded. Visitors seeking peace should plan visits during early morning or afternoon hours on weekdays. Travel Tips - Dress modestly and maintain decorum inside. - Remove shoes before entering prayer sections. - Avoid photography during prayer time. - Visit in daylight for safety and better experience. - Respect local customs and traditions.
